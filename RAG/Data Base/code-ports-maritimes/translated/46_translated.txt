The master of the vessel, as well as any person intervening or using the port, shall be responsible for maintaining the health, cleanliness, and environment of the port. Health is defined as a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being. 

Any violation of these provisions shall be deemed a felony, as defined by this law, for offenses punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years. Misdemeanors are defined as offenses punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars. Violations are defined as offenses punishable by a maximum of fifteen days imprisonment or a fine of sixty dinars. (Article 122, Code of Criminal Procedure).

In accordance with the provisions of the first paragraph of this chapter, the port authority shall notify the offender by any means leaving a written record. In the event of non-compliance, the port authority shall authorize the necessary measures for prevention and cleaning at the expense of the person responsible.
