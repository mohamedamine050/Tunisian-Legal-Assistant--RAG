Subject to the provisions of Chapter 21 of this Code, any occupation of the public domain of ports may be granted only on a temporary and revocable basis, without any compensation or penalty. This shall only be done by permission of the port authority.

The temporary occupation license may be granted for a maximum period of five years, renewable each time for one year.  The conditions and procedures for obtaining a temporary occupation license for the public domain of ports shall be determined by an order, proposed by the Minister responsible for port activities, after obtaining the opinion of the Minister responsible for State Property and Real Estate Affairs, the Minister responsible for Equipment, Housing, Land Planning, and the Minister responsible for the Environment.
