The port authority oversees the rules for the entry and exit of ships to and from maritime ports, as well as mooring, tying and untying operations, sailing, and the designation of their berthing locations based on the ship's characteristics and cargo, in accordance with operational requirements and the specific regulations of the port. The port authority is responsible for designating the berths allocated to ships, except for those in ports, maritime terminals, and berths used under a concession. The port authority monitors the acceptance, loading, unloading, storage, and delivery of goods, materials, and seafood products within the port. It is also responsible for designating storage locations for these items within the maritime port, except for those in ports, maritime terminals, and berths used under a concession.
