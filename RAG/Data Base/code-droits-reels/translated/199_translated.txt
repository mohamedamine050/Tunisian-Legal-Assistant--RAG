The privileged debts secured by movable and immovable property are as follows, in order of priority:

First: Expenses related to the preparation of the deceased.

Second: Debts owed to physicians, pharmacists, and nurses, consisting of treatment fees and the cost of medication for the last six months.

Third: Court costs incurred for the benefit of all creditors to preserve and sell assets that serve as collateral for the creditors.

Fourth: Amounts due to the public treasury from taxes and various dues, under the conditions stipulated in the relevant legislation.

Fifth: Wages, salaries, and the remuneration of any other employee, as well as the cost of the debtor's and his family's living expenses and maintenance, all for the last six months.
