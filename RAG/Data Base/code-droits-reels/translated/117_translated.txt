Judicial division takes place before the First Instance Court.

And if in the lawsuit [1] one party claims a right from another in the presence of the judge; the claimant is called the plaintiff and the party against whom the claim is made is called the defendant. [2] The lawsuit is the right that belongs to every claimant to bring before the court to obtain a judgment on its subject matter.  It is, in relation to the opponent, the right to present reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting that claim.  If a party is under guardianship or absent, whether claimant or defendant, their representative does not require special authorization.
