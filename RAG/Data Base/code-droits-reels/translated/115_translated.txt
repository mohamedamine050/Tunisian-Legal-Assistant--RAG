(Amended by Law No. 46 of 1992, dated May 4, 1992).

The right of the pre-emptor to bring an action for pre-emption lapses after the expiry of one month from the date of notification of the purchase by the buyer, specifying the price agreed upon by the contracting parties, whether it exceeds or falls short of the value of the sale, and the expenses.

In the event of failure to provide such notification, the right to bring an action lapses after the expiry of six months from the date of registration of the contract in the land registry for registered properties, and from the date of registration of the contract with the financial tax office for unregistered properties.
