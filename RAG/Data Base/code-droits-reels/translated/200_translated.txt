Privileged debts secured by movable or immovable property are as follows:

Firstly:  With respect to the proceeds from the sale of the harvest, debts arising from the price of seeds, agricultural work expenses, and harvesting expenses.

Secondly: With respect to the year's yield, crops, and items used for the service of agricultural lands and furniture of rented houses, debts arising from the rent of the quarter and the property, and similar urban produce for the last two years and the current year. The aforementioned privilege does not apply if those items leave the property and rights therein accrue to a third party, except in the case of fraudulent transfer.

Thirdly: With respect to properties that devolved to the heir following partition, any arrears or salary remaining charged to his account.
