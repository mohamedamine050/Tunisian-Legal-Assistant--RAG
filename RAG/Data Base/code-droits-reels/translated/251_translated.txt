The formal mortgage is a contract by which the creditor acquires a real right over an immovable property designated to satisfy his debt; by virtue of this right, he may take precedence over ordinary creditors and creditors subsequent to him in rank in recovering his right from the price of that property, in whichever hands it may be.  They may participate in the bidding.

An increase by the debtor is not accepted unless he offers to pay the price in cash.  The agreed price between the contracting parties is not limited to whether it exceeds or falls short of the sale value.

If the auction is awarded to the creditor, he is deemed to have received the price he offered. The agreed price between the contracting parties is not limited to whether it exceeds or falls short of the sale value.
