The enforcement of foreign judicial decisions is not permitted:

- If the subject matter of the dispute falls exclusively within the jurisdiction of Tunisian courts.

- If the same dispute, between the same parties and for the same reason, was previously decided by Tunisian courts by a decision not subject to any form of appeal.

- If the foreign decision is contrary to public order as understood in Tunisian private international law, or if it was issued in violation of the rights of the defense.

- If the foreign decision has been revoked or its enforcement suspended under the law of the country in which it was issued, or if it is not enforceable in the country in which it was issued.

- If the country issuing the judgment or decision does not respect the principles of reciprocity.

Furthermore, the enforcement of foreign arbitral decisions is not permitted except under the conditions stipulated by Article 81 of the Arbitration Code.
