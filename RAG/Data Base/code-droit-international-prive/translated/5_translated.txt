The Tunisian courts shall also hear:

-1- Claims for civil liability:

* based on negligence, if the act causing the liability was committed or if the damage occurred within Tunisian territory.

* Damage [1]  injury inflicted in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, or his reputation, property or honor. [2] loss or damage caused to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. Damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the actual loss suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he was or will be forced to incur to repair the consequences of the act that caused him harm, as well as what he has been deprived of in the ordinary scope of the consequences of this act.

-2- If the claim [1] a request by one party to another for a right in the presence of a judge; the applicant is called the plaintiff and the respondent is called the defendant. [2] A claim is the right of every claimant to bring it before the court for a judgment in its subject matter. It is for the defendant the right to present reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting that request. is related to a contract of sale or is enforceable in Tunisian territory, unless the contract includes an agreement on the jurisdiction of a foreign court. 

-3- Disputes involving a transferable right located in Tunisian territory. 

-4- Disputes concerning intellectual property if its protection is claimed in Tunisian territory.
