Non-contractual liability is governed by the law of the state where the harmful act occurred. However, if the damage [1] results in injury caused by a violation of the law, affecting the person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation [2] or loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like - damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the loss actually suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that the claimant was or will be obliged to spend to remedy the consequences of the act that caused the harm, as well as the loss of profit within the normal limits of the consequences of this act,  occurred in another state, then the law of that state shall apply if the injured party so requests. If the perpetrator and the injured party have their usual place of residence in the same state, then the law of that state shall apply.
