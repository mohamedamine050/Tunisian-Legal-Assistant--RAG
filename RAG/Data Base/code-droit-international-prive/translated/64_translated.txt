The applicable law governs, specifically, the following aspects of contracts: (1) their existence, (2) their validity, (3) their interpretation, (4) the execution of the obligations arising from them, (5) the consequences of full or partial non-performance of obligations, including the assessment of damages. 

[1] Damages refer to harm inflicted in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. [2] It also includes the loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, or similar matters. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damages refer to the actual loss suffered by the claimant, as well as the necessary expenses incurred or to be incurred by them to remedy the consequences of the harmful act, and the benefits they have been deprived of within the normal scope of consequences of this act.  

The law also governs the methods of compensation. (6) The various aspects of the termination and extinction of obligations, including their lapse due to the passage of time and their prescription based on the expiry of deadlines. (7) The effects of the invalidity of a contract.

The methods of execution and the means taken by the creditor in the event of non-performance are subject to the law of the state where the act of execution is actually performed.
