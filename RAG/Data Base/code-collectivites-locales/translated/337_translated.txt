The Head of the Region is entrusted with the regional arrangements and the implementation of the decisions of the Regional Council. The Head of the Region shall take general administrative decisions that are not specific to any individual, as required by the management of regional affairs. The Governor shall authorize the implementation of decisions taken within the framework of regional arrangements and shall inform the Head of the Region in writing of the outcome of the decisions taken within a period not exceeding two months and shall explain the reasons for non-implementation, if necessary. The Head of the Region may appeal the refusal of the central authority to implement the Region's decisions before the administrative courts.
