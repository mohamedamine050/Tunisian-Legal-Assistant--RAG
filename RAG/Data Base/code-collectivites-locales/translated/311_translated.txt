The Regional Council shall hold a session compulsorily every three months. It shall also hold meetings whenever necessary, upon the invitation of its President or at the request of one-third of its members, or at the request of one-tenth of the voters registered in the electoral register of the region. Taking into account exceptional cases or official holidays, the Regional Council shall not meet during the weekend. Invitations shall be sent at least 15 days before the date of the meeting of the Council. However, the deadlines may be shortened to two days in case of urgency, and in case of extreme urgency, the Council shall meet immediately. The agenda shall be included in the invitation. The invitation shall be included in the minutes of the proceedings, posted at the entrance of the Regional Council headquarters, and published on its dedicated website. It shall also be sent in writing to the members of the Regional Council. Invitations sent by email shall be considered legally valid when proof of delivery is provided. 

**Regarding the Public Session:**

The public session, which shall be held by civil society organizations in accordance with the procedural rules set out in this guide, aims to obtain specific information about the activities or projects undertaken by the public administration, to ensure the integrity, transparency, and fairness of its performance, as well as its compliance with the requirements of public interest.
