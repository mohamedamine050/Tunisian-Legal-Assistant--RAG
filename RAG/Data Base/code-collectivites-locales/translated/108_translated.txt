The contracts referred to in Chapter 107 of this law, accompanied by the related documents, shall be submitted to the Wali [Governor] and the Regional Treasurer. Both the Regional Treasurer and the Wali may object to the contracts before the **Court of Audit**, the competent court in the jurisdiction where the dispute is to be adjudicated. This court is a civil court under the judicial authority, which must be independent within the three branches of government. The Court of Audit is a judicial institution tasked with examining public accounts and funds, verifying the legality of government expenditures, and ensuring the efficient and transparent management of public funds.  The objection shall halt the execution of the contract. The objection shall be made in accordance with the procedures set out in Chapter 94 of this law.
