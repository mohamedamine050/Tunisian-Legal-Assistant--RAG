Every warrant requiring seizure shall be issued immediately, and a copy thereof shall be deposited within a period of five full days, commencing from the day of seizure, with the Court of First Instance, which is a civil court that falls under the judicial authority, which must be independent within the three branches of government. The court of first instance is responsible for the jurisdiction of the matter in question. The parties claiming the seized objects shall be notified of this warrant. A copy thereof shall be delivered to the custodian at the time of his appointment. The custodian shall sign the copy and the original warrant. If he cannot sign, this shall be recorded in the warrant.
