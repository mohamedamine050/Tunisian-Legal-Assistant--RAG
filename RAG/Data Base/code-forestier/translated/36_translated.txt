The following are the rights of usufruct in forests: 1) Collection of dry firewood fallen on the ground. 2) Cutting of thin forest of secondary species without uprooting their roots. 3) The right to use the pasture for feeding livestock owned by the usufructuary, excluding camels. 4) Other rights of usufruct in forests aim at using some forest products intended for domestic purposes without selling them. 5) The right to cultivate some plots of land. The organization of the aforementioned rights of usufruct is governed by a decision of the Minister in charge of Forests.
