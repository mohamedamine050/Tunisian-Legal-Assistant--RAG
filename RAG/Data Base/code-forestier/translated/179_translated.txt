The term "forest" means any plant community, whether natural or artificial, composed of one or more species of trees, shrubs, or forest plants, in a pure or mixed state.

The term "forest land" means any land suitable for the establishment of forests, due to environmental and economic reasons.

The term "halophyte cover" means all lands primarily covered by halophyte plants.

The term "pasture lands" means all uncultivated lands covered by natural or introduced herbaceous or fibrous plants suitable for serving as "food" for animals.

The term "wild animal" means all species of non-domesticated animals, whether vertebrate or invertebrate.

The term "wild plant" means all species of natural plants growing in any environment.
