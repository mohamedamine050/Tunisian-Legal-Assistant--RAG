No one may engage in hunting on private property if they are prohibited from doing so orally or in writing by the owner of the property or their representative, or if such prohibition is publicly announced by means of visible notices.

The following offenses related to hunting on private land shall be prosecuted:

1. **Crimes:**  Crimes, within the meaning of this law, are those punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years.
2. **Misdemeanors:** Misdemeanors are those punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
3. **Violations:** Violations are those punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days, or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)

Hunting offenses committed on private land shall be investigated in the following two cases:

1. **Upon complaint:** By the owner of the property or their representative.
2. **Upon discovery:** By law enforcement officers tasked with enforcing the provisions of this chapter, who are authorized to issue violations of hunting regulations throughout the Republic, when they discover a violation of the provisions and regulations of general administrative decisions not related to a specific individual, regarding hunting and the conservation of animal life.
