## Claims for Damages

### Damages

[1] Harm that occurs in violation of the law and affects a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or reputation. [2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, or other similar consequences. - Damages in crimes and quasi-crimes are the actual loss suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he was or will be forced to incur to repair the consequences of the act that caused him harm, as well as what he was deprived of in the normal range of consequences of this act. 

Claims for damages arising from collisions involving persons or property are extinguished by the passage of two years from the date of the accident. Claims for recourse are extinguished by the passage of one year from the day of payment. All claims for payment for assistance or rescue are extinguished by the passage of two years from the day the assistance or rescue operations end. All claims for contribution to common losses are extinguished by the passage of two years from the arrival of the ship at its intended port or the place of interruption of the journey. They are extinguished by the passage of fifteen years if they arise from a general average. 

### Appointment 

Placing the appropriate individual in a position that aligns with the qualifications and competencies required to perform it. 

### Limitation Period

Loss of the right to pursue a claim due to failure to comply with the deadlines. 

The limitation periods mentioned above apply to minors, those under guardianship, and others who lack legal capacity. They have the right to recourse against their guardians.
