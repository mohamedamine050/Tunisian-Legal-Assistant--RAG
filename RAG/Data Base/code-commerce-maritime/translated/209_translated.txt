The bill of lading shall state: 1) the name and address of the carrier; 2) the name and address of the consignor; 3) the place and date of shipment; 4) the destination and designation of the consignee; 5) the goods delivered to the carrier; 6) the name of the vessel; 7) the terms relating to the carriage charges; 8) a reference to the charter party, if any. The bill of lading issued as described, which is in the possession of the consignor, shall constitute evidence between all parties concerned with the shipment amongst themselves, and between them and the insurers. Any counterpart of the bill of lading that does not state the first five aforementioned details shall only be considered as the beginning of written evidence.
