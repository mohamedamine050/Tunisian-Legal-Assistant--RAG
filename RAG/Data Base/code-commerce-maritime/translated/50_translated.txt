The master shall keep a logbook called "Ship's Log," the pages of which shall be numbered and sealed by a governor appointed by the President of the 
**Court of First Instance**. 

The **Court of First Instance** is typically the first court to hear a dispute and make a decision on the place of registration of the ship. The Ship's Log shall contain the following: 

- A verified statement of all incidents and decisions taken during the voyage. 
- Daily observations regarding weather and sea conditions.
- The date of commencement of the layoff mentioned in the notice.
- Record of births or deaths occurring onboard the ship. 
- Generally, all matters pertaining to the master's duties and responsibilities.

Upon completion, the Ship's Log shall be deposited in writing with the 
**Court of First Instance**.
