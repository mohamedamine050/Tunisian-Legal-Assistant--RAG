Also considered shared losses are damages:

[1] Injury caused by a violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation; [2] Loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, impairment of physical well-being or reputation, and so forth. - Damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the actual loss suffered by the plaintiff and the necessary expenses they have been or will be required to incur to repair the consequences of the act committed in harm to them, as well as what they have been deprived of in the ordinary sphere of consequences of this act, or damage to the cargo, supplies, and fuel during the trading operations referred to in Articles 272 and 273.
