The clause "Exempted from losses" implies that the insurer is exempt from bearing any maritime losses, whether common or particular, except in cases where the insured is permitted to abandon the insured objects. The clause "Exempted from particular losses except . . . . . . " implies that the insurer is exempt from bearing any particular maritime losses except for particular maritime losses resulting from one of the events excepted by the clause, as well as instances where the insured is permitted to abandon the insured objects.
