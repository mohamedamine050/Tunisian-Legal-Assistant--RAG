The Governor may modify any agreement concerning assistance and rescue concluded during a time of danger and under its influence if he deems the agreed-upon terms to be unfair, upon the request of one of the contracting parties. In all circumstances, the Governor may modify the agreement at the request of any party with an interest therein, if it is established that the consent of one of the parties is vitiated due to:

* **Fraud**
* **Fraud**
* **Deception committed with the intention of compelling a person to enter into a legal transaction or inducing them to contract under more detrimental circumstances,**
* **Concealment of the truth**, or
* **An excessive or insufficient consideration that does not correspond with the services rendered.**
