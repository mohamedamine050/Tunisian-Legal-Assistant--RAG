Authorization is granted to proceed with the precautionary seizure in accordance with the procedures and deadlines set forth in the Code of Civil Procedure, Chapters 213 and onwards, and 311 and onwards. The applicant may be required to provide a surety or valid guarantee for the grant of such authorization. The provision of a surety is mandatory if the applicant does not have a place of residence in Tunisia. The decision authorizing the precautionary seizure shall specify the deadline within which the applicant must file their lawsuit before the competent court, which shall not exceed one month. If the lawsuit is not filed within this deadline, the precautionary seizure shall be considered null and void, and the surety, if provided, shall be discharged. The precautionary seizure shall keep the vessel in port.
