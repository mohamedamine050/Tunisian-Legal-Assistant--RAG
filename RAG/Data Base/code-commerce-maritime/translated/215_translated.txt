If the sender's declarations regarding the goods' marks, number, quantity, type, or weight are false, and such falsity constitutes a felony under the meaning of this penal law—that is, crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years—they shall be considered felonies.

Crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars shall be considered misdemeanors.

Crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars shall be considered violations. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure).

The sender shall be liable to the carrier for all damages, losses, and expenses resulting from such false declarations. However, the carrier may not invoke these false declarations against any person other than the sender.
