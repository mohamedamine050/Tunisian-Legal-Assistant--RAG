The insured has the option of either demanding compensation for the damage from the insurer, 
[1] Damage, meaning harm caused in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor,  [2] loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, or similar; - in crimes and similar offenses, the damage is the actual loss suffered by the plaintiff and the necessary expenses they were or will be compelled to incur to remedy the consequences of the act that caused them harm, as well as the benefit they were deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act; 
or choosing, in the circumstances provided by law or contract, to abandon the insured object.
