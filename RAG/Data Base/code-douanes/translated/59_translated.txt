1. Customs officers may board vessels moored in ports or harbors and remain on board until they are unloaded or depart from the ports.

2. Ship masters must accept Customs officers, accompany them, and open to them the hatches, rooms, lockers, and any packages designated for inspection, if requested. In the event of refusal, the officers may request the territorially competent public prosecutor's representative to authorize a member of the public prosecutor's office to be present during the opening of the hatches, rooms, lockers, and packages; or they may request the assistance of a judicial police officer.  A report shall be drawn up on the opening and inspections mentioned above, and this shall be at the expense of the ship master.

3. Customs officers tasked with inspecting vessels and cargo may close the hatches at sunset, and they may not be reopened except in their presence.
