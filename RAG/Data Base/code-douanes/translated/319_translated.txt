The Public Prosecution, the body responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the judicial courts, shall refer to the Customs Administration all information it obtains suggesting the existence of an offense.

Offenses are classified as felonies, meaning crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years; misdemeanors, meaning crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars; and infractions, meaning crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122, Code of Criminal Procedure).

Any customs evasion or maneuver aiming at or resulting in an offense, shall also be referred.
