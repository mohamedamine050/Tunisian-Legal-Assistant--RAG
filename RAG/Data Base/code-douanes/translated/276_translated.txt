1-  Provisions and supplies loaded aboard vessels bound for foreign destinations, not exceeding the quantity necessary for the needs of the ship's crew and passengers for the probable duration of the voyage, are not subject to any form of duty or tax.

2- If it appears that the quantities intended for shipment significantly exceed the needs of the ship's crew and passengers for the probable duration of the voyage, the customs authorities may request the ship's owner or master to reduce that quantity.

3- In all cases, the number of crew members and passengers, the types and quantities of provisions and supplies loaded must be recorded in the shipping permit, which must be endorsed by customs officials.
