1. Customs officers have the right to carry weapons in the performance of their duties.

2. In addition to cases of legitimate defense, which is the authorization granted by law to defend oneself, another person, or property during an attack; they may use their weapons in the following circumstances:

a. If they are subjected to violence, physical assault, or threats from armed persons.

b. If they are unable to stop vehicles, vessels, or other means of transport by any other means, in the event that their drivers fail to comply with an order to stop.

c. If they are unable by any other means to prevent the passage of a group of persons who have failed to comply with orders to stop.

d. If they are unable to apprehend, alive, animals used in fraudulent operations, such as dogs, horses, and others, or those attempted to be imported or exported fraudulently, or those traveling illegally.
