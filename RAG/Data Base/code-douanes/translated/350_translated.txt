In all instances of ascertained customs and excise offenses, and in order to guarantee the payment of the fines incurred,  the means of transport and goods that are the subject of the dispute, and which are not subject to forfeiture, may be detained until the presentation of a guarantor.  A guarantor [1] is one who undertakes the obligation of another while remaining himself also obligated, or one who adds his liability to that of another for an obligation, whether present or future [2]; meaning a person who provides or issues a surety bond, a guarantee of payment on demand, a letter of guarantee, or any other form of guarantee: This is for the purpose of securing the fulfillment of any obligation in favor of a creditor or securing the aforementioned fines.
