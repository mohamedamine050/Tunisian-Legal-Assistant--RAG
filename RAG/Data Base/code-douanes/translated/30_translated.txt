1. To determine the value at customs, in accordance with the provisions of Article 23 of this Code, the following shall be added to the price actually paid or payable for the imported goods, in addition to the price agreed upon by the contracting parties, whether exceeding or falling short of the value of the goods:

A. The following elements borne by the purchaser, even if not included in the price agreed upon by the contracting parties, whether exceeding or falling short of the value of the goods:

- Commissions and brokerage fees, excluding purchasing commissions.
- The cost of containers considered, for customs purposes, as part of the goods in question.
- Packaging costs, including the cost of labor and materials.

B. The value of the following products and services, appropriately reduced when provided by the purchaser, directly or indirectly, free of charge or at a reduced cost, and used in the production and sale for export of the imported goods, if this value is not included in the price actually paid or payable:

- Materials, components, parts, and similar elements incorporated into the imported goods.
- Tools, molds, dies, and similar items used in the production of the imported goods.
- Materials consumed in the production of the imported goods.
- Engineering, research, artistic work, designs, plans, and drawings carried out outside of Tunisia and necessary for the production of the imported goods.

C. Royalties and license fees relating to the goods to be assessed, which the purchaser must pay, directly or indirectly, as a condition of sale of the goods to be assessed, if these royalties and fees are not included in the price actually paid or payable.

D. The value of any part of the proceeds from any resale, transfer, or subsequent use of the imported goods that accrues directly or indirectly to the seller.

E. Transportation and insurance costs for the imported goods.

F. Loading, unloading, and other costs associated with the transport of the imported goods to the point of entry into Tunisian customs territory.


2. Each element added, in accordance with the provisions of this Article, to the price actually paid or payable must be based solely on objective and quantifiable data.


3. For the determination of value at customs, no element may be added to the price actually paid or payable, except for the elements provided for in this Article.


4. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Article, the following shall not be added to the price actually paid or payable for the imported goods when determining the value at customs:

A. Costs related to the right to reproduce the imported goods in Tunisia.

B. Payments made by the purchaser for the right to distribute or resell the imported goods, unless such payments are a condition of the sale for export of the imported goods to Tunisia.
