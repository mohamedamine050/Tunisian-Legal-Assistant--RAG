1. The provisions governing seizure for offenses and misdemeanors committed outside the customs area, and which have been observed in offices, warehouses, and other places subject to the supervision of customs authorities, shall apply.

2. Seizure may also be carried out in all places when pursuing goods in flagrante delicto or when an offense is committed.  Crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years are described as felonies within the meaning of this law.  Crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, are described as misdemeanors.  Crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine not exceeding sixty dinars are described as offenses.  Pursuant to Article 290 of this Code of Criminal Procedure, or upon the accidental discovery of goods which, through the statements of their holder or through definitive documents found in their possession, are shown to be smuggled.

3. In the event of seizure following pursuit in flagrante delicto, the report shall necessarily include:

a.  With respect to goods subject to a transit permit:  A statement that the goods were pursued without interruption from the time they crossed the internal border of the customs area until their seizure, and that they were not accompanied by the necessary documents for their transport within the customs area.

b. With respect to other goods: A statement that the goods were pursued without interruption from the time they crossed the border until their seizure.
