1- Customs officers, forestry officers, and officers possessing the status of judicial police officers pursuant to the provisions of Article 10 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, as well as police officers, National Guard officers, and members of the National Army whose duties include guarding land, sea, or air borders, shall observe customs offenses and misdemeanors.

2- The officers mentioned in the first paragraph of this Article may, when observing a customs offense or misdemeanor described as an offense punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, seize all liable to forfeiture and detain correspondence and all other documents related to the seized items. They may also detain items designated to secure the payment of fines as a precautionary measure.

3- These officers may not arrest a suspect—meaning one suspected of committing a crime before the charge is filed—except in cases of flagrante delicto.
