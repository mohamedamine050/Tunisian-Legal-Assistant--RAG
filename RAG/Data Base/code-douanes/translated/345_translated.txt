If a claim is filed [1] whereby one party requests their right from another in the presence of a judge; the requester is called the plaintiff and the requested party is called the defendant. [2] A claim is the right that belongs to every claimant to submit to the court to obtain a judgment on its subject matter.  It is, for the opposing party, the right to present reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting that claim. Based on a seizure, the burden of proving the non-commission of the offense or misdemeanor rests upon the seized party.
