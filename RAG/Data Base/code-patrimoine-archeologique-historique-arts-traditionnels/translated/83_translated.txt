Whoever performs the works specified in Articles 9, 10, 11, 23, 28, 30, 31, 43, and 46 of this Code without adhering to the procedures stipulated in the aforementioned articles shall be punished by imprisonment for one year and a fine of twenty thousand dinars.

In case of recidivism, the penalty shall be doubled. (Abrogated and replaced by Article 1 of Decree No. 43 of 2011 dated May 25, 2011)

Furthermore, anyone who intentionally authorizes construction on an archaeological site shall be subject to the same penalties.

The perpetrators of the offenses referred to in this article shall be required to restore the historical monuments and damaged buildings to their original state, repair all damages caused by their actions, and bear the expenses of repairing the damages and restoration.

In all cases where a building is constructed without a permit on an archaeological, cultural, or protected site, the Governor or the Mayor, as the case may be, shall, upon the request of the Minister in charge of Heritage, issue a demolition order and execute it without delay. They shall also have the right to request the assistance of the public authorities, if necessary, and to undertake all necessary works at the expense of the violator.
