Amended by Law No. 23 of 2002 dated February 14, 2002.

The licensee, or any contracting party or quasi-contracting party who may have recourse, whether directly through a contract—a contract being a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights regarding the specific transaction—or indirectly through a contract—a contract being a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights regarding the specific transaction—is authorized to import, without undertaking foreign trade procedures, the supply of:

A. All equipment, machinery, fittings, materials, and vehicles actually intended for use within the scope of exploration, research, and exploitation activities in the context of electricity production as defined in Article 3.66(b) of this Code;  motor vehicles belonging to the services and necessary for transport operations.  The same privileges are also granted to the holder of an electricity production license as defined in Article 3.66 of this Code, or to any contracting party or quasi-contracting party who may have recourse,

B. whether directly through a contract—a contract being a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights regarding the specific transaction—or indirectly through a contract—a contract being a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights regarding the specific transaction—for handling.
