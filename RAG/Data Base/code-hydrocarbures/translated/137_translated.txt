1. Article 137:  Infractions of the provisions of this Code and the regulations adopted for its implementation are ascertained by reports drawn up in accordance with the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure by judicial officers, employees of the departments in charge of hydrocarbons, and other agents entrusted with this task.

2. Article 137: The reports drawn up in accordance with the first paragraph of Article 137 of this Code are deemed valid unless proven otherwise; they are not subject to any form of appeal and must be recorded in the accounts within ten days of their date, otherwise they are considered null and void.

3. Article 137: The reports drawn up by the employees of the departments in charge of hydrocarbons are referred to the Public Prosecutor's Office, the body responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the courts, accompanied by the opinion of the head of those departments.
