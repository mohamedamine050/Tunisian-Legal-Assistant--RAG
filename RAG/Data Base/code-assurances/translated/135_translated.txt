Compensation for damages shall be paid for both:

**Physical Damages:**

[1] Injury caused by an act contrary to law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation. 
[2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being or reputation, or any other similar consequences.
- In crimes and quasi-crimes, damages refer to the actual loss suffered by the claimant, the necessary expenses incurred or to be incurred to remedy the consequences of the act that caused the harm, as well as the benefits lost within the normal scope of the consequences of such act.

**Professional Damages:**

Paid in the form of a lump sum or in installments, at the request of the victim or as determined by the judge if the victim is a minor or incapacitated, after deducting the amount of benefits paid by the social security funds.

**Social Security**

A right guaranteed by society, supported by the state, and protected by law. It aims to protect citizens in cases of old age, disability, illness, work-related injuries, occupational diseases, loss of a breadwinner, unemployment, loss of livelihood, pregnancy, childbirth, assistance in bearing family burdens, as well as in cases of disasters, emergencies, and death.

These payments are made under the heading of work accidents and social security systems.
