The resources of the Road Accident Victims Guarantee Fund consist of: Contributions from insurance companies; contributions from policyholders; amounts paid to victims and recovered from those responsible for accidents in the cases stipulated in Article 175 of this Code; and other resources that may be allocated to it by virtue of existing laws or regulations.  The assessment and calculation of these contributions, and the methods for their collection, are governed by the following conditions:  The contribution of insurance companies is determined based on the Fund's costs and distributed according to each company's share of insurance premiums or membership fees related to third-party liability insurance resulting from the use of motor vehicles and their trailers as stipulated in Article 110 of this Code. The policyholders' contribution is determined based on third-party liability insurance premiums or membership fees issued and net of cancellations and payments for the past year. The aforementioned provisions apply to unregistered vehicles in any of the registries approved in Tunisia that are covered by border insurance. The rules applicable to the single premium apply to the policyholders' contribution stipulated in the first paragraph of this Article, with regard to collection procedures, duties, inspection of violations, penalties, disputes, limitation periods, and recovery. The percentages of the contributions referred to in this Article are determined by order.  A contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights with respect to the specific transaction.  In it, the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a periodic income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer.
