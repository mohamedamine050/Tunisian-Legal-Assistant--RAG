The amount of compensation for damage shall be equal to:

**Damage**

[1] Injury caused by a violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. [2] Loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damage refers to the actual loss incurred by the plaintiff and the necessary expenses that he was forced to, or will be forced to, incur to repair the consequences of the act committed against him. It also includes any benefits he was deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act.

**Physical Damage** is the product of the number of points representing permanent disability multiplied by a monetary value representing the value of one disability point.
