[1] Harm caused in violation of the law affecting a personâ€™s body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation; [2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, damage to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. - Damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the actual loss incurred by the claimant and the necessary expenses that they were or will be required to spend to repair the consequences of the act that caused them damage, as well as what they were deprived of in the ordinary scope of the consequences of this act.

The loss arising from temporary incapacity for work shall be the actual loss of income during the period of incapacity, which is determined by the initial medical certificate or subsequent medical certificates. The injured party shall be compensated on the basis of three-quarters of the actual loss of their income, as provided for in Article 127 of this Code, after deducting payments from the employer or social security funds or similar structures. Compensation for loss of income shall be paid in a lump sum.
