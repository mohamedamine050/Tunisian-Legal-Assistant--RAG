Compensation for damages resulting from permanent disability includes:

**Damages:**

[1] Harm caused in violation of the law that affects a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or reputation. 
[2] Loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damage refers to the actual loss suffered by the claimant, the necessary expenses incurred or to be incurred to remedy the consequences of the act that caused harm, as well as the loss of benefits within the ordinary scope of the consequences of this act.

These include physical, occupational, moral, and aesthetic damages, as well as expenses incurred in seeking assistance from another person. 

In case of aggravation of damages resulting from permanent disability, compensation shall be awarded according to the procedures and criteria relating to permanent disability damages set forth in this chapter.

Claims for the damages referred to in the preceding paragraph shall not be admissible after the lapse of five years from the date of determination of the final disability rate.
