In addition to the data stipulated in Article 3 of this Code, the Takaful insurance institution must include the following data within the insurance contract:  The contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights with respect to the specific transaction.  Therein, the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a periodic income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in return for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer. The Takaful insurance institution’s commitment to Sharia standards; clarification that the payment of the subscription fee is by way of a commitment to donation; the Takaful insurance institution’s commitment to achieving complete separation between the accounts of subscribers and the accounts of shareholders; the adopted methods of managing Takaful insurance operations and the management of the employment of subscription fees; the company's policy in employing technical savings funds; the adopted method of distributing insurance surpluses; the commitment of the Takaful insurance institution to provide a Qard Hasan loan to the subscribers' fund, as defined in Article 213 of this Code, in the event of its inability to fulfill its obligations.
