The Board of the Authority is the competent body to carry out the Authority's functions and may take any decisions it deems necessary to achieve its assigned objectives.  For this purpose, the following powers are specifically assigned to the Board of the Authority:  – setting the general policy of the Authority and approving the plans and programs necessary for its implementation, particularly those related to the organization and methods of control; – studying matters of a legislative, regulatory, and organizational nature in the field of insurance (Insurance: a contract whereby the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a regular income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer); – preparing draft texts related thereto at the request of the Minister of Finance; – ensuring the proper implementation of the provisions of the Insurance Code (Insurance: a contract whereby the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a regular income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer) and its implementing texts and issuing general administrative regulations, not related to a specific person, necessary for this purpose by a bulletin issued by the Authority; – studying requests for granting licenses to insurance institutions; – granting licenses to insurance intermediaries and withdrawing them; – approving the rules of professional conduct to be prepared by the insurance professions; – reviewing supervisory reports and taking appropriate action in accordance with the provisions of Article 87 of this Code; – examining petitions submitted concerning insurance services; – monitoring the activity of the insurance sector (Insurance: a contract whereby the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a regular income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer) and its related professions; – conducting studies related to the development, organization, and qualification of the insurance sector (Insurance: a contract whereby the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a regular income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer) in response to international standards; – setting the organizational structure in accordance with the provisions of Article 190 of this Code; – approving the financial statements and the estimated budget (Budget: a study of the revenues, costs, and conditions of a project over a specific period); – setting procedures for concluding transactions in accordance with the applicable legislation. Except for the powers mentioned in the first, seventh, twelfth, and thirteenth paragraphs of this Article, the Board of the Authority may delegate some of its powers to the Head of the Authority.
