Subject to the provisions of this Code, insurance brokers shall exercise their profession in accordance with the provisions of the Commercial Code, particularly those relating to brokerage contracts.  The relationships between insurance agents, insurance producers, life insurance agents, and insurance institutions are governed by the provisions of the Code of Obligations and Contracts, particularly those relating to paid agency. 

A contract of insurance is a binding legal agreement that obliges the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights with respect to the specific transaction. Under this contract, the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a regular income, or any other monetary compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer.
