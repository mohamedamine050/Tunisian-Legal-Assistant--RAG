The Takaful insurance institution must establish a Sharia Supervisory Board responsible for monitoring and overseeing the institution's transactions and rendering an opinion on their compliance with Sharia standards.  The Sharia Supervisory Board shall comprise three members appointed by the institution's general assembly for a three-year term, renewable twice. Members of the Sharia Supervisory Board must declare any conflict of interest throughout their term. A conflict of interest includes any direct or indirect personal interest or direct or indirect personal relationship that could affect the Board's performance and independence.  A member of the Sharia Supervisory Board may not hold more than two memberships on Sharia Supervisory Boards of Takaful insurance institutions.

A Takaful contract is one in which the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or beneficiary a sum of money, a periodic income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the Takaful insurer.
