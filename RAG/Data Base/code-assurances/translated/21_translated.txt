The obligation to respond to a person's actions before the law or to compensate for the damage caused by them.

The insurer may be wholly or partially exempted from its liability to the insured if it is no longer able to stand in the place of the insured due to the latter's actions. Contrary to the provisions of this chapter, the insurer is not entitled to pursue a claim for recourse against the insured's descendants, ascendants, in-laws, employees, workers, servants, and, in general, any person who habitually resides in the insured's home, except in the case of intentional harm to a third party by one of them.
