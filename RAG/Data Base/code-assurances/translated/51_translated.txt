The Minister of Finance, "upon the proposal of the Authority," may partially or fully withdraw the license referred to in Article 48 of this Code in the following circumstances: 1) When the institution does not operate in accordance with the regulations or its bylaws. 2) When the financial status of the institution does not provide sufficient guarantees to allow it to fulfill its obligations. 3) When the equity of the insurance institution does not reach the percentage specified in Article 58 of this Code. 4) Upon the dissolution or bankruptcy of the institution. 5) If the institution does not commence its activity within one year from the date of notification of the license decision, or if it ceases to subscribe insurance contracts for a similar period. The effect of insurance contracts that are still in effect on the date of the license withdrawal shall continue until the publication of a decision by the Minister of Finance determining their fate.
