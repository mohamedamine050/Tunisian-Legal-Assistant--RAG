If the [legal] guardian acts contrary to the provisions of Article 37 of this Code by obtaining a conservatory attachment on an aircraft not subject to conservatory attachment, or by the debtor providing a guarantee in order to avoid this, or in order to obtain the lifting of the attachment, then the guardian is liable for the damage resulting therefrom.

**Damage** [1] Injury caused by a violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation [2] Loss or damage to property, or deprivation of comfort, or impairment of physical well-being, reputation, etc. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damage refers to the actual loss suffered by the plaintiff, the necessary expenses which he has been or will be forced to incur to repair the consequences of the act which caused him harm, as well as the benefit which he has been deprived of within the normal scope of the consequences of this act.
