(Repealed and replaced by Law No. 116 of 2001-2, dated November 26, 2001). Water represents a national wealth that must be developed, protected, and utilized in a manner that ensures a continuous response to the needs of all citizens and economic sectors. Water conservation is considered one of the most important means of developing, protecting, and rationalizing the use of water resources. Works aimed at developing national water resources, promoting their economic use, improving their quality, and protecting them are of public interest.
