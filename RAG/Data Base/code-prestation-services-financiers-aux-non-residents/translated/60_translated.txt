Non-resident lending institutions, as defined in this Code, are considered lending institutions as defined by Law No. 65 of 2001, dated July 10, 2001, relating to lending institutions, which have the status of non-residents as defined by the legislation related to exchange. Non-resident lending institutions include non-resident banks and non-resident financial institutions. Non-resident lending institutions may professionally provide one or more of the services listed in Articles 51 and 52 of this Code. Banks, and only banks, may receive deposits from the public, regardless of their duration or form. Non-resident lending institutions authorized as banks are considered public limited companies within the meaning of the law relating to the financial market.
