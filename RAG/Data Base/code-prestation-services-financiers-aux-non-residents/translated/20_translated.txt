Collective investment institutions in securities subject to relaxed investment rules may:

- Invest up to 35% of their assets in securities issued by a single entity,

- Borrow funds up to 10% of their assets,

- Hold up to 35% of the same class of financial instruments from a single source,

- Enter into contracts constituting forward financial instruments,

- Provide guarantees on their assets necessary for entering into contracts related to their activity, including in the form of a repurchase agreement.

The cases in which the aforementioned percentages may be increased and the types of financial instruments subject to a single source, as well as the limits and forms under which collective investment institutions in securities subject to relaxed investment rules may enter into contracts constituting forward financial instruments and provide guarantees on their assets, shall be regulated by an order.
