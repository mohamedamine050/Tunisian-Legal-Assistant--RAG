The financial statements of non-resident financial services providers established under Tunisian law and the agencies and branches of institutions with their registered office abroad shall be subject to the audit of one or more auditors.

At least two auditors must be appointed by non-resident credit institutions with public shareholding.

The auditor or auditors are appointed for a period of three years, which may be renewed once.

When appointing a single auditor, the auditor must be registered on the list of chartered accountants in Tunisia.

In the case of appointing two or more auditors, at least one auditor must be registered on the list of chartered accountants in Tunisia.

In all cases, the auditor who bears personal responsibility for the content of the audit report must be registered on the list of chartered accountants in Tunisia as a member.
