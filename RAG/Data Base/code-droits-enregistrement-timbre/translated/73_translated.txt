Public officials and financial collectors who have advanced payments to the parties may recover these payments by means of a writ of execution from the President of the primary court.

The primary court is often the first court to consider the dispute and render a decision within its jurisdiction. The writ of execution may be challenged according to the procedures applicable to the enforcement order, as amended by the provisions of (Article 2 of Law No. 82 of the year 2000, dated August 9, 2000), concerning the issuance of the Tax Law and Procedure Code.

Assets collected by the State in the form of payments, whereby a payment is a cash sum paid by individuals and companies proportionally to their income or profits.  (Starting January 1, 2002).
