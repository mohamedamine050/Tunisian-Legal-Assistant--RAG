The following are exempt from the single insurance premium:

1) Reinsurance contracts. 2) Insurance contracts relating to agricultural and maritime fishing risks, as defined in Article 27 of Law No. 70-0 of December 27, 2007. 3) Contracts insuring goods export risks and export credit insurance contracts. 4) Compulsory insurance contracts in the field of residential construction, in accordance with current legislation. 5) Life insurance contracts, capital formation contracts, and annuity contracts. 6) Insurance contracts covering risks that may occur outside Tunisian territory. 7) Insurance contracts concluded by wholly-owned exporting institutions, as defined by current tax legislation, within the framework of their activity, and as added by Article 12 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017, concerning the revision of the tax privileges system.

A contract is a binding legal agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights with respect to the specific transaction.  In an insurance contract, the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a periodic income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in consideration of premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer.
