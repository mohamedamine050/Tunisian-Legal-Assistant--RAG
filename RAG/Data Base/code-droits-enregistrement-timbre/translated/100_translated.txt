I - Insurance companies and institutions are required, in the event of a fire insurance contract in effect on the date of death covering movable property located in Tunisia and belonging to an estate whose opening has been reported, or movable property owned by the spouse of a deceased person, to submit to their respective performance monitoring center, within fifteen days following the date they learned of the death, a declaration containing the following:

1. The name or corporate name and address of the insurer.
2. The name, surname, and address of the insured, as well as the date of their death or the date of their spouse's death.
3. The number, date, and duration of the insurance contract, and the value of the insured property.  These declarations shall be made on forms provided by the tax administration.  The contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights concerning the specific transaction.  Under this contract, the insurer undertakes to pay the insured or the beneficiary a sum of money, a periodic income, or any other financial compensation in the event of the occurrence of the insured event, in exchange for premiums or any other financial payment made by the insured to the insurer.


II - Heirs and legatees are required to state whether the movable property bequeathed to them upon death is the subject of a fire insurance contract in effect on the date of death. In this case, they must state the date of the contract, the name or corporate name and address of the insurer, and the amount of the insured capital. A declaration of inheritance that does not include the aforementioned information shall be considered nonexistent with respect to the property mentioned therein.
