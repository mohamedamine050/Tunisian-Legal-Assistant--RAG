If the dispute arising between the Tunisian State and the foreign investor cannot be settled by amicable agreement, it may be referred to arbitration pursuant to a specific agreement between the parties.

If the dispute arising between the Tunisian State and the Tunisian investor cannot be settled by amicable agreement, and it objectively has an international character, the parties may submit it to arbitration pursuant to an arbitration agreement. In this case, the arbitration proceedings are subject to the provisions of the Arbitration Code.

In all other cases, the Tunisian courts shall have jurisdiction to hear the dispute.
