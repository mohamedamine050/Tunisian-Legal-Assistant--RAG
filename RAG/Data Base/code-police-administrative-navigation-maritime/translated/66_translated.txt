The Minister responsible for Maritime Trade may temporarily or permanently prohibit the command of any Tunisian vessel by a master who has been found guilty of any of the following offenses:

1. **Offenses** defined as felonies within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by death or imprisonment for more than five years.
   **Offenses** defined as misdemeanors within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
   **Offenses** defined as infractions within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)
    - **Offenses** relating to navigation regulations, particularly those relating to navigation during wartime.
    - **Offenses** relating to transporting prohibited passengers, weapons, gunpowder, or narcotics.
    - **Offenses** of breach of trust regarding the vessel or its cargo with intent to embezzle.
    - **Offenses** involving damage to the vessel resulting from negligence or inattention.
2. **Offenses** defined as felonies within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by death or imprisonment for more than five years.
   **Offenses** defined as misdemeanors within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
   **Offenses** defined as infractions within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)
    - **Offenses** relating to general administrative regulations, not directed at any specific person, aimed at preventing collisions. 
3. **Offenses** defined as felonies within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by death or imprisonment for more than five years.
   **Offenses** defined as misdemeanors within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
   **Offenses** defined as infractions within the meaning of this law, which are punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)
    - **Offenses** relating to general administrative regulations, not directed at any specific person, concerning the command certificate. 

Furthermore, the crew log of any vessel whose master or captain has exceeded the limits specified in their certificate, shall be temporarily withdrawn if it is proven that such violation was committed at the behest of the vessel's outfitter.
