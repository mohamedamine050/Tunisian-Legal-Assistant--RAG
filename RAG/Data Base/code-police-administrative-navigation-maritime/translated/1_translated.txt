## English Translation:

Ships of Tunisian nationality, regardless of their tonnage, manufactured in Tunisia, or declared as legally acquired from the enemy, or confiscated due to offenses described as felonies within the meaning of this criminal law, are considered:

* **Felonies:** Crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years. 

* **Misdemeanors:** Crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.

* **Offenses:** Crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122, Code of Criminal Procedure)

According to Tunisian law, ships manufactured in Tunisia and vessels of all other nationalities that have sunk on the Tunisian coast are considered Tunisian if:

(a) They are declared as maritime salvage and acquired by Tunisian individuals for a price less than one-third of their value. 

(b) They have been repaired in Tunisia and the cost of the repairs exceeds double the purchase price. 

(c) Their port of affiliation is in Tunisia.
