**Replaced by Section 1 of Law No. 1 of 2008 dated January 8, 2008** - Within the scope of its powers, the National Communications Authority shall impose sanctions on telecom network operators and telecom service providers who have been found to be in violation of the legislative and regulatory provisions relating to the field of communications or decisions of the National Communications Authority, in accordance with the following procedures:

1. **Issuance of a Notice:** The head of the National Communications Authority shall issue a notice to the violator to cease the unlawful practices within a period not exceeding one month. 

2. **Non-Compliance with the Notice:** If the violator fails to comply with the notice within the specified time frame, the National Communications Authority may issue an order to cease the practices mentioned in the notice immediately or impose specific conditions for carrying out their activities.

3. **Non-Compliance with the Order:** If the violator fails to comply with the aforementioned order, the National Communications Authority shall impose a financial penalty not exceeding 3% of their turnover for the past fiscal year, excluding payments (**Replaced by Section 1 of Law No. 10 of 2013 dated April 12, 2013**).

The Authority may authorize the publication of decisions imposing sanctions on public telecom network operators or telecom service providers at the expense of the party against whom the judgment is rendered, in newspapers selected by the Authority for this purpose (**Added by Section 2 of Law No. 10 of 2013 dated 2013**).

4. **Suspension of Activity:** If investigations reveal that the violation poses a risk to the normal operation of the telecommunications sector, the National Communications Authority shall issue a decision to suspend the activity related to this sector for a period not exceeding three months. The activity can only be resumed after the violation is rectified.

If investigations reveal the existence of a **misdemeanor** (**Misdemeanor** refers to crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars), or a **violation** (**Violation** refers to crimes punishable by a sentence not exceeding fifteen days imprisonment or sixty dinars fine), that requires criminal prosecution, the National Communications Authority shall refer the case to the **public prosecutor** (**Public Prosecutor** refers to the legal representative of the state, responsible for prosecuting crimes) with territorial jurisdiction for the purpose of initiating criminal proceedings, as appropriate.
