Members of the board and its staff are required to maintain professional secrecy concerning the matters and information they become aware of in the performance of their duties. The President of the board may refuse to deliver documents that compromise the confidentiality of transactions and that are not necessary for the conduct of proceedings or for the exercise of the parties' rights. The parties are required to maintain the confidentiality of information exchanged between them, and they are prohibited from exploiting such information for purposes other than the dispute or disclosing it to their interests, partners or branches. (The second paragraph was deleted and replaced by Chapter One of Law No. 1 of 2008 dated January 8, 2008.)
