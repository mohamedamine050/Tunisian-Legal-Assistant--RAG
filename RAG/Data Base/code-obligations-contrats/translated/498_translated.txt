The oath, administered by one party or litigant to another to resolve the dispute between them, if the party lacks the evidence permitted by law to prove their claim, relying on the conscience, integrity, and religious duty of the opposing party, shall not bind the opposing party except with regard to their own actions or knowledge.  Heirs, and in particular others, are not bound by the oath administered by one party or litigant to another to resolve the dispute between them, if the party lacks the evidence permitted by law to prove their claim, relying on the conscience, integrity, and religious duty of the opposing party, except for what they know of the matters.
