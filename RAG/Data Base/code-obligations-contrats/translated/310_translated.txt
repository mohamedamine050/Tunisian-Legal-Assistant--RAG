Whoever possesses something in good faith is entitled to retain it until he is reimbursed for:

Firstly:  Expenditures incurred for its preservation and maintenance.

Secondly: Expenses incurred for its repair prior to the claim being brought against him, up to the extent that its value was increased; after the claim is brought, only necessary expenses are considered, and no retention is allowed for luxury expenses.

Thirdly:  What is provided for in other provisions of the law.
