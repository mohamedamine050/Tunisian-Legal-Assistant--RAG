I. The income tax shall be levied on the total income or elements of income subject to tax, based on the total amount of net income determined in accordance with the provisions of the second section of this chapter, after deducting the following charges if they have not been taken into account for the assessment of one of the categories of income:

1. Premiums on revenue paid obligatorily and gratuitously.

2. Premiums or membership fees paid by the subscriber or member under life insurance contracts or group insurance contracts or mutual life insurance contracts or contracts for the formation of capital or mutual contracts for the formation of capital, up to a limit of 10,000 dinars per year, provided that such contracts include one of the following guarantees:

- Guarantee of a capital or income for the insured or his spouse or for his descendants or for his ascendants for an actual period of not less than 8 years.

- Guarantee of accounting units for the benefit of the insured or his spouse or his descendants or his ascendants, payable after a minimum period of not less than 8 years.

- Guarantee of capital or income upon death for the benefit of the spouse or descendants or ascendants.

The provisions of this paragraph shall apply to life insurance premiums or membership fees paid by members under group insurance contracts or collective mutual insurance contracts with an actual membership period of not less than 8 years, without their contribution or participation in such contracts being less than a minimum contribution, the rate of which shall be determined by a ministerial order issued by the Minister of Finance.

The purchase of individual contracts by the subscriber or member or the subscription to group contracts by the member, prior to the expiry of the eight-year period stipulated above, will necessitate the payment of the income tax on the total income or elements of income subject to tax that have not been paid under the provisions of this Code, plus the fines due according to the legislation in force. Late payment penalties shall not be levied if the subscriber or member purchases individual contracts or the member subscribes to group contracts before the expiry of the eight-year period stipulated above, or if the purchase takes place following the occurrence of exceptional events, as determined by the legislation in force, or after a saving period of not less than five years (amended by Chapter 23-2 of Law No. 70 of 2007 dated December 27, 2007, and by Chapter 24-2 and 3 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013, and by Chapter 27-3 of Law No. 66 of 2017 dated December 18, 2017, and by Chapter 12 of Law No. 78 of 2019 dated December 23, 2019).

The concerned party is required to present to the insurance company or mutual insurance company a certificate of receipt, which shall be issued by the competent tax authorities, confirming that he has settled his tax obligations in respect of insurance premiums or membership fees that have been used. In the absence of such a certificate, the insurance company or the mutual insurance company shall be jointly and severally liable with the subscriber or member of the individual contracts or the member of the group contracts, to pay the amounts due. (Added by Chapter 61 of Law No. 90 of 2004 dated December 31, 2004, and amended by Chapter 24-2 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013, and by Chapter 12 of Law No. 78 of 2019 dated December 23, 2019).

3. (Added by Chapter 46 of Law No. 111 of 1998 dated December 28, 1998) Premiums paid as repayment of principal and interest on university loans.

4. Interest and commissions paid in respect of loans relating to the acquisition or construction of a single dwelling, the cost of which does not exceed 200,000 dinars, excluding value-added tax. These provisions shall apply to acquisition or construction transactions under Murabaha sale contracts.

These provisions shall apply to amounts whose collection date falls on or after January 1, 2016.

These provisions shall not apply to persons who own a dwelling on the date of acquisition or construction of a dwelling under the provisions of this article. (Added by Chapter 39-1 of Law No. 71 of 2009 dated December 21, 2009, and amended by Chapter 26-1 of Law No. 30 of 2015 dated August 18, 2015).

5. Contributions paid by non-wage earners who are enrolled in one of the legal social security schemes. (Added by Chapter 44 of Law No. 71 of 2009 dated December 23, 2009).

II. In addition to the above, the following shall be deducted from the tax base: the interest earned by the taxpayer during the year in respect of special savings accounts opened with banks or with the Tunisian National Savings Fund, or in respect of bond loans issued as of January 1, 1992, up to an annual amount of five thousand dinars (5,000 dinars), provided that this deduction shall not exceed three thousand dinars (3,000 dinars) for interest arising from special savings accounts opened with banks and with the Tunisian National Savings Fund. (Amended by Chapter 30 of Law No. 98 of 1991 dated December 31, 1991, and by Chapter 13 of Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016).

VIII. Subject to the provisions of Article 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989 dated December 30, 1989, concerning the promulgation of the Code of Income Tax on Natural Persons and Corporate Tax, the following amounts shall be deducted from the income subject to tax: amounts deposited in accounts designated as “equity savings accounts,” opened with a lending institution having the status of a bank or with a stockbroker for the purpose of subscribing to or acquiring shares listed on the stock exchange, treasury bills that are subject to regularization, shares of variable capital investment companies, or units of mutual funds for the investment in securities referred to in Article 10 of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions promulgated by Law No. 83 of 2001 dated July 24, 2001, which use their capital or assets for the acquisition of shares listed on the stock exchange and treasury bills subject to regularization. This deduction shall be limited to 50,000 dinars per year. (Amended by Chapter 30 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001, and by Chapter 45 of Law No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003, and by Chapter 4 of Decree No. 28 of 2011 dated April 18, 2011, and by Chapter 13 of Decree No. 100 of 2011 dated October 21, 2011).

This deduction shall be subject to:

- (Repealed by Chapter 46 of Law No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003).

-  Attachment to the annual tax return of a certificate of deposit issued by the institution with which the equity savings account is opened.

-  No withdrawal of the amounts deposited in the aforementioned accounts for a period of not less than five years, beginning on January 1 of the year following the year of deposit.

Any withdrawal made before the expiry of the period mentioned above shall result in the payment of the due tax, which, if not paid, shall be subject to fines calculated in accordance with the applicable tax legislation. No late payment penalties shall be levied if the deposited funds are withdrawn after the end of the third year following the year of deposit, or if the withdrawal occurs following the occurrence of exceptional events.

A list of exceptional events shall be determined by a joint order of the Minister of Finance, the Minister of Public Health and the Minister of Social Affairs. (Added by Chapter 31 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001).

In this case, the limitation periods provided for in Article 72 of this Code shall apply as of January 1 of the year following the year in which the deposited funds are withdrawn.

The income tax on the total income or elements of income subject to tax, which was the subject of exemption, shall be levied on the institution with which the account is opened, if the deposited funds are not used for the purposes for which they were deposited and in accordance with the conditions relating to the management of the aforementioned accounts, plus the fines calculated in accordance with the applicable tax legislation.

In this case, the limitation periods provided for in Article 72 of this Code shall apply as of January 1 of the year following the year in which the deposited funds are not used for the purposes for which they were deposited, or in which the conditions relating to the management of the aforementioned accounts are violated.

The conditions for opening the aforementioned accounts, the manner of managing them, and the manner of using the amounts and securities deposited therein shall be determined by ministerial order. (Added by Chapter 4 of Law No. 92 of 1999 dated August 17, 1999, concerning provisions aimed at promoting the financial market).
