The owner of a quarter or a building bears absolute liability for damages.

Damage [1] is harm caused in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. [2] It is the loss or damage inflicted on property, the deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like.  - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damage is the loss actually suffered by the claimant, the necessary expenses they were or will be compelled to incur to repair the consequences of the act committed to their detriment, as well as any benefit they were deprived of within the normal scope of the consequences of that act.

Damage arising from the collapse or partial collapse of a building due to its age, lack of maintenance, or structural defects is included in this ruling. This judgment applies to anything attached to the building, such as trees and equipment pertaining to buildings and other things attached to the principal thing by virtue of subordination. If the land belongs to one owner and the debris to another, the owner of the debris is liable for the damages.
