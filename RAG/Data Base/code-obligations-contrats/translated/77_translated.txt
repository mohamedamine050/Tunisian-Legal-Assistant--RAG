## English Translation of the Arabic Legal Text:

**I.** Subject to the minimum tax provided for in Articles 12 and 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989 dated 30 December 1989 relating to the issuance of the Income Tax Code for Natural Persons and the Corporate Tax Code, the following shall be deductible from the taxable base: the income or profits subscribed in the capital of investment companies with development capital as provided for in Law No. 92 of 1988 dated 2 August 1988 relating to investment companies, as amended and supplemented by subsequent or related legislation, or the amounts deposited with them in the form of development capital funds, which, prior to the expiration of the period stipulated in Article 21 of the same law, are used by the said companies, from capital subscribed and released or from amounts deposited with them in the form of development capital funds not originating from foreign financing sources or from resources from the state budget, in subscribing to shares, or in social bonds, or in warrants convertible into shares, in accordance with the limits and conditions set forth in Article 22 of the same law, issued by institutions authorized to benefit from tax privileges relating to reinvestment.

This deduction shall be made within the limits of the amounts actually used by the development capital investment company pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph, and shall not exceed the income or profit, net of the company's liabilities, resulting from including the company's capital in the company's debts at the end of the fiscal year, subject to tax.

The amounts actually used by the development capital investment company pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph shall be deducted within the limits of the income or profit, net of the company's liabilities, resulting from including the company's capital in the company's debts at the end of the fiscal year, subject to tax, regardless of the minimum tax provided for above, in the case of the aforementioned company using the subscribed and released capital or the amounts deposited in the form of development capital funds not originating from foreign financing sources or from resources from the state budget, in subscribing to shares, or in social bonds, or in warrants convertible into shares mentioned above, issued by the institutions referred to in Articles 63 and 65 of this Code.

Benefitting from the deduction provided for in this paragraph requires the following conditions:

- The annual tax return must be accompanied by a certificate issued by the development capital investment company certifying that the aforementioned company has used the released capital or the amounts deposited in the form of development capital funds pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph;

- The amounts deposited in the form of development capital funds, corresponding to the amounts used pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph, must not be withdrawn for a period of five years commencing on the first of January of the year following the year in which they were used;

- The development capital investment company must not reduce its capital for a period of five years commencing on the first of January of the year following the year in which it used the released capital pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph, except in the case of a reduction to absorb losses;

- Accounting must be kept in accordance with the accounting legislation for entities with respect to persons who engage in an industrial or commercial activity or a non-commercial profession as defined in this Code.

**II.** The deduction provided for in paragraph 1 of this Article shall be applied within the same limits to the income or profits subscribed and released in the shares of joint investment funds in development capital as provided for in Article 22, second paragraph, of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions that use their assets pursuant to paragraph 1 mentioned above, as well as to the shares of joint investment funds in development capital as provided for in Article 22, third paragraph, of the same Code, that use their assets in subscribing to shares of the aforementioned joint investment funds in development capital, pursuant to the legislation governing them.

Benefitting from the deduction provided for in this paragraph requires the following conditions:

- The annual tax return must be accompanied by a certificate issued by the manager of the joint investment funds in development capital certifying that the assets of the aforementioned funds were used pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph;

- The redemption of subscribed shares that were converted to benefit from the deduction must not be requested for a period of five years commencing on the first of January of the year following the year in which the fund used its assets pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph;

- Accounting must be kept in accordance with the accounting legislation for entities with respect to persons who engage in an industrial or commercial activity or a non-commercial profession as defined in this Code.

**III.** Subject to the minimum tax provided for in Articles 12 and 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989 dated 30 December 1989 relating to the issuance of the Income Tax Code for Natural Persons and the Corporate Tax Code, the following shall be deductible within the limits of the income or profit, net of the company's liabilities, resulting from including the company's capital in the company's debts at the end of the fiscal year, subject to tax: the income or profits subscribed in the capital of investment companies with development capital as provided for in Law No. 92 of 1988 dated 2 August 1988 relating to investment companies, as amended and supplemented by subsequent or related legislation, or the amounts deposited with them in the form of development capital funds, which are obligated to use, before the expiration of the period stipulated in Article 21 of the same law, at least 65% of the released capital and at least 65% of each amount deposited with them in the form of development capital funds not originating from foreign financing sources or from resources from the state budget, to acquire or subscribe to shares, or social bonds, or warrants convertible into shares, in accordance with the limits and conditions set forth in Article 22 of the same law, newly issued by institutions authorized to benefit from tax privileges relating to reinvestment.

The deduction shall be made regardless of the minimum tax provided for above. The same conditions apply in the case of a development capital investment company undertaking to use at least 75% of the subscribed and released capital and at least 75% of each amount deposited with them in the form of development capital funds not originating from foreign financing sources or from resources from the state budget, to subscribe to shares, or social bonds, or warrants convertible into shares mentioned above, newly issued by the institutions referred to in Articles 63 and 65 of this Code.

The requirement that the shares, social bonds, or warrants convertible into shares must be newly issued does not apply if it concerns acquiring contributions in the capital of institutions granted the right to benefit from tax privileges relating to reinvestment operations.

Benefitting from the deduction provided for in this paragraph requires the following conditions:

- The annual tax return must be accompanied by a certificate of the subscribed capital release or payment of the amounts, issued by the development capital investment company, and by the company's commitment to use the released capital or the amounts deposited in the form of development capital funds pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph;

- Issuance of new shares;

- The amounts deposited in the form of development capital funds must not be withdrawn for a period of five years commencing on the first of January of the year following the year in which they were paid;

- The capital must not be reduced for a period of five years commencing on the first of January of the year following the year in which the subscribed capital was released, except in the case of a reduction to absorb losses;

- Accounting must be kept in accordance with the accounting legislation for entities with respect to persons who engage in an industrial or commercial activity or a non-commercial profession as defined in this Code.

**IV.** The deduction provided for in paragraph III of this Article shall be applied within the same limits to the income or profits subscribed and released in the shares of joint investment funds in development capital as provided for in Article 22 bis of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions that are obligated to use their assets pursuant to the conditions set forth in the aforementioned paragraph III, as well as to the shares of joint investment funds in development capital as provided for in Article 22, third paragraph, of the same Code, that use at least 65% or 75%, as the case may be, of their assets in subscribing to shares of the aforementioned joint investment funds in development capital.

Benefitting from the deduction provided for in this paragraph requires the following conditions:

- The annual tax return must be accompanied by a certificate of the subscription and release of the shares, issued by the fund manager, and by his commitment to use the fund's assets pursuant to the provisions of this paragraph;

- The redemption of subscribed shares that were converted to benefit from the deduction must not be requested for a period of five years commencing on the first of January of the year following the year in which they were released;

- Accounting must be kept in accordance with the accounting legislation for entities with respect to persons who engage in an industrial or commercial activity or a non-commercial profession as defined in this Code.

**V.** In the event of development capital investment companies referred to in paragraphs I and III of this Article transferring the contributions that have benefited from tax privileges, or transferring them back, they must reuse the proceeds of the transfer or transfer back as provided for in Article 22 of Law No. 92 of 1988 dated 2 August 1988 relating to investment companies, as amended and supplemented by subsequent or related legislation, pursuant to the provisions of the aforementioned paragraphs.

Likewise, in the event of the joint investment funds in development capital referred to in paragraphs II and IV of this Article transferring the contributions that have benefited from tax privileges, or transferring them back, they must reuse the proceeds of the transfer or transfer back as provided for in Article 22, fourth paragraph, of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions pursuant to the provisions of the aforementioned paragraphs.

**VI.** Development capital investment companies as provided for in paragraphs I and III of this Article shall be jointly and severally liable with the beneficiaries of the deduction, each within the limits of their respective benefits, for the payment of the income tax, or corporate tax due and not paid pursuant to the provisions of the aforementioned paragraphs, and the penalties related thereto, in the event of the failure to use the released capital and the amounts deposited in the form of development capital funds pursuant to the conditions set forth in the same paragraphs, or in the event of the reduction of their capital before the end of the specified period.

The managers of joint investment funds in development capital as provided for in paragraphs II and IV of this Article shall be jointly and severally liable with the beneficiaries of the deduction, each within the limits of their respective benefits, for the payment of the income tax, or corporate tax due and not paid pursuant to the provisions of the aforementioned paragraphs, and the penalties related thereto, in the event of the failure to comply with the condition of using the fund's assets pursuant to the aforementioned paragraphs, or in the event of enabling the holders of the shares to redeem their shares before the end of the specified period.

"The tax privilege provided for in this Article shall not be granted for subscription operations intended for the acquisition of land, except for reinvestment operations in the institutions referred to in Article 76 of this Code." (Added by Article 37-2 of Decree-Law No. 66 of 2017 dated 18 December 2017).
