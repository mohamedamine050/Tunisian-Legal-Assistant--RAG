If the lessee cultivated the land and his planting did not sprout during the term of his lease, but sprouted in the following year, he is entitled to continue managing the aforementioned land under a lease equivalent to the agreed-upon lease, if he proves, upon the expiry of the lease, the existence of the planting in the aforementioned state.  All of this is subject to the condition that the lessee was neither fraudulent nor negligent.
