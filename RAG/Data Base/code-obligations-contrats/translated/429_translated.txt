The judicial decision results from the defendant's silence in the court hearing when the judge called upon him to respond to the claim.

[1] One party claimed a right against another in the presence of the judge; the claimant is called the plaintiff, and the party against whom the claim is made is called the defendant. [2] The claim is the right of anyone who has a demand to present it to the court for judgment on its subject matter.  As for the defendant, it is the right to present reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting the claim directed against him. He insisted on his silence and did not request an extension to respond.
