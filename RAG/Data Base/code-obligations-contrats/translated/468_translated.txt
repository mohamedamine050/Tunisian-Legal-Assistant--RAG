Private ledgers and other correspondence, such as letters, notes, and loose papers, written or signed by their holder, shall not constitute evidence for the writer but only against him in the following instances:

Firstly: If they contain an explicit statement implying the creditor's receipt of part of his debt or something else implying discharge.

Secondly: If they state that the purpose of the writing is to establish evidence for the named person where he previously lacked such evidence.
