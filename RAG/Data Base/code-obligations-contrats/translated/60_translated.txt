I. The deadlines for filing the annual income tax declaration for natural persons or the corporate income tax are set out below, even in the case of complete exemption under tax privileges.

1. Natural persons:

a) Until February 25th for individuals earning income from movable capital, securities, real estate, or income from abroad other than wages, pensions, and life annuities. (Amended by Article 27 of Law No. 77 of 2008 dated December 22, 2008 and Article 47 of Law No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010).

a' - The deadline provided for in sub-paragraph "a" above applies to the declaration of the surplus value referred to in Article 31 bis and the surplus value referred to in the second paragraph of Article 3 of this Code. (Added by Article 64 of Law No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003 and amended by Article 41-14 of Law No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010).

b) Until April 25th for merchants,

c) Until May 25th for service providers, individuals engaged in industrial activities or non-commercial professions, as well as individuals engaged in multiple activities or earning more than one type of income. The income referred to in sub-paragraph "a" above is considered a single type of income.

d) Until July 25th for individuals engaged in traditional crafts.

This deadline also applies to individuals who, in addition to profits from traditional crafts, earn income referred to in sub-paragraph "a" of this paragraph. (Added by Article 80 of Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997).

e) Until August 25th for individuals earning profits from agricultural and fishing operations.

The declaration is to be filed until the 25th day of the third month following the close of the fiscal year if it is closed on a date other than December 31st. This deadline also applies to individuals who, in addition to profits from agricultural and fishing operations, earn income referred to in sub-paragraphs "a" and "d" of this paragraph. (Added by Article 81 of Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997).

f) Until December 5th for employees and recipients of pensions or life annuities, for wages, pensions, and life annuities of Tunisian and foreign origin. (Amended by Article 25 of Law No. 98 of 1991 dated December 31, 1991, Article 37 of Law No. 125 of 1993 dated December 27, and Article 28 of Law No. 77 of 2008 dated December 22, 2008).

This deadline also applies to individuals who, in addition to wages, pensions, or life annuities, earn income referred to in sub-paragraphs "a" and "e" of this paragraph. (Added by Article 82 of Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997).

2. Legal persons:

The declaration must be filed no later than March 25th of each year or no later than the 25th day of the third month following the close of the fiscal year if it is closed on a date other than December 31st.

This deadline applies to the tax referred to in sub-paragraph "c' " of paragraph 1 of Article 52 of the Code of Income Tax on Natural Persons and Corporate Income Tax. (Added by Article 25-3 of Law No. 59 of 2014 dated December 26, 2014).

If the declarations relating to the determination of corporate income tax for companies subject by law to an audit by a certified accountant are filed before the holding of the annual general meeting of shareholders at which the accounts for the year in question are approved, or before the approval of the accounts for the year in question by a certified accountant for single-person limited liability companies, they shall be deemed provisional and may be amended within fifteen days following the date of approval or certification of the accounts, as the case may be, and no later than the 25th day of the third month following the latest date set out in the preceding sub-paragraph. (Amended by Article 37 of Law No. 125 of 1993 dated December 27, 1993 and Article 67 of Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006).

II. The declaration relating to the surplus value referred to in paragraphs 2 and 3 of Article 27 of this Code shall be filed no later than the end of the third month following the actual transfer, and the tax due on the real estate surplus value referred to in paragraph 3 of Article 27 of this Code shall be paid on the basis of an initial declaration to be filed during the month following the date of notification of the determined value by a state property expert.

The tax collected on this basis constitutes an advance payment to be deducted from the tax due upon the actual transfer of the entire property or part thereof. This advance payment constitutes the minimum amount of tax due on the value realized upon the transfer.

The transfer of ownership of state-owned agricultural land for the purpose of transfer can only be authorized after the presentation of proof of payment of income tax on the total income or income components, on account of real estate surplus value. The aforementioned certificate can be issued upon the filing of the initial declaration of surplus value and proof of payment of the tax on that basis, unless the State recovers the property in accordance with the law in force.

In the event of failure to declare income tax on account of the surplus value referred to in paragraph 2 of Article 27 of this Code, the tax authorities may, after the expiry of one month from the date of notification to the concerned party, apply a mandatory advance payment on account of the aforementioned tax at a rate of 2.5% of the transfer price declared in the contract. (Amended by Article 6 of Law No. 82 of 2000 dated August 9, 2000 relating to the issuance of the Code of Tax Rights and Procedures).

The tax shall not be increased if it is proven that it was deducted at source in accordance with the provisions of Article 52 of this Code. (Amended by Article 108 of Law No. 122 of 1992 dated December 29, 1992 and Article 6 of Law No. 73 of 1998 dated August 4, 1998 relating to the simplification of tax procedures and the reduction of tax rates).

III. The persons referred to in the third paragraph of Article 7 of this Code shall file their declaration and pay the taxes due by them during the month preceding the month of their departure.

IV. In case of death, the heirs shall file the declaration within six months from the date of death, and the tax due shall not exceed half of the net inheritance before the payment of inheritance taxes.
