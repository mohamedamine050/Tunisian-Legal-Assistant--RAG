(Added by Article 92-1 of Act No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013).

2) Income from movable capital realized by legal persons not subject to company tax or exempt from it in full pursuant to legislation in force, as well as income from movable capital realized by the common investment fund in securities provided for in Law No. 83 of 2001 dated July 24, 2001 concerning the issuance of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions, shall be subject to a final and non-refundable withholding tax at a rate of 20%. This withholding shall be performed by the person paying this income. (Amended by Article 31 of Act No. 98 of 1991 dated December 31, 1991 and by Article 21 of Act No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001).

The provisions of this paragraph shall apply to movable capital realized by startup assistance funds and common investment funds in development capital as provided for in the legislation governing them. (Added by Article 5 of Law No. 59 of 2005 dated July 18, 2005 concerning tax provisions aimed at encouraging the creation of startup assistance funds, and amended by Article 25-2 of Act No. 106 of 2005 dated December 19, 2005).

These provisions shall not apply to income from movable capital in foreign currency or in convertible dinars. (Added by Article 99 of Act No. 122 of 1992 dated December 29, 1992 and amended by Article 43 of Act No. 111 of 1998 dated December 28, 1998).

3) (Added by Article 70 of Act No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003) Non-residents established in Tunisia whose period of activity in Tunisia does not exceed six months shall be subject to income tax or company tax by way of withholding applied to the amounts accruing to them in this context at the following rates:

- 5% of the gross turnover for construction work,

- 10% of the gross turnover for installation operations,

- 15% of the gross turnover or receipts for other services.

(Amended by Article 24-1 of Act No. 59 of 2014 dated December 26, 2014).

However, the persons concerned may choose to be subject to income tax or company tax on the basis of the income or net profits realized in respect of the aforementioned activities, which shall be determined in accordance with the provisions of this Code.

"This option shall be exercised by means of a request filed for this purpose with the competent tax office or monitoring center when filing the declaration of presence provided for in Article 56 of this Code by the persons concerned. The persons concerned shall inform the debtor of the amounts accruing to them in respect of the aforementioned activities of this option." (Amended by Article 24-2 of Act No. 59 of 2014 dated December 26, 2014).

If the option to be subject to income tax or company tax on the basis of the net profits realized in Tunisia in respect of the aforementioned activities is chosen and the tax and accounting obligations provided for in this Code are not fulfilled, the income tax or company tax due in respect of the amounts accruing to them cannot be less than the amount of tax calculated on the basis of the withholding provided for in the second sub-paragraph of this paragraph.

4) (Added by Article 28 of Act No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015) Non-residents established in Tunisia who have not declared their presence as required by Article 56 of this Code shall be subject to tax by way of withholding at a rate of 15% of the gross amounts accruing to them.

"This rate shall be increased to 25% if it concerns persons established in Tunisia and resident in a country or territory with a preferential tax system within the meaning of number 12 of Article 14 of this Code". (Added by Article 50 of Act No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018).

This withholding shall be exempt from income tax for natural persons or from company tax due on their activity in Tunisia.

However, if the persons concerned regularize their tax situation in accordance with the legislation in force, the aforementioned withholding shall be deducted from income tax for natural persons or from company tax due on their activity in Tunisia, or shall be refundable in accordance with the legislation in force.

III. Income tax due on salaries, wages, pensions, and life annuities, including the value of material benefits, shall be subject to compulsory withholding by the employer or the person obliged to pay the income or pensions, who is established or resident in Tunisia.

Taxpayers who receive salaries or wages from companies, associations, or private individuals resident or established outside Tunisia shall withhold tax calculated under the same conditions as the withholding referred to in the first sub-paragraph of this paragraph (Amended by Article 36 of Act No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006).

IV. Withholdings effected under any heading whatsoever shall be paid to the relevant tax office:

- By natural persons, within fifteen days of the month following the month in which these withholdings were effected, and within the first fifteen days of the month following each quarter of the calendar year in which these withholdings were effected for persons referred to in Article 44 bis of this Code, and within the first fifteen days of January and July following the semester in which these withholdings were effected for persons operating in the sector of passenger transport by taxis and rural transport vehicles subject to the estimated system provided for in Article 44 bis of this Code. (Amended by Article 54-1 of Act No. 106 dated December 19, 2005, by Article 62 of Act No. 70 of 2007 dated December 27, 2007, and by Article 337 of Act No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010).

- By legal persons, within the first twenty-eight days of the same month.

However, withholding exempt from income tax or company tax that has not been effected shall be considered to be a permanent debt of the actual debtor thereof, and the aforementioned withholding shall in this case be subject to the following tax burden rule:

100 x withholding rate

100 - withholding rate

(Amended by Article 33 of Act No. 125 of 1993 dated December 27, 1993 and by Article 23 of Act No. 59 of 2014 dated December 26, 2014).

The withholding rate referred to in the third sub-paragraph of sub-paragraph "b" and in sub-paragraphs "c", "c bis", and "e" of paragraph 1 of this Article shall be increased to 25% if it concerns bonuses or income accruing to persons resident or established in a country or territory with a preferential tax system within the meaning of number 12 of Article 14 of this Code (Amended by Article 35-4 of Act No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018).

(The last paragraph of this Article was repealed by Article 35-5 of Act No. 56 of 2018 dated December 2018).
