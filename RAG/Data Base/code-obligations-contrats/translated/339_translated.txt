Obligations are extinguished in the following ways:

First: Performance.

Second: Impossibility of Performance.

Third: Voluntary Release.

Fourth: Novation.

Fifth: Set-off.

Sixth: Confusion.

Seventh: Extinction of the claim by lapse of time.

Eighth: Rescission.

[1] A claim is a request by one party for a right against another in the presence of a judge; the requestor is called the claimant and the party against whom the request is made is called the defendant. [2] A claim is the right of every entitled party to bring their case before the court to obtain a judgment on the subject matter.  It is, for the opposing party, the right to present grounds for defense or objections aimed at refuting that claim.
