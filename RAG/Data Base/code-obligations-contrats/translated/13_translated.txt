Contrary to the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 12 of this Code, the following expenses are not deductible for the purpose of determining profit:

1. Gifts of all kinds and hospitality expenses, including the costs of banquets and parties, exceeding one percent of the gross turnover achieved by the institution, provided that the deduction does not exceed a maximum amount of twenty thousand dinars in each fiscal year. 

2. Income tax, taxes levied on total income or income components, or corporate tax, including deductions from the completed supply in its name, which are borne by the institutions instead of the actual claimants thereof, as well as fees on trips abroad. (Amended by Article 51 of Decree-Law No. 127 of 1994 dated December 26, 1994, by Article 51-1 of Decree-Law No. 101 of 2002 dated December 17, 2002, by Article 41-11 of Decree-Law No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010, and by Article 39 of Decree-Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013).

3. Commissions, brokerage fees, trade discounts, or other similar discounts, efficiency bonuses referred to in sub-paragraph 1 of paragraph "A" of paragraph 1 of Article 52 of this Code, leases referred to in paragraph 11 bis of Article 53 of this Code, fees, rewards, and grants referred to in paragraph 3 of Article 30 of this Code, which are borne by the institution and which are not declared in accordance with the conditions of paragraph 3 of Article 55 of this Code. (Amended by Article 92 of Decree-Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001, by Article 29-4 of Decree-Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, and by Article 30-5 of Decree-Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016).

4. All expenses related to secondary residences, aircraft, and pleasure boats referred to in paragraph 4 of Article 15 of this Code.

5. Rent, maintenance expenses, supplies, fuel, and tolls incurred for tourism vehicles with a power exceeding 9 fiscal horsepower, except those whose original purpose is exploitation. (Amended by Article 52 of Decree-Law No. 109 dated December 25, 1995).

6. Profits paid to the operator or to partners in partnerships or limited partnerships in return for the amounts they deposit in the institution's fund in addition to their capital contributions.

7. The operator's or nominal partner's salary.

8. Settlements, fines, seizures, and penalties of any kind incurred by those who violate the legal provisions relating to prices, trade, the distribution of various goods, or the supervision of exchange, the basis of taxes, contributions, or levies, and, in general, any offense described as felonies under the meaning of this law, crimes that are punishable by death or imprisonment for more than five years. Offenses are described as misdemeanors, crimes that are punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars. Offenses are described as violations, crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure).

9. Donations, grants, and sponsorships that have not been declared in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 59 of this Code. (Added by Article 48 of Decree-Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997, and amended by Article 60-2 of Decree-Law No. 66 of 2017 dated December 18, 2017).

10. The portion of the rent subsidies required of lessees of fixed assets subject to financial lease contracts or lease contracts for the purpose of returning the purchase price of the aforementioned assets by lending institutions engaged in financial leasing or leasing activities. (Added by Article 43 of Decree-Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006, and amended by Article 2-36 and 3 of Decree-Law No. 7 of 2011 dated December 30, 2011).

11. Expenses equal to or exceeding 20,000 dinars, excluding the performance of value-added tax, that are paid in cash. (Added by Article 34-1 of Decree-Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013).

12. Expenses relating to amounts paid to persons residing or established in a country or territory with a preferential tax regime. (Added by Article 1-34 of Decree-Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016, and amended by Article 35-1 of Decree-Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018).

Persons are considered to be residing or established in a country or territory with a preferential tax regime if the tax levied on them in that country or territory is less than 50% of the income tax or corporate tax levied in Tunisia on the same activity. A list of countries and territories with a preferential tax regime shall be established by a decision of the Minister in charge of finance. (The second paragraph was added by Article 3-2 of Decree-Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018).
