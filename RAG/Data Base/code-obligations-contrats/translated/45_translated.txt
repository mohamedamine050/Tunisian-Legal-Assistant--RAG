## English Translation:

I. The following companies and other legal entities, regardless of their purpose and those carrying out their activities in the Tunisian territory, are subject to the corporate income tax, taking into account the provisions of Article 4 of this Code:

1. Companies as stipulated in Article 7 of the Commercial Companies Code. (Amended by Article 62-1 of Law No. 98 of the year 2000 dated December 25, 2000).

2. Production, consumption, or service cooperatives and their federations.

3. Public institutions and bodies with industrial and commercial characteristics, enjoying financial autonomy and affiliated with the State, the Governorates, or municipalities. 

4. Civil companies, if it is established that they actually possess the characteristics of capital companies.

5. Partners in partnership companies, members of economic interest groups, and joint owners of joint debt funds as stipulated in Article 4 of this Code, which have the legal form of taxable entities subject to corporate income tax. (Amended by Article 56 of Decree-Law No. 123 of the year 2001 dated December 28, 2001).

6. Associations that do not carry out their activities according to the provisions of the legislation pertaining to them. (Added by Article 21 of Decree-Law No. 59 of the year 2014 dated December 26, 2014).

7. (Added by Article 11-1 of Decree-Law No. 78 of the year 2019 dated December 23, 2019) Joint fund as stipulated in the Insurance Code, as amended and supplemented by subsequent provisions, particularly Law No. 47 of the year 2014 dated July 24, 2014.

II. The corporate income tax is also applicable to non-resident and non-established legal entities in the Tunisian territory who generate income in Tunisia or realize a capital gain resulting from the transfer of real estate located in Tunisia or the rights attached to it, or the social rights in civil companies, real estate companies, not attached to establishments located in Tunisia, in respect of these incomes or this capital gain alone. 

The capital gain subject to corporate income tax resulting from the transfer of real estate or the rights mentioned in this paragraph shall be equal to the difference between the transfer price and the cost price or the acquisition price.

The tax is not levied on the income referred to in the third paragraph of Article 3 of this Code. (Amended by Article 43 of Decree-Law No. 111 of the year 1990 dated December 31, 1990, supplemented by Article 41 of Decree-Law No. 111 of the year 1998 dated December 28, 1998, and amended by Article 46 of Decree-Law No. 101 of the year 2002 dated December 17, 2002, and by Article 41-11 of Decree-Law No. 58 of the year 2010 dated December 17, 2010).

The tax is also not levied on premiums or membership fees for reinsurance ceded, or premiums for insurance or membership fees paid to reinsurers, subject to the principle of reciprocity. (Added by Article 3-4-7 of Decree-Law No. 54 of December 2013 and amended by Article 11-2 of Decree-Law No. 78 of the year 2019 dated December 23, 2019).

III. The corporate income tax is also applicable to non-resident and non-established legal entities in the Tunisian territory in respect of the capital gain referred to in the second paragraph of Article 3 of this Code. The capital gain subject to taxation is determined according to the provisions of Article 33 of this Code.

The tax is not levied on the capital gain referred to in the second, third, fourth, and fifth sub-paragraphs of paragraph 1 of Article 11 of this Code and on the capital gain referred to in paragraph VII, fourthly, of Article 48 of this Code accruing to non-resident and non-established legal entities in the Tunisian territory. (Added by Article 41-10 of Decree-Law No. 58 of the year 2010 dated December 17, 2010, and amended by Article 10 of Decree No. 100 of the year 2011 dated October 21, 2011).
