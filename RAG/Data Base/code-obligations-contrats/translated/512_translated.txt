The judge may not direct the oath of assessment concerning the value to the claimant unless it cannot be determined otherwise, and he may consider the consequences of the oath.  The oath, which one party or litigant directs to the other party to resolve the dispute between them, if the party lacks the evidence permitted by law to prove his claim, resorting to the conscience, integrity and debt of this party.  And in reducing the amount claimed if it is deemed excessive.
