Employee

An employee is a worker who works for wages.

The manufacturer is liable for any wrongdoing or negligence committed by those acting on his behalf in the manufacture, or by those he employed or used, as if it were committed by him.  He is not liable if the nature of the service or manufacture necessitated him to seek assistance from others, provided that he proves: Firstly, that he exerted every effort in selecting those he assisted and in supervising them.

Secondly, that he did everything necessary to prevent harm.

Harm
[1] Harm is injury inflicted in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. [2] Loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, or damage to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, harm is the loss actually suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he has been or will be forced to incur to repair the consequences of the act committed to his detriment, as well as what he has been deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act or to remedy its consequences.
