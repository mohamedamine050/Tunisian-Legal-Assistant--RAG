Guarantor

[1]  A guarantor is one who undertakes the obligation of another while the obligation remains on the original obligor, or it is the addition of one person's liability to that of another for an obligation, whether present or future. [2] This means a person who provides or issues a guarantee or surety bond payable on demand, a letter of guarantee, or any other form of guarantee; this is for the purpose of securing the fulfillment of any obligation in favor of a creditor.

For what he received in his capacity as an agent, based on Articles 1005, 1006, and 1018 to 1028 above. However, if the agent is compensated, his guarantee is governed by Article 1021 above.
