It is permissible to quash a final and binding judgment in the following instances:

Firstly: If the forgery of documents or other evidence upon which the judgment was based is proven, and this forgery was the original or sole cause of its issuance.

Secondly: If it is proven that the judgment was based on a manifest error that was the original or sole cause of its issuance.

Thirdly: If facts are proven that necessitate the disciplinary action of the judge.
