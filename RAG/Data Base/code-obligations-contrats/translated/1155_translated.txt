The principal is not bound by acts of the agent that exceed the limits of the agency or are outside of it, except in the following instances:

First: If the principal has knowledge of and approved the act, even without express authorization.

Second: If the principal benefited from the act.

Third: If the agent deviated from the principal's instructions in a manner that is more advantageous.

Fourth: If the agent deviated from the principal's instructions by increasing costs, provided that the difference is insignificant or is within the tolerance accepted in commerce or the custom of the place of contract.
