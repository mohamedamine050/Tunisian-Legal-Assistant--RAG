Every person shall guarantee compensation for damages.

Damages [1] are harm inflicted in violation of the law, affecting a person’s body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation; [2]  loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, impairment of physical well-being or reputation, or similar.  - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damages are the losses actually suffered by the claimant, the necessary expenses they were or will be compelled to incur to repair the consequences of the act that caused them harm, as well as any benefit they were deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of that act.
