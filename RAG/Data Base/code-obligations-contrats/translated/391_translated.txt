The claim shall not be extinguished by the passage of time in the following instances:

Firstly: Between spouses so long as their marriage contract remains in effect.  The contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their rights and obligations regarding the specific transaction.  Their marriage between them.

Secondly: Between parents and their children.

Thirdly: Between a minor and legal entities, and between a principal and an agent, manager, or guardian, unless the mandate is revoked, the agency is terminated, or their account is definitively settled.
