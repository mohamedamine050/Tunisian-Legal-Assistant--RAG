The claim is extinguished by the passage of time in the manner prescribed in Articles 403, 404, 405, and 406, even if dealings continue thereafter between those mentioned therein by way of supply, receipt, and services.  [1] A claim arises when one person requests a right from another in the presence of a judge; the requester is called the claimant and the one from whom it is requested is called the defendant. [2] A claim is the right of anyone with a demand to bring it before the court for judgment on its subject matter.  For the defendant, it is the right to present grounds of defense or arguments aimed at refuting that demand.
