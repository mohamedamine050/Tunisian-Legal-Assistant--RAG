Notwithstanding the provisions of Article 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989, dated December 30, 1989, relating to the issuance of the Tax Code on Personal Income and Corporate Income Tax, the following shall be deducted from the income tax base:

- Taxes withheld on total income or income components.
- Two-thirds of the income derived from:

- Direct investments within the meaning of Article 3 of Law No. [Insert Law Number] relating to investments carried out by child care and senior care institutions, education and research institutions, vocational training institutions, production institutions, cultural industries and youth activation, entertainment, health and hospital institutions, and direct investments within the meaning of Article 3 of Law No. [Insert Law Number] relating to investments in private university housing projects. The concerned activities are regulated by a government order.

- Direct investments within the meaning of Article 3 of Law No. [Insert Law Number] relating to investments carried out by institutions specialized in collecting, converting, valorizing, recycling, or treating waste and refuse.

The deduction provided for above shall be applied under the same conditions to the exceptional profits referred to in paragraph 1 bis of Article 11 of this Code.

Benefiting from this deduction requires maintaining accounting records in accordance with the accounting legislation for institutions.

Profits derived from direct investments within the meaning of Article 3 of Law No. [Insert Law Number] relating to investments in supporting and combating pollution activities mentioned above, as well as exceptional profits referred to in paragraph 1 bis of Article 11 of this Code, shall be subject to corporate income tax under the same conditions and at the rate provided for in paragraph 1 of paragraph 4 of paragraph I of Article 49 of this Code. (Amended by Article 16-7 of Legislative Decree No. 56 of 2018, dated December 27, 2018).
