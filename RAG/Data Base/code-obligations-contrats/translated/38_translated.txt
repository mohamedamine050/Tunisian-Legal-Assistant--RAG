4.  Payments, grants, and services provided in any capacity whatsoever pursuant to the legislation on first aid, insurance, and social security.

(Amended by Article 41 of Law No. 101 of 2002, dated December 17, 2002).

6.  Specific payments granted to cover the user’s job-related or work-related expenses, if these payments are justified.

7.  Housing savings surplus paid to holders of housing savings contracts. (Amended by Article 30 of Law No. 98 of 1991, dated December 31, 1991).

8.  Surplus on foreign currency or convertible dinar bonds.

 - Shares listed on the Tunis Stock Exchange that were subscribed to or acquired before January 1, 2011, or shares as part of a listing process on the Tunis Stock Exchange, shares listed on the Tunis Stock Exchange that are subscribed to or acquired from January 1, 2011, if they are disposed of or transferred after the end of the year following the year of subscription or acquisition;

  - Shares of variable capital investment companies and units of collective investment funds in securities provided for in Article 10 of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions issued by Law No. 83 of 2001, dated July 24, 2001;

 - Units of assistance funds for start-ups provided for in Law No. 58 of 2005, dated July 18, 2005, and units of collective investment funds in development capital provided for in Article 22, thirdly, of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions issued by Law No. 83 of 2001, dated July 24, 2001, which use their assets to subscribe to units of the assistance funds for start-ups mentioned above in accordance with the legislation governing them;

 - Shares and social bonds disposed of or transferred for the account of third parties, natural persons, by variable capital investment companies that were subscribed to or acquired under paragraph 1 of Article 77 of this Code, as well as units of collective investment funds in development capital subscribed to under paragraph II of the same article, if the disposal or transfer is carried out after the end of the fifth year following the year of subscription to or acquisition of the shares, social bonds, and units, up to a limit of 50% of the capital gain realized. (Amended by Article 15 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

  - Shares and social bonds disposed of or transferred for the account of third parties, natural persons, by variable capital investment companies that were subscribed to or acquired under paragraph III of Article 77 of this Code, as well as units of collective investment funds in capital subscribed to under paragraph IV of the same article, (Amended by Article 15 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

(Amended by Article 9-2 of Decree No. 100 of 2011, dated October 21, 2011).

 To benefit from the exemption, the annual tax return for the year in which the exemption is applied must be accompanied by the abovementioned commitment, certified by the Capital Market Authority.

 The income tax in respect of the capital gain that benefited from the exemption under the provisions of this paragraph is to be supplemented by late payment penalties as provided for in the legislation in force, if the beneficiaries of the exemption have not deposited with the competent performance monitoring center or office, a certificate certifying that the parent company or holding company benefiting from the contribution has been listed on the Tunis Stock Exchange no later than the end of the third month following the end of the deadline specified above.

 Late payment penalties are not due if a certificate is provided by the Capital Market Authority attesting that the failure to list the parent company or holding company on the Tunis Stock Exchange is due to causes beyond its control. (Added by Article 32 of Law No. 85 of 2006, dated December 25, 2006).
