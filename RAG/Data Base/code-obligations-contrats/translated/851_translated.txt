The lease for service or manufacture terminates in one of the following ways:

First: By the expiry of the term stipulated in the contract, or upon completion of the manufacture or service for which the lease was entered into.

Second: By judicial termination of the contract in the manners specified by law.  The contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights concerning the specific transaction.

Third: If the completion of the service or manufacture becomes impossible due to an unforeseen event or force majeure occurring before or after commencement, or if the service provider dies, except as otherwise provided by law. The lease does not terminate upon the death of the lessor.
