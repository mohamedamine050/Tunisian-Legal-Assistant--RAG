The sale of a commercial or industrial establishment includes the commercial registers, reports, documents, letters, and papers related to the sale, as well as the machinery and other things necessary for its operation, and the signs and images placed on the door of the establishment indicating the type of industry or trade, the trademark or brand, and all materials and goods contained therein, provided that these are known and have a reputation for attracting customers, and the certificates obtained by the seller for inventions related to his trade or industry, and trade secrets, unless the seller and buyer agree otherwise.

Excluded from the aforementioned sale are items specific to the seller, such as certificates obtained for academic achievements, awards, and stamps bearing his signature, unless the contract terms stipulate otherwise.  If the owner's name is included in the sign or image placed on the door of the establishment, the purchaser or assignee must add an indication of the transfer.
