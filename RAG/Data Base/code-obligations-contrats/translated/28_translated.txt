I. The gross income of real estate, whether fully or partially leased, consists of the sum of the gross rents received by the owner plus the amount of expenses normally borne by the owner and for which the lessee is liable as per the agreement, minus the amount of expenses borne by the owner on behalf of the lessee.

Gross rents include, in particular, rents derived from leasing rights of attachment, as well as the lease of ownership or exploitation rights.

II. To determine the net income of built properties, 20% of the gross income is deducted for management expenses, security, insurance, depreciation, as well as justified repair and maintenance costs, and the property tax paid on the rental value. (Amended by Article 21-1 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015).

The net income of unbuilt leased properties is determined by deducting from the gross income the justified expenses incurred in generating the income, as well as the property tax on unbuilt land that was paid.

III. The provisions of Articles 10 to 20 of this Code apply to persons who are proven to have kept accounting records in accordance with the accounting legislation for companies. (Amended by Article 76 of Law No. 88 of 1997, dated December 29, 1997).

IV. (Amended by Article 47-2 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, in relation to the first subparagraph of paragraph 1) For the application of paragraphs 2 and 3 of Article 27 of this Code, the excess value subject to tax is equal to the difference between the price of disposal of the properties and rights referred to, as declared or revised following tax audits, and the price of acquisition or donation or construction, including the value of the land, as declared or revised following tax audits, plus the amount of justified expenses and 10% for each year of ownership. 

The cost price is determined, in the case of disposal of usufruct rights and ownership rights, on the basis of a portion of the value of the property in accordance with the table provided in Article 38 of the Code of Registration and Stamp Duties.

In order to enable beneficiaries of international lands that have lost their agricultural character to obtain a certificate of relinquishment prior to the final disposal of these lands, the excess value is calculated on the basis of the value determined by an expert in state property. (Amended by Article 107 of Law No. 122 of 1992, dated December 1992, and by Article 5 of Law No. 73 of 1998, dated August 4, 1998, on the simplification of tax procedures and the reduction of tax rates).

The cost price is determined in the case of donations, compensation, and inherited properties on the basis of the values declared in the donation contracts, compensation contracts, or in the declarations filed for transfer upon death. (Amended by Article 60-22 of Law No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004).

The cost price is determined, in the case of disposals of donated properties between ascendants and descendants and between spouses, on the basis of their value at the date of ownership by the original donor. In this case, the ownership period is calculated from the date of ownership by the original donor. (Added by Article 4 of Law No. 69 of 2006, dated October 28, 2006, concerning the exemption of donations between ancestors and descendants and between spouses from proportional registration duty, and amended by Article 47-3 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013).
