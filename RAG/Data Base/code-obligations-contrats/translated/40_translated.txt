## English Translation of the Arabic Legal Text:

**I.** Every head of household, as defined in Article 5 of this Code, has the right to deduct 300 dinars from their net income. (Amended by Article 54-1 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017)

**II.** The head of household also has the right to deduct an additional 100 dinars for each of the first four children under their care. (Amended by Article 54-2 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017)

Children under the care of the taxpayer are considered to be their biological or adopted children who are under the age of 20 on January 1st of the year of tax assessment, provided that they do not have separate income from the income taxed in the taxpayer's name.

**III.** The deduction referred to in the second paragraph of this Article is increased to:

- 1000 dinars for each child pursuing higher education without benefiting from a grant, who is not over 25 years old on January 1st of the year of tax assessment. (Amended by Article 40-1 of Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2004 and by Article 94-1 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013)

- 2000 dinars for each disabled child, regardless of their age or grade. (Amended by Article 50 of Law No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004, by Article 40-2 of Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2009, by Article 94-2 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, and by Article 55 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017)

**IV.** In addition, every taxpayer has the right to deduct for each of their parents under their care, up to 5% of the net taxable income, with a maximum limit of 450 dinars, subject to the following conditions:

- The taxpayer must declare the deducted amount in the income statement of the beneficiary of the pension, which must be filed at the same time as the taxpayer's statement.

- The income of the parent or parents under care must not exceed twice the guaranteed minimum wage.

If multiple children care for their parents, the deduction amount is divided equally among all children.

**V.** (Added by Article 49 of Law No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004, amended by Article 93-2 of Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2009, and repealed by Article 73-2 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013)
