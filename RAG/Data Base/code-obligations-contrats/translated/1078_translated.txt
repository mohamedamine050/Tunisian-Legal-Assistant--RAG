There is no guarantee for the lender in the following instances:

First: If he was unaware of the claim or of any latent defects in the loan.

Second: If those defects or risks were apparent, such that they were not hidden from the borrower.

Third: If the borrower was informed of the existence of the defects or risks or of the claim.

Fourth: If the sole cause of the damage was the borrower's trespass or negligence.
