(Amended by Law No. 95 of 1995 dated November 9, 1995)

Every person shall guarantee compensation for damages.

[1] Harm inflicted in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation. [2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or impairment of physical well-being, reputation, or the like. – In crimes and quasi-crimes, damages are the losses actually suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he has been or will be compelled to incur to repair the consequences of the act that caused him harm, as well as what he has been deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act.

Damages arising from minors and others of unsound mind residing with them, even if they are of legal age, shall be compensated unless one of the following conditions is proven:

– That he exercised all necessary supervision. – That he exercised all necessary supervision. – That he was unaware of the dangerous condition of the injured party.
