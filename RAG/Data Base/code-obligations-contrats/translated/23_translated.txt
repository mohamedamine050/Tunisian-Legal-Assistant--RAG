The profits derived from the exploitation of agricultural or marine fishing land are considered income from the exploitation of agricultural properties that are enjoyed by the owners who exploit them themselves, or by farmers or others, as well as income derived from marine fishing.

These profits include, in particular, the crops produced from the cultivation of grains, vines, citrus fruits, olive trees, or other trees, as well as the raising of poultry, bees, or the cultivation of roses, vegetables, or forests, and the raising of livestock. It also includes crops derived from the breeding of marine organisms, and the rental of grazing rights, which are earned by any person who exploits the land as an owner or lessee.
