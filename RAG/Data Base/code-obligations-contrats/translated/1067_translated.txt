The lender may compel the borrower to return the loan, even before the borrower has benefited from it or before the agreed-upon period has elapsed, in the following circumstances:

First: If the lender is compelled to reclaim the loan due to an unforeseen and certain necessity.

Second: If the borrower misuses the loan or uses it for a purpose other than that for which it was lent.

Third: If the borrower fails to perform the necessary maintenance.
