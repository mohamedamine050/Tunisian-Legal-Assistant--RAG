A general power of attorney is the authorization granted to an agent to act on behalf of his principal in all matters, or in a specific matter.

By virtue of this power of attorney, the agent may do what is in the best interest of the principal, according to the nature of the transaction and commercial custom.  He is authorized to collect what is due to his principal from third parties, to pay the principal's debts, to take any action to preserve the principal's rights, to litigate concerning possession, and to act against the debtors of his principal and settle his accounts to the extent necessary to fulfill the mandate.
