Whoever informs about a non-existent fact concerning another, while believing in its truth without gross negligence or serious error, shall not be held liable for the obligation to respond to the person's actions before the law or to compensate for the damage caused to the person to whom the information relates, in either of the following two cases:

Firstly: If the informer or the person to whom the information reached has a legitimate interest in informing.

Secondly: If the informer has a commercial transaction or a legal duty that led him to inform about what he knows.
