II. (1) Withholding tax shall be exempt from income tax or corporate tax for the amounts stipulated in sub-paragraphs (b) and (c) of paragraph I of this Article granted to non-residents and non-established individuals in Tunisia, and sub-paragraphs (e), (c) bis, (c) ter of paragraph I of this Article, and sub-paragraph (3) of this paragraph. (Amended by Article 32, Act No. 113 of 1996, dated December 30, 1996; Article 71, Act No. 80 of 2003, dated December 29, 2003; Article 569, Act No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004; Article 19-5, Act No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013; and Article 19-5, Act No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015).

However, the withholding tax referred to in the fifth indent of sub-paragraph "b" of paragraph I of this Article may be deducted from the tax due on the capital gain referred to in paragraph II of Article 45 of this Code. (Added by Article 50, Act No. 101 of 2002, dated December 17, 2002).

Contracts transferring ownership of real estate and rights subject to a transfer between non-residents must be registered and accompanied by a certificate issued by the competent tax authorities attesting to the payment of the capital gains tax by the transferor. (Added by Article 92-2, Act No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013).

Persons subject to withholding tax on capital gains realized from the transfer or reassignment of bonds or related rights as stipulated in sub-paragraph "E bis" of paragraph I of this Article may choose to be subject to income tax for natural persons according to the provisions of Article 33, point 18 of Article 38, and paragraph 3 of paragraph III of Article 44 of this Code, or to corporate tax at a rate of 25% according to the provisions of the first sub-paragraph of paragraph III of Article 45 of this Code. (Added by Article 41-13, Act No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010, and deleted and replaced by Article 12-2 of Decree No. 100 of 2011, dated October 21, 2011).

In this case, the withholding tax is deducted from the income tax or corporate tax due on the capital gain resulting from the transfer of shares, social bonds, or the aforementioned shares according to the provisions of Article 54 of this Code. (Added by Article 41-13, Act No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

For transfers or reassignments between non-residents, companies issuing the bonds subject to the transfer or reassignment, and managers of funds stipulated in the relevant legislation, must require the new acquirer of the bonds or related rights to provide a certificate issued by the competent tax authorities attesting to the payment of the tax due on the capital gain from the transfer or reassignment of the bonds, the aforementioned shares, or related rights, or their non-liability, before granting them their share of distributed profits or income. (Added by Article 92-1, Act No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013).

Withholding tax performed on distributed income according to the provisions of this Article may be deducted from the annual income tax due or refunded for natural persons whose distributed income does not exceed 10,000 dinars annually. (Added by Article 19-6, Act No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013).


(2) Income from movable capital realized by legal persons not subject to corporate tax or wholly exempt therefrom under current legislation, as well as income from movable capital realized by the common fund for investment in securities stipulated in Law No. 83 of 2001, dated July 24, 2001, relating to the issuance of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions, shall be subject to a final and non-refundable withholding tax of 20%. This withholding tax shall be performed by the person paying this income. (Amended by Article 31, Act No. 98 of 1991, dated December 31, 1991; and Article 21, Act No. 123 of 2001, dated December 28, 2001).

The provisions of this paragraph apply to movable capital realized by start-up assistance funds and common funds for investment in development capital, as stipulated in the relevant legislation. (Added by Article 5 of Law No. 59 of 2005, dated July 18, 2005, relating to tax provisions aimed at encouraging the creation of start-up assistance funds, and amended by Article 25-2, Act No. 106 of 2005, dated December 19, 2005).

These provisions do not apply to income from movable capital in foreign currency or convertible dinars. (Added by Article 99, Act No. 122 of 1992, dated December 29, 1992, and amended by Article 43, Act No. 111 of 1998, dated December 28, 1998).


(3) (Added by Article 70, Act No. 80 of 2003, dated December 29, 2003) Non-resident individuals established in Tunisia whose activity in Tunisia does not exceed six months are subject to income tax or corporate tax through withholding tax applied to amounts accruing to them in this context at the following rates:

- 5% of gross turnover for construction work,

- 10% of gross turnover for installation operations,

- 15% of gross turnover or receipts for other services.

(Amended by Article 24-1, Act No. 59 of 2014, dated December 26, 2014).

However, those concerned may choose to be subject to income tax or corporate tax based on the net income or profits realized for the activities mentioned above, which are determined according to the provisions of this Code.

The choice is exercised through a request submitted to the competent tax office or control center when making the declaration of presence stipulated in Article 56 of this Code by those concerned. Those concerned shall inform the debtor of the amounts accruing to them for the aforementioned activities of this choice. (Amended by Article 24-2, Act No. 59 of 2014, dated December 26, 2014).

If the choice is made to be subject to income tax or corporate tax based on net profits realized in Tunisia for the activities mentioned above and there is a breach of tax and accounting obligations stipulated in this Code, the income tax or corporate tax due on the amounts accruing to them cannot be less than the tax calculated based on the withholding tax stipulated in the second sub-paragraph of this paragraph.

(4) (Added by Article 28, Act No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015) Non-resident individuals established in Tunisia who have not filed the declaration of presence required by Article 56 of this Code are subject to tax through a withholding tax of 15% of their gross amounts.

This rate is increased to 25% if it concerns individuals established in Tunisia and residing in a country or territory with a preferential tax system within the meaning of number 12 of Article 14 of this Code. (Added by Article 50, Act No. 56 of 2018, dated December 27, 2018).

This withholding tax is exempt from income tax for natural persons or corporate tax due on their activity in Tunisia.

However, if those concerned settle their tax situation according to current legislation, the aforementioned withholding tax is deducted from the income tax for natural persons or corporate tax due on their activity in Tunisia, or is refundable according to current legislation.


III. Income tax due on salaries, wages, fees, and lifetime income, including the value of in-kind benefits, requires mandatory withholding at source by the employer or the party obligated to pay the income or fees established or resident in Tunisia.

Taxpayers who receive salaries or wages from companies, associations, or individuals residing or established outside Tunisia must withhold tax calculated according to the same conditions as the withholding referred to in the first sub-paragraph of this paragraph. (Amended by Article 36, Act No. 85 of 2006, dated December 25, 2006).


IV. Withholding taxes performed under any heading whatsoever shall be paid to the relevant tax office:

- By natural persons, within fifteen days of the month following the month in which these withholdings were made, and within the first fifteen days of the month following each trimester of the calendar year in which these withholdings were made for the persons referred to in Article 44 bis of this Code, and within the first fifteen days of January and July following the semester in which these withholdings were made for persons active in the sector of passenger transport by taxis and rural transport vehicles subject to the estimated system provided for in Article 44 bis of this Code. (Amended by Article 54-1, Act No. 106, dated December 19, 2005; Article 62, Act No. 70 of 2007, dated December 27, 2007; and Article 337, Act No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

- By legal persons within the first twenty-eight days of the same month.

However, withholding tax exempt from income tax or corporate tax, which has not been performed, is considered outstanding to the actual debtor and the aforementioned withholding tax in this case shall be due according to the following tax burden rule:

100 x withholding tax rate
-------------------------
100 - withholding tax rate


(Amended by Article 33, Act No. 125 of 1993, dated December 27, 1993; and Article 23, Act No. 59 of 2014, dated December 26, 2014).

The withholding tax rate mentioned in the third indent of sub-paragraph "b" and sub-paragraphs "c", "c" bis, and "e" of paragraph 1 of this Article is increased to 25% if it concerns rewards or income accruing to persons residing or established in a country or territory with a preferential tax system within the meaning of number 12 of Article 14 of this Code. (Amended by Article 35-4, Act No. 56 of 2018, dated December 27, 2018).

(The last paragraph of this Article was deleted by Article 35-5, Act No. 56 of 2018, dated December 2018).
