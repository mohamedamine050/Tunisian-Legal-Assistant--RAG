I. Except for those engaged in agriculture, fishing, and individual entities subject to the estimated tax referred to in Article 44 bis of this Code, legal persons subject to corporate tax and natural persons subject to income tax engaged in a commercial activity or a non-commercial profession shall pay three advance payments called "provisional installments" representing the tax due on their gross income or profits. (Amended by Article 28, Law No. 113 of 1996 dated December 30, 1996, Article 62, Law No. 111 of 1998 dated December 28, 1998, and Article 35-1, Law No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010).

Natural persons engaged in traditional crafts are exempt from paying the first provisional installment referred to in Section IV of this Article. (Amended by Article 36, Law No. 125 of 1993 dated December 27, 1993).

Persons referred to in Article 4 of this Code are exempt from paying provisional installments in respect of:
Income tax
Income tax
Withholding taxes on total income or income elements
due on their share of profits and income generated by the companies, groups, and funds referred to in the same Article. (Added by Article 56, Law No. 101 of 1999 dated December 31, 1999 and amended by Article 57, Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001).

II. The provisional installments payable from the second year of activity are collected in installments, each equal to 30% of the tax due on the income or profits of the previous year.

Capital gains or losses resulting from the disposal of tangible fixed assets, intangible fixed assets, and securities portfolio are not taken into account in determining the basis for calculating provisional installments; however, the basis for calculating provisional installments shall not be less than the minimum tax provided for in Articles 44 and 49 of this Code. (Added by Article 45, Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006).

III. Amounts deducted from the source in the form of advance payments, as provided for in Article 52 of this Code, are deducted from the provisional installments payable under Section 1 of this Article.

In the event of partial or total inability to apply this deduction, the remaining installments are deducted from future installments or, if necessary, from:
Income tax
Income tax
Withholding taxes on total income or income elements
or from corporate tax.

IV. Provisional installments are declared and paid:

- Within the first twenty-five days of June, September, and December following the closing date of the financial year for natural persons.

- Within the first twenty-eight days of June, September, and December following the closing date of the financial year for legal persons. (Amended by Article 36, Law No. 125 of 1993 dated December 27, 1993 and Article 71, Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006).
