I. The corporate tax applies to the following companies and other legal persons, regardless of their purpose, operating in Tunisia, taking into account the provisions of Article 4 of this Code:

1. Companies stipulated in Article 7 of the Commercial Companies Code (amended by Article 62-1 of Law No. 98 of 2000, dated December 25, 2000).

2. Production, consumption, or service cooperatives and their federations.

3. Public establishments and bodies with an industrial and commercial nature, enjoying financial independence and affiliated with the State, governorates, or municipalities.

4. Civil companies, if it is established that they actually have the characteristics of capital companies.

5. Partners in limited partnerships, members of economic interest groupings, and joint owners in common debt funds stipulated in Article 4 of this Code, which have the form of legal persons subject to corporate tax (amended by Article 56 of Law No. 123 of 2001, dated December 28, 2001).

6. Associations that do not operate in accordance with the provisions of the legislation concerning them (added by Article 21 of Law No. 59 of 2014, dated December 26, 2014).

7. (Added by Article 11-1 of Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019) The common fund stipulated in the Insurance Code, as amended and completed by subsequent texts, particularly Law No. 47 of 2014, dated July 24, 2014.


II. The corporate tax is also levied on non-resident and non-established legal persons in Tunisia who generate income originating in Tunisia, or realize capital gains resulting from the transfer of property located in Tunisia, or rights relating thereto, or social rights in civil companies, relating to real estate not attached to establishments located in Tunisia, only for the amount of this income or capital gain.

The capital gain subject to corporate tax, resulting from the transfer of the aforementioned property or rights in this paragraph, equals the difference between the sale price and the cost or acquisition price.

The tax is not levied on income stipulated in paragraph three of Article 3 of this Code (amended by Article 43 of Law No. 111 of 1990, dated December 31, 1990; completed by Article 41 of Law No. 111 of 1998, dated December 28, 1998; amended by Article 46 of Law No. 101 of 2002, dated December 17, 2002; and by Article 41-11 of Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

The tax is also not levied on premiums or contributions for reinsurance ceded, and on premiums or contributions paid to reinsurers, provided that the principle of reciprocity is applied (added by Article 3-4-7 of Law No. 54 of December 2013 and amended by Article 11-2 of Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019).


III. The corporate tax is also levied on non-resident and non-established legal persons in Tunisia, for the capital gain stipulated in paragraph two of Article 3 of this Code. The capital gain subject to tax is determined according to the provisions of Article 33 of this Code.

The tax is not levied on the capital gain stipulated in subparagraphs two, three, four, and five of paragraph I of Article 11 of this Code, and on the capital gain stipulated in paragraph VII, fourthly, of Article 48 of this Code, realized for the benefit of non-resident and non-established legal persons in Tunisia (added by Article 41-10 of Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010, and amended by Article 10 of Decree No. 100 of 2011, dated October 21, 2011).
