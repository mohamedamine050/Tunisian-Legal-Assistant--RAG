I. The net income from non-commercial activities consists of the difference between the gross receipts realized during the tax year and the expenses incurred during the same year.

The provisions of Articles 10 to 20 of this Code apply to persons who demonstrate that they maintain accounting records in accordance with the accounting legislation for institutions (amended by Article 76 of Law No. 88 of 1997, dated December 29, 1997).

II. However, those concerned may choose to be taxed on the basis of an estimated profit equal to 80% of their gross receipts, upon submission of their income tax return.

III. If those concerned are subject to income tax for a given year according to the actual system, this system is final and not subject to any form of appeal. (Added by Article 71 of Law No. 123, dated December 28, 2001).
