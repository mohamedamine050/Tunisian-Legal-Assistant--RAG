Exempt from corporate tax within the limits of their social purpose are:

1. Professional associations that do not primarily carry out profit-making activities; the excess of a company's assets over its liabilities by including the company's capital within its debts at the end of the fiscal year; and which have resources of a fiscal or para-fiscal origin.

2. (Repealed by Article 28-1 of Law No. 66 of 2017 dated December 18, 2017).

3. Savings and thrift funds whose administration is free of charge.

4. Public establishments and bodies of the State or local public authorities that do not have a profit-making purpose.

5. Service cooperatives whose activity contributes to the trade in agricultural crops or fishing and operating within wholesale markets.

6. Agricultural service cooperatives (Amended by Article 51 of Law No. 94 of 2005 dated October 18, 2005, relating to agricultural service cooperatives).

7. Workers' production cooperatives.

8. Loan fund and assistance to local communities. (Added by Article 62 of Law No. 125 of 1993 dated December 27, 1993).

9. Variable capital investment companies as provided for in Law No. 83 of 2001 dated July 24, 2001, relating to the issuance of the Code of Collective Investment Undertakings. (Added by Article 5 of Law No. 88 of 1995 dated October 30, 1995, concerning special tax provisions for investment companies and amended by Article 94 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001).
