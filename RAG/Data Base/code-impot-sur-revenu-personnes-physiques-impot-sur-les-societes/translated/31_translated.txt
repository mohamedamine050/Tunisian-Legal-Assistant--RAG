I. If a legal entity, possessing legal personality and separate assets and financial liabilities independent from the financial liabilities of its members or shareholders, is no longer considered a company subject to corporate tax, its profits and reserves integrated into the capital for less than five years shall be distributed among the partners according to their respective shares.

II. Except in the case of a single-member limited liability company as stipulated in the Commercial Companies Code, the collection of all shares or social assets by a single partner constitutes a case of cessation of activity. (Amended by Article 622-2 of Law No. 98 of December 2000).
