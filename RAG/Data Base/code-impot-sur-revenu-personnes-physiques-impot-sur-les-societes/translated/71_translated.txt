Regardless of the provisions of Articles 12 and 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989, dated December 30, 1989, relating to the issuance of the tax code on the income of natural persons and corporate tax, establishments, other than those operating in the financial sector and energy sectors, except for renewable energies, mines, real estate development, on-site consumption, commerce, and telecommunications network operators, shall deduct a percentage of their profits or income derived from exploitation for the first four years of activity, as well as the exceptional profits provided for in paragraph I bis of Article 11 of this code and under the same conditions, determined as follows:

- 100% for the first year,

- 75% for the second year,

- 50% for the third year,

- 25% for the fourth year.

Benefiting from this deduction requires maintaining accounting records in accordance with the accounting legislation for establishments.

The provisions of this article also apply to establishments experiencing economic difficulties that are referred to under paragraph II of Article 11 bis of this code, with respect to income or profits derived from exploitation for the first four years starting from the date of referral.  The deduction is granted based on a decision from the Minister in charge of Finance or from a person authorized by the Minister in charge of Finance.

Benefiting from the aforementioned deduction requires fulfilling the conditions stipulated in Article 39, fourthly, of this code.
