I. Each head of household, as defined in Article 5 of this Code, has the right to deduct 300 dinars from their net income. (Amended by Article 54-1, Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017).

II. The head of household also has the right to an additional deduction of 100 dinars for each of the first four children under their care. (Amended by Article 54-2, Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017).

Children considered under the taxpayer's care are their biological or adopted children under the age of 20 on January 1st of the tax year, provided they do not have separate income from that declared by the aforementioned taxpayer.

III. The deduction mentioned in the second paragraph of this article is increased to:

- 1000 dinars for each child pursuing higher education without receiving a grant, not exceeding 25 years of age on January 1st of the tax year. (Amended by Article 40-1, Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2004 and by Article 94-1, Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013).

- 2000 dinars for each disabled child, regardless of age or order of birth. (Amended by Article 50, Law No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004; Article 40-2, Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2009; Article 94-2, Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013; and Article 55, Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017).

IV. In addition, each taxpayer has the right to deduct, for each parent under their care, up to 5% of their net taxable income, with a maximum of 450 dinars, provided that:

- The deducted amount is declared in the income tax return of the pension beneficiary, which must be submitted at the same time as the taxpayer's return.

- The income of the parent(s) under care does not exceed twice the guaranteed minimum industrial wage.

If more than one child supports the parents, the deduction amount is divided among all children.

V. (Added by Article 49, Law No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004; amended by Article 93-2, Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2009; and repealed by Article 73-2, Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 2013).
