Not Subject to Tax:

1.  Life annuities and periodic grants paid to victims of workplace accidents or their beneficiaries.

2.  Life annuities paid as compensation for harm; [1] injury inflicted in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or assets; [2] loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like.  - In crimes and quasi-crimes, harm is the loss actually suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he has been or will be compelled to incur to remedy the consequences of the act that caused him harm, as well as what he has been deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act - by virtue of a judgment; a written court decision resolving a legal or factual issue, for bodily harm suffered.

3.  Salaries, wages, and grants paid by foreign states to their personnel assigned to the Tunisian government within the framework of technical cooperation.

4. Payments, grants, and services provided in any capacity whatsoever in accordance with the legislation relating to first aid, insurance, and social security.

5.  End-of-service benefits, within the limits defined by the legislation relating to employment, or within the limits of the amounts defined within the framework of redundancy operations for economic reasons and approved by the Committee; a formal method of assembling persons with related expertise from various parts of the institution - overseeing dismissal or labor inspection - or defined within the framework of decisions of the Committee; a formal method of assembling persons with related expertise from various parts of the institution -  sanitation and restructuring of establishments with public shareholdings. (Amended by Article 41 of Law No. 101 of 2002, dated December 17, 2002).

6.  Specific payments allocated to cover job-related expenses borne by employees, provided that these payments are justified.

7.  Savings surpluses paid to holders of housing savings contracts. (Amended by Article 30 of Law No. 98 of 1991, dated December 31, 1991).

8.  Surpluses of investments and bonds in convertible foreign currency or dinars.

9.  Surpluses of current accounts opened between industrialists, traders, and agricultural operators, provided that the transactions recorded in the current account are strictly related to the profession.

10. (Amended by Article 50 of Law No. 127 of 1994, dated December 26, 1994, and by Article 23 of Law No. 123 of 2001, dated December 28, 2001, and amended by Article 19-2 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 2013, with the cancellation provisions applying to distributions made from January 1, 2015).

11.  Expatriation allowance, bonuses, grants, and other benefits received by employees for their activities abroad, provided that the employer is resident or established in Tunisia and that the activity relates to:

    - Technical, economic, social, or environmental studies, or technical assistance.
    - Construction, installation, maintenance operations, or related monitoring activities.

    (Added by Article 60 of Law No. 88 of 1997, dated December 29, 1997).

12. Interest on savings accounts for education opened with banks for the benefit of children. The conditions for applying this measure are set by order. (Added by Article 45 of Law No. 111 of 1998, dated December 28, 1998).

13. (Repealed by Article 15-18 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

14. Contributions or membership fees paid by lessors under collective life insurance contracts or collective life insurance contracts for capital formation or collective insurance contracts for capital formation, as provided for in paragraph 2 of paragraph I of Article 39 of this Code. (Added by Article 47 of Law No. 123 of 2001, dated December 28, 2001, and amended by Article 24-5 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, and by Article 12 of Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019).

15. Surpluses or profits from investment savings accounts provided for in Article 39 bis of this Code, up to 4,000 dinars per year. (Added by Article 22 of Law No. 101 of 2002, dated December 17, 2002, and amended by Article 27-2 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017).

16. Amounts paid under insurance contracts or mutual insurance contracts as provided for in paragraph 2 of paragraph I of Article 39 of this Code, except for amounts paid under life insurance contracts or mutual life insurance contracts for capital formation or mutual insurance contracts for capital formation, in execution of the commitments of the subscriber or member as provided for in the legislation in force. (Added by Article 44 of Law No. 80 of 2003, dated December 29, 2003, and amended by Article 24-6 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, and by Article 12 of Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019).

17. The capital gains provided for in the second paragraph of Article 3 and in Article 31 bis of this Code, resulting from the securities and related rights mentioned below:

    - Shares listed on the Tunisian Stock Exchange subscribed to or acquired before January 1, 2011, or shares as part of a listing operation on the Tunisian Stock Exchange;
    - Shares listed on the Tunisian Stock Exchange subscribed to or acquired from January 1, 2011, if they are disposed of or transferred after the end of the year following the year of subscription or acquisition;
    - Shares of variable capital investment companies and units of common investment funds in securities, as provided for in Article 10 of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions issued by Law No. 83 of 2001, dated July 24, 2001;
    - Units of start-up assistance funds, as provided for in Law No. 58 of 2005, dated July 18, 2005, and units of common investment funds in development capital, as provided for in Article 22, thirdly, of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions issued by Law No. 83 of 2001, dated July 24, 2001, which use their assets to subscribe to units of the aforementioned start-up assistance funds, in accordance with the relevant legislation;
    - Shares and social bonds disposed of or transferred to third parties by natural persons from development capital investment companies subscribed to or acquired under paragraph 1 of Article 77 of this Code, as well as units of common investment funds in development capital subscribed to under paragraph II of the same Article, if the disposal or transfer takes place after the end of the fifth year following the year of subscription or acquisition of the shares, social bonds, and units, up to 50% of the capital gain realized. (Amended by Article 15 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).
    - Shares and social bonds disposed of or transferred to third parties by natural persons from development capital investment companies subscribed to or acquired under paragraph III of Article 77 of this Code and units of common investment funds in development capital subscribed to under paragraph IV of the same Article. (Amended by Article 15 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).
    - Shares and social bonds representing a contribution to the capital of the parent company or holding company, provided that the parent company or holding company undertakes to list its shares on the Tunisian Stock Exchange within a maximum period of the end of the year following the year of exemption. This period may be extended by one year by decision of the Minister of Finance on the basis of a reasoned report from the Financial Market Authority. (Amended by Article 9-2 of Decree No. 100 of 2011, dated October 21, 2011).

    To benefit from the exemption, the annual tax return for the year of exemption must be accompanied by the aforementioned commitment, certified by the Financial Market Authority.  Income tax on capital gains that benefited from the exemption under the provisions of this paragraph shall include the late payment penalties due under the legislation in force if the beneficiaries of the exemption do not deposit with the competent performance monitoring center or office a certificate attesting to the listing of the parent company or holding company on the Tunisian Stock Exchange within a maximum period of the end of the third month following the expiry of the aforementioned period.  Late payment penalties shall not be due if a certificate issued by the Financial Market Authority attests that the failure to list the parent company or holding company on the Tunisian Stock Exchange is due to reasons beyond its control. (Added by Article 32 of Law No. 85 of 2006, dated December 25, 2006).

18. Capital gains provided for in Article 31 bis or capital gains provided for in the second paragraph of Article 3 of this Code, up to 10,000 dinars per year. (Added by Article 63 of Law No. 80 of 2003, dated December 29, 2003, and amended by Article 3-4-7 of Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

18 bis. (Added by Article 41-8 of Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010, and repealed by Article 9-3 of Decree No. 100 of 2011, dated October 21, 2011).

19. (Added by Article 13 of Law No. 85 of 2006, dated December 25, 2006, and its provisions repealed by Article 15-16 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

20. Income from the rental of agricultural land allocated to large-scale farming under lease agreements concluded for a period of not less than three years.

    To benefit from this exemption:

    - The lessee must undertake in the lease agreement to allocate the land to large-scale farming;
    - The income tax return must be accompanied by a certificate issued by the competent regional departments of the ministry responsible for agriculture attesting that the land subject to the lease was allocated to large-scale farming in accordance with agricultural practices during the year concerned by the exemption. (Added by Article 13-1 of Law No. 77 of 2008, dated December 22, 2008).

21. Grants awarded within the framework of interventions by the National Employment Fund and grants awarded by institutions within the same framework. (Added by Article 28-1 of Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

22. Allowances provided for in Decree No. 97 of 2011, dated October 24, 2011, concerning compensation for martyrs and victims of the January 14, 2011 revolution. (Added by Article 13 of Decree No. 97 of 2011, dated October 24, 2011).

23. (Added by Article 5-73-1 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, and repealed by Article 3-14-5 of Law No. 78 of 2016, dated December 17, 2016).

24. Amounts resulting from pari-mutuel betting on horse races and sports prediction contests organized by public institutions in accordance with the relevant legislation. (Added by Article 56-1 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017).

25. (Added by Article 44 of Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019) Value of the allowance granted for transport to and from the workplace by establishments located in regional development zones and carrying out activities not excluded from benefiting from regional development benefits, as defined by the legislation in force.
