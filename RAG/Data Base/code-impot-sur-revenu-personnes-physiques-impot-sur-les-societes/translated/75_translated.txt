To benefit from the provisions of Articles 73 and 74 of this Code, in addition to the conditions stipulated in paragraphs 1, 2, and 4 of Article 72, paragraph 3 of this Code, the following conditions must be met (amended by Article 21-2 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017):

- Maintaining accounts in accordance with the accounting legislation for institutions, for persons carrying out industrial or commercial activities or a non-commercial profession as defined in this Code.  The shares or social contributions must be newly issued.

-  No reduction in the subscribed capital for five years starting from January 1st of the year following the year in which the subscribed capital was released, except in the case of a reduction to absorb losses.

- Beneficiaries of the offering must attach a tax declaration on the income of natural persons or corporate tax, with a certificate of release of the subscribed capital or its equivalent.

- No transfer of shares or social contributions that benefited from the offering before the end of the two years following the year of release of the subscribed capital.

-  Agreements concluded between companies and subscribers must not include guarantees or bonuses unrelated to the results of the subscription process.

- Profits or income reinvested must be recorded in a special account under liabilities in the budget, not distributable except in the event of transfer of shares or social contributions that benefited from the offering. This applies to companies and persons carrying out industrial or commercial activities or a non-commercial profession as defined in this Code.

Reinvestment income or profits referred to in this paragraph means income or profits generated by accounting compliant with the accounting legislation for institutions that have not been distributed or allocated to any other purpose, within the limits of taxable income or profits.
