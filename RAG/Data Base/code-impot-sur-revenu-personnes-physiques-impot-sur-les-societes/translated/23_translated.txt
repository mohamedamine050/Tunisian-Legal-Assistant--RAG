Profits from agricultural or maritime fishing exploitation are considered income from the exploitation of agricultural properties, benefiting owners who exploit them themselves, farmers, or others, as well as income derived from maritime fishing.

These profits specifically include the harvests resulting from the cultivation of grains, vines, fruit trees, olive trees, and other trees; the raising of poultry and bees; the cultivation of flowers and vegetables; forests; livestock farming; as well as the harvests resulting from the raising of marine organisms and the rental of grazing rights, which are achieved by any person exploiting as an owner or lessee.
