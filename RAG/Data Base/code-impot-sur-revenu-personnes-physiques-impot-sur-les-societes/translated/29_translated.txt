I. Legal entities whose distributed profits are considered income from securities are:

1. Companies subject to corporate tax, as well as cooperatives and their federations.

2. Joint ventures that effectively possess a legal form making them subject to corporate tax.

3. Tunisian establishments of foreign companies that have the form of companies referred to in subparagraphs 1 and 2 of the first paragraph of this section.


II.  The following are considered distributed income:

a. All profits or proceeds not allocated to reserves and not integrated into capital.

b. All sums or values placed at the disposal of partners, shareholders, and warrant holders not deducted from profits.

c. Profits earned in Tunisia by the entities referred to in subparagraph 3 of the first paragraph of this section, considered distributed to partners not residing in Tunisia.


II bis.  Also considered distributed income are the income from shares of collective investment funds in securities as provided for in the Code of Collective Investment Institutions issued by Law No. 83 of 2001 dated July 24, 2001, as well as shares of start-up assistance funds provided for in Law No. 58 of 2005 dated July 18, 2005, excluding the capital gains relating to these shares or the rights related thereto, as provided for in the second paragraph of Article 3 and in the second paragraph of Article 31 bis of this Code. (Added by Article 22 of Amending Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001 and amended by Article 2 of Law No. 59 of 2005 dated July 18, 2005 concerning tax provisions aimed at encouraging the creation of start-up assistance funds, and by Amending Law No. 106 of 2005 dated December 19, 2005, and by Article 6 of Decree No. 100 of 2011 dated October 21, 2011).

Distributed income does not include the insurance surplus distributed to subscribers after deducting the savings allocated to counteracting fluctuations in compensation rates and covering the deficit of the subscribers' fund as provided for in the Insurance Code, as amended and supplemented by subsequent texts, in particular Law No. 47 of 2014 dated July 24, 2014. (This paragraph was added by Article 11-7 of Amending Law No. 78 of 2019 dated December 23, 2019).


III. The profits referred to in subparagraph a of the second paragraph of this section consist of the profits forming the basis for calculating corporate tax.

However, these profits:

- Include tax-exempt profits or profits of foreign origin.

- Exclude corporate tax, penalties, fines, and non-deductible expenses from the corporate tax base, provided that this does not result in a decrease in net assets.
