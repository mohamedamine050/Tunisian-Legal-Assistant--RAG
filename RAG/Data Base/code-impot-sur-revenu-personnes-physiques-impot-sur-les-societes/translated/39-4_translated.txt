(Added by Article 14 of Law No. 85 of 2006, dated December 25, 2006, concerning the facilitation of the transfer of businesses).

I. Subject to the provisions of Article 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989, dated December 30, 1989, relating to the issuance of the Income Tax Code for individuals and corporate tax, natural persons acquiring businesses under paragraph II of Article 11 bis of this Code shall benefit from the deduction of reinvested income or profits, up to the limit of income or profits subject to income tax for the year of acquisition. (Amended by Article 15 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

The deduction is granted based on a decision issued by the Minister of Finance or by a person authorized by the Minister of Finance.

Benefitting from the deduction requires:

- That the business being transferred is active in sectors specified in Chapter IV of this Code: (Amended by Article 15-8 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).
- That the acquiring business continues to operate the acquired business for a period of not less than three years starting from January 1st of the year following the year of acquisition.
- Maintaining accounting records compliant with the accounting legislation for businesses, for natural persons realizing income in the category of industrial and commercial profits or in the category of non-commercial professional profits.
- That the assets of the acquired business are recorded in the balance sheet of the year concerned by the deduction for the acquiring business. The annual tax return for the year of the deduction must be accompanied by a list of the acquired elements under paragraph II of the aforementioned Article 11 bis, including in particular the acquisition value and a copy of the aforementioned decision of the Minister of Finance. (Amended by Article 15 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

The beneficiary of the deduction shall be required to pay the unpaid tax on the reinvested amounts, plus any applicable late payment penalties according to the legislation in force, if they cease operating the acquired business before the expiry of the three years following the year of the deduction.  These provisions shall not apply in the event of cessation of operation for reasons beyond their control, determined by order.

II. (Repealed by Article 15-6 of Law No. 8 of 2017, dated February 14, 2017).

The tax exemption provided for in this Article shall not be granted for subscription operations intended for the acquisition of land. (Added by Article 1373 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017).
