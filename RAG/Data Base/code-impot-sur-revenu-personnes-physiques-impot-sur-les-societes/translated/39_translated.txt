I. Income tax is levied on the total income or income elements based on the gross amount of net income determined according to the provisions stipulated in Section II of this chapter, after deducting the following expenses, if not already considered in the assessment of any income category:

1. Mandatory and gratuitous installments of revenue.

2. Installments or subscription fees paid by the subscriber or member under life insurance contracts or mutual life insurance contracts, or capital formation contracts, or mutual capital formation contracts, up to a limit of 10,000 dinars annually, provided that one of the following guarantees is available in these contracts:

    - Guarantee of capital or income for the insured, his/her spouse, ascendants, or descendants for an effective period of not less than 8 years.

    - Guarantee of unit accounts for the benefit of the insured, his/her spouse, ascendants, or descendants, payable after a minimum period of not less than 8 years.

    - Guarantee of capital or income upon death for the benefit of the spouse, ascendants, or descendants.

These provisions apply to life insurance premiums or subscription fees paid by a member under group insurance contracts or group mutual insurance contracts with an effective membership period of not less than 8 years, provided that his/her contribution or participation in these contracts is not less than a minimum contribution, the rate of which is determined by a decision of the Minister of Finance.

If the subscriber or member of individual contracts or the member of group contracts purchases an insurance contract or a mutual insurance contract before the expiry of the eight-year period mentioned above, the income tax already levied on the total income or income elements that were not paid in accordance with the provisions of this code will be added to the penalties incurred in accordance with the current legislation.  Late payment penalties are not incurred if the subscriber or member of individual contracts or the member of group contracts purchases an insurance contract or a mutual insurance contract following the occurrence of unforeseen events as defined by the current legislation, or after a savings period of at least five years (amended by Article 23-2 of Law No. 70 of 2007 dated December 27, 2007, and by Articles 24-2 and 3 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013, and by Article 27-3 of Law No. 66 of 2017 dated December 18, 2017, and by Article 12 of Law No. 78 of 2019 dated December 23, 2019).

The purchaser must present to the insurance company or mutual insurance company a certificate received from the competent tax authorities proving that he/she has settled his/her tax situation concerning the premiums or subscription fees that benefited from the deduction.  In the absence of this, the insurance company or mutual insurance company is jointly and severally liable with the subscriber or member of individual contracts or the member of group contracts for the payment of the amounts due (added by Article 61 of Law No. 90 of 2004 dated December 31, 2004, and amended by Article 24-2 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013, and by Article 12 of Law No. 78 of 2019 dated December 23, 2019).

3. (Added by Article 46 of Law No. 111 of 1998 dated December 28, 1998) Installments paid for the repayment of the principal and interest of university loans.

4. Surpluses and commissions paid in connection with loans related to the acquisition or construction of a single dwelling, the cost of which does not exceed 200,000 dinars, excluding VAT. These provisions apply to acquisitions or constructions under Murabaha sale contracts. These provisions apply to amounts whose due date for collection is from January 1, 2016.  These provisions do not apply to individuals who own a dwelling on the date of acquisition or construction of a dwelling under the provisions of this article (added by Article 39-1 of Law No. 71 of 2009 dated December 21, 2009, and amended by Article 26-1 of Law No. 30 of 2015 dated August 18, 2015).

5. Contributions paid by self-employed workers enrolled in a legal social security scheme. (Added by Article 44 of Law No. 71 of 2009 dated December 23, 2009).

(Amended by Article 30 of Law No. 98 of 1991 dated December 31, 1991, and by Article 13 of Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016).

VIII. Subject to the provisions of Article 12 bis of Law No. 114 of 1989 dated December 30, 1989, relating to the issuance of the Code of Income Tax on Individuals and Corporate Tax, the amounts deposited in accounts called "equity savings accounts" opened with a lending institution having the status of a bank or with a stockbroker for the purpose of subscribing to or acquiring shares listed on the stock exchange and negotiable treasury bills, or shares of variable capital investment companies or units of common funds for investment in securities stipulated in Article 10 of the Code of Collective Investment Institutions issued by Law No. 83 of 2001 dated July 24, 2001, which use their capital or assets to acquire shares listed on the stock exchange and negotiable treasury bills, are deducted from the taxable income. The deduction is limited to 50,000 dinars per year. (Amended by Article 30 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001, and by Article 45 of Law No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003, and by Article 4 of Decree No. 28 of 2011 dated April 18, 2011, and by Article 13 of Decree No. 100 of 2011 dated October 21, 2011).

To benefit from this deduction:

- (Repealed by Article 46 of Law No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003).

- The annual tax return must be accompanied by a certificate of deposit issued by the institution with which the equity savings account is opened.

- The amounts deposited in the aforementioned accounts must not be withdrawn for a period of not less than five years from January 1 of the year following the year of deposit.

Any withdrawal made before the expiry of the aforementioned period will result in the payment of the due tax, to which penalties calculated according to the current tax legislation will be added. Late payment penalties are not incurred if the deposited amounts are withdrawn after the end of the third year following the year of deposit, or if the withdrawal is due to unforeseen events.

A list of unforeseen events shall be determined by a joint decision of the Minister of Finance, the Minister of Public Health, and the Minister of Social Affairs (added by Article 31 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001).

In this case, the limitation periods provided for in Article 72 of this code shall apply from January 1 of the year following the year in which the deposited amounts were withdrawn.

The income tax that was subject to exemption for the institution with which the account was opened, in the event that the deposited amounts were not used for the purposes for which they were deposited and according to the conditions relating to the management of the aforementioned accounts, will be added to the penalties calculated according to the current tax legislation.


In this case, the limitation periods provided for in Article 72 of this code shall apply from January 1 of the year following the year in which the deposited amounts were not used for the purposes for which they were deposited, or in which the conditions relating to the management of the aforementioned accounts were violated.

The conditions for opening the aforementioned accounts, the manner of managing them, and the manner of using the amounts and securities deposited therein shall be determined by order. (Added by Article 4 of Law No. 92 of 1999 dated August 17, 1999, relating to provisions aimed at boosting the financial market).
