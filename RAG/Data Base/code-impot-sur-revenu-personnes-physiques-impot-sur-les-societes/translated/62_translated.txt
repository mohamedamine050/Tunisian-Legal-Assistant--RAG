I. Legal entities specified in Articles 4 and 45 of this Code, and natural persons subject to income tax on industrial and commercial profits or profits from non-commercial professions, and any natural person choosing to be subject to income tax under the actual system by keeping accounts in accordance with the accounting legislation for institutions, are required to do so. (Amended by Article 75, Law No. 88 of 1997, dated December 29, 1997, and by Article 63, Law No. 90 of 2004, dated December 31, 2004).


II. Persons who keep their accounts using computers must:

- Deposit a copy of the initial or amended program recorded on magnetic tapes with the performance monitoring office, in exchange for a receipt;

- Inform the aforementioned office of the type of equipment used, its location, and any changes that may occur to this data. (Amended by Article 75, Law No. 88 of 1997, dated December 29, 1997).


III. Exempt from keeping the documents referred to in paragraphs I and II of this Article are:

1. Natural persons referred to in Article 44 bis of this Code. (Amended by Article 37-3, Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

2. Natural persons benefiting from the estimated system for determining income under the categories of income referred to in Articles 21 and 27 of this Code.

However, they are required to keep a numbered and indexed register, stamped by the monitoring authorities, in which receipts and expenses are recorded daily on the basis of supporting documents. (Amended by Article 34, Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).

3. Natural persons benefiting from the estimated system for determining income under the categories of income referred to in Article 23 of this Code.

4. (Added by Article 70, Law No. 127 of 1994, dated December 26, 1994, amended by Article 64, Law No. 111, dated December 28, 1998, and repealed by Article 39-1, Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010).


III bis (Added by Article 39-1, Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010, and supplemented by Articles 8-1 and 2, Law No. 54 of 2014, dated August 19, 2014). Regardless of the provisions of this Article, individual entities that generate income in the category of industrial and commercial profits and profits from non-commercial professions may keep simplified accounting in accordance with the accounting legislation for institutions, provided that their annual turnover does not exceed:

- 300,000 dinars for purchasing for resale activities, transformation activities, and on-site consumption activities;

- 150,000 dinars for service activities and non-commercial professions.


III ter (Added by Article 18-1, Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015). Regardless of the provisions of this Article, natural persons specified in Article 44 bis of this Code who choose to be included in the actual system or who are included in the actual system, and whose annual turnover does not exceed 150,000 dinars, may choose to determine their net profit based on simplified accounting relying on keeping:

- A numbered and indexed register, stamped by the competent tax authorities, in which gross receipts and expenses are recorded daily on the basis of supporting documents;

- An inventory register, numbered and indexed by the competent tax authorities, in which fixed assets and inventories are recorded annually.


IV. Commercial ledgers or other accounting documents, and in general all documents required to be kept or submitted in accordance with the provisions of this Code, must be retained for ten years.
