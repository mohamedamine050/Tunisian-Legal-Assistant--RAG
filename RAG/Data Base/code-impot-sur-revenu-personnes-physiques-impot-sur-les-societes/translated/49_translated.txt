1. The corporate tax rate applicable to profit exceeding the company's liabilities is determined by including the company's capital in its outstanding debts at the end of the financial year subject to tax, deducting anything less than one dinar by 25%.

This rate is reduced to 20% for profits derived from the original activity, as well as the exceptional profits stipulated in paragraph 1 bis of Article 11 of this Code, under the same conditions, for companies whose annual turnover does not exceed:

● 1 million dinars for conversion activities and purchasing activities for resale,

● 500,000 dinars for service activities and non-commercial professions.

The aforementioned turnover limits are calculated excluding payments.

A 25% rate also applies to the added value stipulated in paragraph II of Article 45 of this Code. However, those concerned may choose to pay corporate tax on the aforementioned added value at a rate of 15% of the sale price.

However, the corporate tax rate is set for profits derived from the original activity, as well as the exceptional profits stipulated in paragraph 1 bis of Article 11 of this Code, under the same conditions, at:

1. 10%, for:

- Entities engaged in traditional craft activities, agricultural activities, fishing, or the equipping of fishing vessels;

- Profits derived from investments in the agricultural and fishing sectors after the fulfillment of the total exemption period stipulated in Article 65 of this Code;

- Profits derived from investments made in regional development zones after the fulfillment of the total exemption period stipulated in Article 63 of this Code;

- Profits derived from investments in support activities and pollution control stipulated in Article 70 of this Code;

- Purchasing centers for retail businesses organized as service cooperatives subject to the general law on cooperatives;

- Service cooperatives formed between producers for the purpose of selling their products wholesale;

- Consumption cooperatives subject to the general law on cooperatives;

- Profits generated within the framework of projects with a commercial or industrial nature, benefiting from the Youth Employment Program or the National Fund for the Promotion of Traditional Industries and Small Businesses.

2. 13.5%, for:

- Profits derived from the electronics, electrical, and mechanical industries;

- Profits derived from the manufacture of cars, aircraft, ships, trains, and their components;

- Profits derived from cable manufacturing;

- Profits derived from the manufacture of pharmaceuticals, medical equipment, and devices;

- Profits derived from the textile, clothing, leather, and footwear industries;

- Profits derived from the food industry;

- Profits generated by call centers;

- Profits derived from providing information technology renewal services, software development, and data processing;

- Profits generated by international trade companies operating in accordance with the legislation concerning them;

- Profits derived from packaging and wrapping materials;

- Profits derived from providing integrated logistics services. The list of aforementioned services shall be determined by a government order;

- Profits derived from the manufacture of plastic products;

- "Profits derived from providing high value-added consulting and study services, the value and conditions of which are determined by a government order." (Added by Article 26, Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019)


3. 35%, for:

- Banks and financial institutions stipulated in Law No. 48 of 2016, dated July 11, 2016, concerning banks and financial institutions, and non-resident financial institutions operating under the Code of Financial Services for Non-Residents;

- Investment companies stipulated in Law No. 92 of 1988, dated August 2, 1988, as amended and supplemented by subsequent texts, particularly Law No. 104 of 2005, dated December 19, 2005;

- "Insurance companies, including cooperative insurance and mutual insurance funds, stipulated in the Insurance Code as amended and supplemented by subsequent texts, particularly Law No. 47 of 2014, dated July 24, 2014"; (Amended by Article 11-3, Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019)

- Debt collection companies stipulated in Law No. 4 of 1998, dated February 2, 1998, concerning debt collection companies, as amended and supplemented by Law No. 42 of 2003, dated June 9, 2003;

- Communication network operators stipulated in the Communications Code issued by Law No. 1 of 2001, dated January 15, 2001, as amended and supplemented by Law No. 46 of 2002, dated May 7, 2002;

- "Profits derived from providing services stipulated in Article 1.130 of the Hydrocarbons Code issued by Law No. 93 of 1999, dated August 17, 1999, as amended and supplemented by subsequent texts, and from providing hydrocarbon transport services to companies operating under the legislation concerning hydrocarbons"; (Amended by Article 25, Law No. 78 of 2019, dated December 23, 2019)

- Entities operating in the hydrocarbon production and transport sector subject to a tax regime under special agreements, and entities transporting oil products via pipelines;

- Entities operating in the refining and wholesale sale of oil products stipulated in Law No. 45 of 1991, dated July 1, 1991, concerning oil products;

- Large commercial areas stipulated in the Code of Land Planning and Urban Development, as amended and supplemented by subsequent texts, particularly Law No. 78 of 2003, dated December 29, 2003;

- Car sales agents;

- Operators under the original designation of a foreign trade name or trademark stipulated in Law No. 69 of 2009, dated August 12, 2009, concerning distribution trade, excluding entities with an integration rate equal to or exceeding 30%. (Provisions of paragraph I were repealed and replaced by Article 14, Law No. 56 of 2018, dated December 27, 2018)


II. The annual tax cannot be less than a minimum tax equal to:

- 0.2% of the gross turnover with a minimum of 500 dinars, payable even if no turnover is achieved, for companies not subject to corporate tax at a rate of 10% or 13.5%;

- 0.1% of the turnover for which the resulting profits are subject to corporate tax at a rate of 10%, 13.5%, or the turnover achieved from the sale of products or services subject to an administrative price approval system, whose gross profit margin does not exceed 6% according to the legislation and regulations in force, with a minimum of 300 dinars, payable even if no turnover is achieved. (The provisions of the first paragraph of paragraph II were amended by Article 16-3, Law No. 56 of 2018, dated December 27, 2018)

The minimum tax shall not apply to new entities during the project implementation period, which in all cases does not exceed three years from the date of filing the declaration of existence stipulated in Article 56 of this Code.

The minimum tax applies to entities that have ceased operations and have not filed the declaration stipulated in paragraph 1 of Article 58 and paragraph IV of Article 49 decimo of this Code.

The minimum tax stipulated in this paragraph is increased by 50% if paid after one month from the expiry of the legal deadlines.

The provisions of this paragraph do not apply to entities benefiting from the total exemption of their profits or income from exploitation during the period stipulated for them by the legislation in force. (Amended by Laws No. 88 of 1997, No. 106 of 2005, No. 80 of 2006, No. 58 of 2010, and by Article 48-2, Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013)
