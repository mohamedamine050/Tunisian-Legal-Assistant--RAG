Contrary to the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 12 of this Code, the following shall not be deductible for the purpose of profit determination:

1. Gifts of all kinds and hospitality expenses, including banquet and party expenses, exceeding one percent (1%) of the company's gross turnover, provided that the deduction does not exceed a maximum amount of twenty thousand dinars (20,000 TND) in each fiscal year.

2. Income tax, taxes levied on total income or income components, or corporate tax, including deductions made by the companies on its behalf, and those borne by the companies instead of actual claimants, as well as fees for trips abroad. (Amended by Article 51 of Law No. 127 of 1994 dated December 26, 1994, Article 51-1 of Law No. 101 of 2002 dated December 17, 2002, Article 41-11 of Law No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010, and Article 39 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013).

3. Commissions, brokerage fees, trade or other discounts, and efficiency bonuses provided for in sub-paragraph (a) of paragraph 1 of Article 52 of this Code,  leases provided for in paragraph II bis of Article 53 of this Code, and fees, rewards, and grants provided for in paragraph 3 of Article 30 of this Code, which are borne by the company and not declared in accordance with the conditions of paragraph 3 of Article 55 of this Code. (Amended by Article 92 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001, Article 29-4 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, and Article 30-5 of Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016).

4. All expenses related to secondary residences, aircraft, and pleasure boats referred to in paragraph 4 of Article 15 of this Code.

5. Rent, maintenance expenses, supplies, fuel, and mileage allowances paid for tourist cars exceeding 9 fiscal horsepower, except those whose original purpose is for business use. (Amended by Article 52 of Law No. 109 dated December 25, 1995).

6.  Excess payments made to the operator or partners of partnerships or joint ventures in return for amounts they deposit into the company's fund in addition to their capital contributions.

7. The operator's or nominal partner's salary.

8. Settlements, fines, seizures, and penalties of any kind incurred by those violating the legal provisions relating to prices, trade, distribution of various goods, or exchange control, tax or contribution assessment, or duties; and in general, any offense described as: felonies, meaning crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years; misdemeanors, meaning crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or a fine exceeding sixty dinars; and infractions, meaning crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and current legislation).

9. Grants, subsidies, and sponsorships not declared in accordance with the provisions of paragraph II of Article 59 of this Code. (Added by Article 48 of Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997 and amended by Article 60-2 of Law No. 66 of 2017 dated December 18, 2017).

10. The installment of rental aid due on users of fixed assets subject to financial lease agreements or lease agreements for the purpose of repaying the cost of acquiring the aforementioned assets by lending institutions engaged in financial leasing or leasing activities. (Added by Article 43 of Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006 and amended by Articles 2-36 and 3 of Law No. 7 of 2011 dated December 30, 2011).

11. Expenses equal to or exceeding 20,000 dinars, excluding VAT, paid in cash. (Added by Article 34-1 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013).

12. Expenses related to amounts paid to individuals residing or domiciled in a country or territory with a preferential tax regime. (Added by Article 1-34 of Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016 and amended by Article 35-1 of Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018).

Individuals shall be considered residents or domiciled in a country or territory with a preferential tax regime if the tax levied on them in that country or territory is less than 50% of the income tax, taxes levied on total income or income components, or corporate tax levied in Tunisia for the same activity. A list of countries and territories with preferential tax regimes shall be determined by a decision of the Minister of Finance. (The second paragraph was added by Article 3-2 of Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018).
