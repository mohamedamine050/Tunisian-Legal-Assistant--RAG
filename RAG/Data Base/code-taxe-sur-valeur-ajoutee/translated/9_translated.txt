## English Translation of the Arabic Legal Text:

**I**

1) From the Value Added Tax (VAT) levied on taxable operations, the taxpayer shall deduct the amount of VAT actually borne by the elements of the price of a taxable operation, "as well as the VAT that has been deducted from the supplier in accordance with Article 19bis of this Code" (added by Article 38 of Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997).

Taxpayers shall deduct, in total, from the amount of VAT due in accordance with Articles 1 and 2 above, the amount of VAT actually borne on their local purchases from taxpayers or their own deliveries, imports they have carried out, and services necessary for their operational requirements.

If the VAT due for a given month does not allow for the full deduction of the deductible VAT, the remaining amount shall be carried forward to subsequent months.

If the contract between two companies includes the execution of a service and the equipment and materials required for it, and if the project owner imports or purchases locally, in their own name, all or part of the equipment and materials stipulated in the contract, the company executing the project shall be entitled to deduct the VAT paid by the project owner.

If the aforementioned equipment and materials benefit from the suspension of VAT application, their value shall be transferred to the project owner without VAT being calculated.

1bis) (Added by Article 50 of Law No. 70 of 2007 dated December 27, 2007; amended by Article 37 of Law No. 7 of 2011 dated December 31, 2011, and amended by Article 16 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015). From the VAT levied on taxable operations, the taxpayer shall deduct the VAT levied on purchases of equipment, machinery, and real estate intended for use under financial lease agreements and lease agreements concluded by lending institutions and by microfinance institutions as stipulated by Decree No. 117 of 2011 dated November 5, 2011, concerning the regulation of microfinance institution activities, "regardless of the accounting recording of these purchases."

1ter) (Added by Article 33 of Law No. 27 of 2011 dated December 31, 2011, and amended by Articles 16-19 and 16-10, and by Article 222 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015). With due regard to the current legislation, taxpayers subject to VAT shall deduct the VAT relating to their necessary purchases for their activities from lending institutions and from microfinance institutions as stipulated by Decree No. 117 of 2011 dated November 5, 2011, concerning the regulation of microfinance institution activities, under sale and repurchase, sale and deferred payment, or production contract agreements.

To benefit from the right to deduct, the invoice, fee note, or contract shall specify the amount of VAT borne by the lending institution and the microfinance institution as stipulated by Decree No. 117 of 2011 dated November 5, 2011, concerning the regulation of microfinance institution activities, for their purchases made under these agreements.

2) To benefit from the deduction provided for above, taxpayers must:

a) Possess invoices and fee notes for their local purchases of goods and services issued in accordance with the conditions set out in Article 18 hereafter, or certificates for VAT deduction from the supplier. (Amended by Article 39 of Law No. 88 of 1997 dated December 29, 1997, and by Article 22-2 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015)

"However, for deductions from the supplier falling within the scope of application of the electronic platform provided for in Article 55 of the Code of Income Tax for Individuals and Corporate Tax, the deduction of VAT deducted from the supplier shall be limited to the amounts of VAT listed on this platform, taking into account the scope of application and deadlines set out in the decision referred to in Article 55 mentioned above."

(Added by Article 41-3 of Decree No. 21 of 2021 dated December 28, 2021).

b) Possess VAT deduction receipts from the Customs Administration for their imports.

c) Keep a special register numbered and signed by the VAT control centers or offices to which their activity is subject, containing an account of their local purchases from taxpayers, as well as imports and services subject to VAT, if their accounts are not kept in accordance with the provisions of Article 18 hereafter. The purchase account must be closed monthly and must contain the type and value of purchases, imports, and services, indicating the amount of VAT paid.

They must also record in this register, clearly and without any gaps or strikethroughs, all deliveries made or services provided, regardless of the title, as well as receipts received, with an indication of the amount of VAT related to them.

However, the foregoing provisions do not apply to assets subject to depreciation, which remain subject to the provisions of the accounting legislation for companies (Amended by Article 84 of Law No. 101 of 2002 dated December 17, 2002).

d) Record in their accounting system the assets subject to depreciation at their purchase price or cost price minus the VAT deducted in accordance with the aforementioned conditions, adjusting it if necessary according to the provisions set out in paragraph III below.

The VAT included in purchase invoices and fee notes conforming to the provisions of Article 18 of this Code and approved by the tax authorities shall be deducted to adjust the transaction number without resorting to accounting.

**II**

-1) For taxpayers who do not pay VAT on all their transactions, the amount of VAT that can be deducted shall be calculated based on a pro rata share resulting from the ratio between the following elements carried out during the previous year:

- Receipts subject to VAT, plus receipts arising from exports of goods or services subject to VAT or deliveries made under the aforementioned deferred VAT application system, and receipts related to international air transport operations, taking into account the VAT due or not yet required to be paid, on the one hand. (Amended by Article 40 of Law No. 123 of 2001 dated December 28, 2001, and by Article 19 of Law No. 70 of 2007 dated December 27, 2007).

- The amounts referred to in the subparagraph above, plus receipts arising from exempt or excluded VAT operations, on the other hand.

2) For newly partially subject taxpayers, the calculation referred to above shall be adjusted based on the estimated receipts for the first year of their activity.

**III**

1-) At the end of each calendar year, persons partially subject to VAT shall determine the pro rata share of the deduction referred to above, taking into account the elements carried out during the same calendar year.

2) With regard to assets subject to depreciation, an adjustment must be made if the change in the pro rata share of the deduction during the aforementioned year exceeds 5%, either up or down, in relation to the deduction made. The additional deduction or payment of VAT resulting from this change in the pro rata share shall be made in January of the year following the year in which this deduction occurred.

**IV**

-1) In the event of an unjustified loss of assets or goods, taxpayers subject to VAT shall make the adjustment provided for partially subject taxpayers in accordance with the conditions referred to in paragraph III above.

1bis) a) Taxpayers subject to VAT shall not be required to pay the VAT borne by in-kind donations they make to the Tunisian Solidarity Union, up to 1% of their annual turnover, excluding VAT.

The value of in-kind donations shall be assessed on the basis of the cost price excluding VAT at the level of the donor company.

b) However, taxpayers subject to VAT shall pay the VAT borne by in-kind donations made to the Tunisian Solidarity Union exceeding the limit mentioned above, or to other associations. In these cases, VAT shall be calculated based on the cost price excluding VAT and applying the VAT rate applicable to the product donated. VAT shall be paid in the following deadlines:

- Within January of the year following the year of donation for the VAT on in-kind donations made to the Tunisian Solidarity Union exceeding the limit mentioned above.

- Within the month following the month in which the donation was made for in-kind donations made to other associations.

c) To benefit from the provisions of subparagraph 1bis of paragraph IV of this Article, taxpayers subject to VAT who make in-kind donations must provide the competent VAT control office, within the month following the month in which the in-kind donations were made, with a list containing in particular:

- The names, addresses, and tax identification numbers of the beneficiaries of the in-kind donations.

- The date on which the in-kind donations were made.

- A list of the in-kind donations, the cost price, and the VAT rate applicable to the donated product. (Added by Article 57 of Law No. 80 of 2003 dated December 29, 2003).
