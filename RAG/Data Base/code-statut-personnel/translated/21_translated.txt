(Amended by Decree No. 1 of 1964 dated February 20, 1964, ratified by Law No. 1 of 1964 dated April 21, 1964.) - A void marriage is one that is contracted subject to a condition that is incompatible with the essence of the contract, or that is contracted without regard for the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 3, the first paragraph of Article 5, and Articles 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, and 20 of this Code. If criminal proceedings are initiated pursuant to the provisions of Article 18 above, a single judgment shall be rendered on both the crime and the nullity of the marriage. The spouses who resume or continue cohabitation despite the declaration of the nullity of their marriage shall be punished with imprisonment for a term of six months. Article 53 of the Penal Code shall not apply to the offenses provided for in this Article.
