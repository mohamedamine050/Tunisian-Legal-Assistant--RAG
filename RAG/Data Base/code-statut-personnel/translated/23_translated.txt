Neither of the spouses has the right to request the termination of the joint ownership. If one of the spouses files a lawsuit for the division of the joint property, the creditors have the right to intervene in that lawsuit to protect their rights. They may also challenge the judgment issued in the lawsuit, subject to the conditions and procedures stipulated in the Code of Civil Procedure.

The spouses may enter into agreements regarding the liquidation of the joint property during the proceedings of the lawsuit. Such agreements are subject to the court's approval. The creditors have the right to request modifications to the agreements, taking into account their rights.
