A minor who has not reached the age of thirteen is considered incapable of discernment, and all of their acts are void. A minor who has surpassed the age of thirteen is considered capable of discernment, and their acts are valid if they are purely beneficial and void if they are harmful. 

[1] Harm is an injury inflicted in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. [2] Harm includes loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or injury to physical well-being, reputation, or similar interests. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, harm refers to the actual loss suffered by the plaintiff and the necessary expenses they have incurred or will be compelled to incur to repair the consequences of the harmful act, as well as the benefits they have been deprived of within the ordinary scope of the consequences of this act. 

Purely beneficial acts require the authorization of the guardian for their validity, except in the two aforementioned cases.
