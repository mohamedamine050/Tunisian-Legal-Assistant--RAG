Any military personnel who mutinies against the armed forces and those in authority shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of one to six months if the mutiny occurs without weapons, and by imprisonment for a term of six months to two years if the mutiny occurs while the perpetrator is armed. If the mutiny occurs by at least four armed military personnel, the punishment shall be imprisonment for a term of two to five years. 

The maximum penalty shall always be imposed on instigators and leaders of the mutiny, and on the highest-ranking military personnel involved. 

A military personnel who is on leave or vacation and is found wearing their military uniform in a gathering that is likely to disrupt public security, and who remains in such a gathering despite orders from those in authority or public security forces, shall be punished with the penalties prescribed in the first paragraph of this article.
