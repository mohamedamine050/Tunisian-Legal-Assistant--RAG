**(Amended by Section 1 of Decree No. 69 of 2011 dated July 29, 2011.)** - The Public Prosecutor, the entity responsible for defending the interests of society and enforcing the law before the Military Courts, initiates criminal proceedings. 

[1] A lawsuit is a claim by one party against another in the presence of a judge; the claimant is referred to as the plaintiff and the party against whom the claim is made as the defendant. [2] A lawsuit is the right of any individual with a claim to submit it to the court for judgment. It also represents the defendant's right to present defenses or arguments aimed at refuting that claim. 

The Public Prosecutor exercises its functions and seeks the application of the law in accordance with the rules and procedures stipulated in the Code of Criminal Procedure. The Public Prosecutor shall immediately notify the Director General of Military Justice, through the Attorney General of the Republic, in case of apprehension of a suspect and shall conduct investigations in accordance with the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
