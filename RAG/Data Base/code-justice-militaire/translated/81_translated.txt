(Amended by Article 8 of Law No. 23 of 1989 dated February 27, 1989) - Any person who incites, by any means whatsoever, a group of military personnel exceeding three in number to disobey orders of a superior officer or to resist or assault such superior officer shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of six years, if the incitement does not lead to any result. The instigator shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of not less than six years if the incitement results in harm to military services. The instigator to disobedience during war or a state of siege shall be punished by death. The punishment shall be reduced to half if the instigator is a civilian, and the death penalty shall be replaced by imprisonment for fifteen years.
