**As amended by Article 8 of Law No. 23 of 1989 dated February 27, 1989.** - A military person who intentionally commits violence or threatens violence against their superior officer or someone of higher rank during service or in connection therewith shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of six years. The same penalty shall be imposed if the act is committed against military personnel assigned to guard the superior officer or the higher-ranking person. The perpetrator shall, in addition, be sentenced to dismissal from service if he is an officer, in cases where dismissal from military service is not the inevitable consequence of the imposed penalty.  A military person who commits violence while armed shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of five years. A military person who commits acts of violence against a superior officer outside of service or in connection with service shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of three months to two years. The perpetrator shall be punished by imprisonment for a term of six months to three years if he is an officer.
