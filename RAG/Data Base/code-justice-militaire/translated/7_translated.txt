**(Amended by the first section of Decree No. 69 of 2011 dated July 29, 2011).** - Public action may be brought against personal liability and the exercise of personal right before the Military Court (*) or the Investigating Judges, in accordance with the rules and procedures stipulated in the Code of Criminal Procedure.

[1] A request by one party for another to appear before a judge; the requester is called the plaintiff and the party requested is called the defendant. [2] Public action is the right of every claimant to bring their claim before the court to obtain a judgment on the merits of the claim. In relation to the opposing party, it is the right to provide reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting that claim.
