Subject to the provisions of Articles 21, 23, 24, and 26 of this Code, the rectification of omissions, errors, and concealments found in the basis of the assessment, its application rate, or its calculation, concerning the declared tax payment, is as follows:

1. Until the end of the fourth year following the year in which the profit was realised; the excess amount owed by the company over what is due, by including the company's capital in the debts incurred at the end of the financial year, or income, or turnover, or receipt or disbursement of funds, or other operations requiring tax payment. However, for institutions subject to tax under the actual system whose balance sheet closing date does not coincide with the end of the calendar year, the right to rectify the tax payment shall be exercised until the end of the fourth year following the year in which the balance sheet was closed.

Article 17 of Law No. 54 of 2014, dated August 19, 2014, relating to the supplementary budget law for 2014, stipulates the following: The rectification of omissions and concealments found in the basis of the assessment, its application rate, or its calculation, shall be carried out for persons previously convicted by final judgments in cases related to smuggling or parallel trade until the end of the fifteenth year following the year in which the income, receipt or disbursement of funds, or other operations requiring tax payment were realised.  The tax due for a specific financial year is payable until the end of the fourth year following the year in which the balance sheet was closed.  The following are considered operations requiring tax payment within the meaning of this Article:

Expiry of the deadline for fulfilling the conditions required to benefit from tax privileges or differential systems provided for in current legislation.

Breach of obligations required to benefit from tax privileges or differential systems provided for in current legislation. This paragraph was added by Article 45 of Law No. 58 of 2010, dated December 17, 2010, relating to the Finance Law for 2011.


2. Within four years from the date of registration of the contract or declaration concerning registration fees. However, in the event of the conclusion of a contract or the issuance of a judgment within the two years following the date of death, containing a value for real estate exceeding the value listed in the estate declaration, the limitation period shall begin from the date of registration of the contract or judgment. The right of the tax authorities to impose administrative penalties provided for in Articles 84 bis to 85 of the Tax Code shall lapse upon the expiry of the fourth year following the year in which the infringement giving rise to the application of the penalty was committed. This paragraph was added by Article 32 of Law No. 59 of 2014, dated December 26, 2014, relating to the Finance Law for 2015.
