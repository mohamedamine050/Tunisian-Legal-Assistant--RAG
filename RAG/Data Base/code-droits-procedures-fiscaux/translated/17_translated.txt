The Central Bank of Tunisia, banks, and financial institutions, including non-resident banks and financial institutions, companies managing securities portfolios for third parties, investment companies, companies managing funds as provided for by current legislation, stockbrokers, the Central Depository and Clearing and Settlement Company, and the National Post Office, must provide tax authorities, whenever requested in writing, within the framework of a preliminary tax audit or an in-depth tax audit, with the account numbers opened in their name and on behalf of the taxpayer or on behalf of third parties, or opened by third parties on behalf of the taxpayer, during the period not covered by the statute of limitations.  The statute of limitations is the loss of the right to pursue a claim due to non-compliance with deadlines. They must also provide the identity of the account holders, as well as the opening dates of these accounts if opened during the aforementioned period, and their closing dates if closed during the same period, within a maximum period of twenty days from the date of the request.  Insurance companies, including non-resident insurance companies, must also provide tax authorities, whenever requested in writing, with all data relating to the subscription dates of capital formation contracts and life insurance contracts subscribed with them, their numbers, and their maturity dates, within a maximum period of twenty days from the date of the request. Without prejudice to the provisions of the first paragraph of this section, the Central Bank of Tunisia, banks, and the National Post Office must declare to the tax authorities, within the first fifteen days of each calendar quarter, the account numbers they opened or closed during the preceding quarter and the identity of their holders, according to a model prepared by the administration (this paragraph was added by Section 53 of Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018, relating to the Finance Act for 2019).  The institutions referred to in the first and second paragraphs of this section must also comply with a written request from the Director General of Taxes, the Head of the National Audit and Tax Research Unit, the Head of the Research and Tax Evasion Control Unit, the Director of Large Institutions, the Director of Medium-Sized Institutions, or the Head of the Regional Tax Control Center, within a maximum period of twenty days from the date of notification, providing copies of account statements and amounts saved, relating to capital formation contracts or life insurance contracts mentioned above. In the event that these are not provided by the taxpayer to the tax authorities within twenty days of written notification, in accordance with the provisions of Section 10 of this code, or if provided incompletely, the tax authorities may obtain them. This paragraph was amended by Section 66 of Law No. 66 of 2017 dated December 18, 2017, relating to the Finance Act for 2018.

Electronic communication may be used to request and obtain documents and information. (This section was repealed and replaced by Section 1 of Law No. 1 of 2002 dated January 8, 2002, relating to the simplification of tax procedures; it was then repealed and replaced by Section 12 of Law No. 54 of 2014 dated August 19, 2014, relating to the Supplementary Finance Act for 2014; and then repealed and replaced by the provisions of Section 37 of Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016, relating to the Finance Act for 2017).
