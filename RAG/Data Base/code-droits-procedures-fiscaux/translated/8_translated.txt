Taxpayers must, upon each request, present to the authorized tax officials their receipts, documents, invoices, and fee statements, or documents and contracts that serve as substitutes, relating to the payment of due or established taxes.

Assets collected by the State in the form of taxes; a tax is a monetary sum paid by individuals and companies proportionally to their income or profits.

For this purpose, these officials are authorized to conduct unannounced visits to professional premises, shops, and their warehouses, and generally, all places dedicated to activities or operations subject to tax, and to conduct physical inspections of elements related to the commercial, industrial, or professional activity, or accounting books and documents, invoices, fee statements, or documents and contracts that serve as substitutes.  Physical inspections are conducted based on a special assignment for this purpose; a copy of which is delivered directly to the taxpayer or their representative at the beginning of the visit, against a delivery receipt. This paragraph was amended by Articles 22 and 53 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, relating to the Finance Law for 2016.

These inspections are not considered a de facto commencement of the in-depth review of the tax situation, as stipulated in Article 38 of this Code.  Furthermore, when there is evidence of an undeclared activity subject to tax or the commission of tax fraud, these officials are authorized to conduct visits and inspections of suspected premises to examine the violations committed and discover the evidence thereof, in accordance with the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure. Tax officials may seize any documents or objects proving an undeclared activity subject to tax or giving rise to suspicion of a tax offense.

Crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years are classified as felonies within the meaning of this law.  Crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, are classified as misdemeanors.  Crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine of sixty dinars are classified as infractions.  (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure).

Upon conducting any visit within the meaning of this article, a report is drawn up in accordance with the provisions of Articles 71 and 72 of this Code, detailing the procedure and the physical inspections carried out, and providing a detailed description of the seized items. A copy of the report is delivered to the taxpayer or their representative against a delivery receipt. Within the framework of the visits, inspections, and physical inspections stipulated in this article, these officials may also take copies of documents that can be used to monitor and review the taxpayer's tax situation. This paragraph was amended by Article 33 of Law No. 78 of 2019 dated December 23, 2019, relating to the Finance Law for 2020.
