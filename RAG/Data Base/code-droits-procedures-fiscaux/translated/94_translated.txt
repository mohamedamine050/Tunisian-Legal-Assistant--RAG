Imprisonment for a period ranging from sixteen days to three years and a fine ranging from 1000 Dinars to 50,000 Dinars shall be imposed:

- On any person required to issue invoices or fee notes pursuant to tax legislation who has failed to issue invoices or fee notes for sales or services rendered, or who has issued invoices or fee notes containing understated amounts.  In this case, the same penalty shall apply.  The penalty is a sanction and remedy imposed in the name of society upon a person criminally liable for a crime committed, based on a court judgment.  The same penalty shall apply to the buyer if legally obligated to issue invoices or fee notes for their sales or services. (This provision was amended by Article 22 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015, concerning the Finance Law for 2016).

- On any person who issued or used invoices or fee notes containing inflated amounts in the cases stipulated in Article 48, seventhly, of the Tax Code on Income of Natural Persons and Corporate Tax. (This provision was added by Article 38 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, concerning the Finance Law for 2014, and amended by Article 22 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015, concerning the Finance Law for 2016).

- On any person who issued or used invoices or fee notes for fictitious sales or services rendered with the intent to evade payment in whole or in part, or to benefit from tax advantages or recover amounts paid. (This provision was amended by Article 22 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015, concerning the Finance Law for 2016).

- On any person who failed to comply with the provisions of Article 59, thirdly, of the Tax Code on Income of Natural Persons and Corporate Tax, or who altered the transaction recording device, damaged the data contained therein, or falsified it. (This provision was added by Article 48 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015, concerning the Finance Law for 2016).
