Every tax debt subject to the Financial Collector's calculations shall incur a delay penalty calculated at a rate of 0.75% for each month or part thereof of the principal debt. (The penalty rate was reduced from 1% to 0.75% by Article 51 of Law No. 85 of 2006, dated December 25, 2006, relating to the Finance Act of 2007).

The delay period is calculated starting from the first day following the expiration of ninety days from the date of signing the payment demand acknowledging the debt or the notification. Notification is considered to be the debtor's or his agent's signature, in the presence of the competent official, on a notification slip, or on the summons to appear on the specified date. A written court decision resolving a legal or factual issue; or a court decision issued by the court on a specific matter.  The delay period ends on the last day of the month in which the payment was made. This rate is reduced to 0.5% for amounts paid within one year of the expiry of the ninety-day period referred to in the second paragraph of this article. (This paragraph was added by Article 52 of Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2009, relating to the Finance Act of 2010). The procedures for follow-up and enforcement to ensure the recovery of the debt remain applicable. (This paragraph was added by Article 52 of Law No. 71 of 2009, dated December 21, 2009, relating to the Finance Act of 2010). The delay penalty stipulated in this article cannot exceed the amount of the principal debt. (This paragraph was added by Article 68 of Decree No. 21 of 2021, dated December 28, 2021, relating to the Finance Act of 2022).
