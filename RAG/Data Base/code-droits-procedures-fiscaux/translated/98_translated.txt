Any person who fails to maintain the accounting records or registers prescribed by tax legislation, or who refuses to submit them to tax officials, or who destroys them before the expiry of the legally prescribed period for their retention, shall be punished by a fine ranging from 100 dinars to 10,000 dinars.  In the event of recidivism within five years, if the offender is subject to payment under the actual system, he shall be punished by imprisonment for a period ranging from sixteen days to three years and a fine ranging from 1,000 dinars to 50,000 dinars. The fine referred to in the first paragraph of this section shall also apply in the case of failure to submit the software programs, systems, and IT applications prescribed in the first paragraph of Section 9 of this Code, and the data necessary for their use, and the resulting entries and processing, to the tax officials, whether recorded on magnetic media or not, or in the event of failure to grant tax officials access to these programs, systems, and sub-IT applications, registers, and databases referred to in the second paragraph of Section 9 of this Code, as well as the resulting entries and processing. (This paragraph was added by Section 37 of Law No. 54 of 2013 dated December 30, 2013, relating to the Finance Law for 2014 and amended by Section 51 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, relating to the Finance Law for 2016).

A fine of 1,000 dinars shall be imposed on any person who fails to comply with the provisions of the last paragraph of Section 9 of this Code. (This paragraph was added by Section 51 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, relating to the Finance Law for 2016).

Any person who maintains double accounting or uses forged accounting documents, registers, or records with the intent to evade payment in whole or in part, or to benefit from tax exemptions, or to recover amounts paid, shall be punished by imprisonment for a period ranging from sixteen days to three years and a fine ranging from 1,000 dinars to 50,000 dinars.
