Subject to the provisions of Articles 10, 11, and 12 of the Register and Tax Stamp Code concerning the place of registration of contracts and writings, the payments subject to the provisions of this Code shall be levied and declared as follows:

1. At the location of the main establishment for natural persons engaged in a commercial, industrial, craft, or non-commercial professional activity within a single establishment or several establishments located in Tunisia.

2. At the location of the main residence for natural persons who generate income or profits exclusively from activities other than those referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article or from abroad. In the absence of a residence in Tunisia, the payment shall be levied and declared at the location of the main source of income or profits.

3. At the location of the registered office or head office for companies and other legal persons. In the absence of a registered office or permanent establishment in Tunisia, the payment shall be levied and declared at the location of the main source of income or profits.

Regardless of the provisions of the first paragraph of this Article, and except for registration fees, by order, the place of levy of payments subject to the provisions of this Code may be determined based on criteria taking into account, in particular, the sector of activity of the taxpayers, and the importance of the transaction volume. The scope and conditions for applying the provisions of this paragraph shall be determined by order. (This paragraph was added by Article 69 of Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006, relating to the Finance Act for 2007, and completed by Article 48 of Law No. 58 of 2010 dated December 17, 2010, relating to the Finance Act for 2011). Taxpayers who carry out their professional activity in more than one establishment must attach to their tax returns detailed data relating to the activity of each of their establishments, according to a model provided by the administration.  The tax authority that informed the taxpayer of a thorough tax audit or the results of a preliminary tax audit, and of all subsequent actions and procedures, shall remain competent in the event that the taxpayer informs it of a change of address in accordance with the provisions of Article 57 of the Code on Income Tax for Natural Persons and Corporate Tax, after its intervention as mentioned, and within the limits of the payments and the period covered by the information addressed to the taxpayer. (This paragraph was added by Article 50 of Law No. 71 of 2009 dated December 21, 2009, relating to the Finance Act for 2010). The provisions of this Article shall not prevent tax officials within whose district the taxpayer's activity is carried out from exercising their right of control.
