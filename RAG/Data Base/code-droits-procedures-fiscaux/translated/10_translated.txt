Tax authorities' demands and notifications, for which a specific time limit for response is prescribed, are served through their agents, bailiffs, treasury bailiffs, or by registered mail with acknowledgement of receipt.  Service is subject to the provisions of the Code of Civil Procedure.

Service is effected at the principal address declared by the taxpayer in the declaration of existence provided for in Article 56 of the Income Tax Code for Individuals and Companies, or declared in the last annual tax return for individuals not subject to the obligation to file a declaration of existence.  Tax authorities shall not oppose addresses not notified in accordance with the provisions of Article 57 of the Income Tax Code for Individuals and Companies. (This paragraph was added by Article 60 of Law No. 27 of 2012 dated December 29, 2012, concerning the Finance Law for 2013).

In the absence thereof, service is effected at the address contained in the contract, correspondence, or referral. (This paragraph was added by Article 60 of Law No. 27 of 2012 dated December 29, 2012, concerning the Finance Law for 2013).

If the taxpayer dies and their heir cannot be identified despite inquiries by the tax authorities, and no one provides proof of death, the tax authorities shall notify the heir without specifying their name. After a period of thirty days from the date of such notification, service is effected on the heir without specifying their name.  Notification and service are effected at the last address declared to the tax authorities by the deceased; in the absence thereof, at their last known address according to the aforementioned authorities. (This paragraph was added by Article 54 of Law No. 71 of 2009 dated December 21, 2009, concerning the Finance Law for 2010).
