In the event of a delay in payment following intervention by tax audit services, the funds collected by the state in the form of payments—the payment being a sum of money paid by individuals and companies proportionally to their income or profits—are subject to the following:

The rate of the late payment penalty stipulated in the first paragraph of Article 81 of this Code is increased to 1.25%. This late payment penalty is calculated on the assessed income based on the apparent and obvious personal expenses and wealth growth stipulated in Article 36 of the Code on Income Tax for Individuals and Corporate Tax, starting from January 1st of the year following the year concerned by the wealth growth. (This paragraph was completed by Article 19 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, relating to the Finance Act for 2016, and amended by Article 51 of Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018, relating to the Finance Act for 2019).

In addition to the late payment penalty stipulated in the first paragraph of this Article, a fixed penalty is added, calculated at a rate of:

1.25% of the amount due if the delay in payment does not exceed 60 days; 2.5% of the amount due if the delay in payment exceeds 60 days. (This paragraph was added by Article 51 of Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018, relating to the Finance Act for 2019).  The penalty stipulated in the first paragraph of this Article is reduced by 20% if the amount due is paid within a maximum of thirty days from the date of acknowledgment of debt stipulated in Article 45 of this Code, provided that the acknowledgment of debt is made before the expiry of the objection period stipulated in Article 55 of this Code. (This Article was amended by Article 48 of Law No. 85 of 2006 dated December 25, 2006, relating to the Finance Act for 2007; by Article 46 of Law No. 78 of 2016 dated December 17, 2016, relating to the Finance Act for 2017; and by Article 51 of Law No. 56 of 2018 dated December 27, 2018, relating to the Finance Act for 2019).

The penalty stipulated in this Article shall not apply to amounts due following a thorough or limited tax audit, within the limit of the excess payment approved by the tax authorities or by the courts by final judgment within the framework of the same audit process. (This paragraph was added by Article 23 of Law No. 77 of 2008 dated December 22, 2008, relating to the Finance Act for 2009, and amended by Article 48 of Decree No. 21 of 2021 dated December 28, 2021, relating to the Finance Act for 2022).
