Any person who fails to maintain the accounting records or registers stipulated by tax legislation, or who refuses to submit them to tax officials, or who destroys them before the expiry of the legally prescribed retention period, shall be punished by a fine ranging from 100 dinars to 10,000 dinars.  In the event of recidivism within five years, if the offender is subject to payment under the actual system, he shall be punished by imprisonment for a period ranging from sixteen days to three years and a fine ranging from 1,000 dinars to 50,000 dinars. The fine referred to in the first paragraph of this section shall also apply in the case of failure to submit the software, systems, and IT applications stipulated in the first paragraph of Article 9 of this Code, and the data necessary for their use, and the resulting records and processes, to tax officials, whether recorded on magnetic media or not, or failure to allow them access to them; or in the case of failure to allow tax authorities access to the subsidiary software, systems, and IT applications, registers, and databases referred to in the second paragraph of Article 9 of this Code, as well as the resulting records and processes. (This paragraph was added by Article 37 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, concerning the Finance Act for 2014, and amended by Article 51 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015, concerning the Finance Act for 2016).

A fine of 1,000 dinars shall be imposed on any person who fails to comply with the provisions of the last paragraph of Article 9 of this Code. (This paragraph was added by Article 51 of Law No. 53 of 2015, dated December 25, 2015, concerning the Finance Act for 2016).
