Any person who commits the following acts shall be punished by imprisonment for a period ranging from sixteen days to three years and a fine ranging from 1000 dinars to 50000 dinars:

- Creating fictitious legal situations or submitting forged documents, invoices, or expense reports, or concealing the true legal nature of a contract or agreement with the intent to benefit from tax advantages, reduce the amount due, or obtain a refund (this paragraph was amended by Article 53 of Law No. 53 of 2015 dated December 25, 2015, relating to the Finance Act of 2016).

- Engaging in transactions that result in the transfer of property to a third party in order to evade the payment of tax debts.

- Increasing the surplus of value-added tax or consumption tax, or reducing the turnover figure to evade the payment of tax or duty, or to benefit from a refund of tax or duty amounts.  The penalty shall apply in cases where the shortfall or increase equals or exceeds 30% of the declared turnover or surplus payment.  The penalty is a sanction and remedy imposed in the name of society upon a person criminally liable for a crime committed, based on a court judgment.
