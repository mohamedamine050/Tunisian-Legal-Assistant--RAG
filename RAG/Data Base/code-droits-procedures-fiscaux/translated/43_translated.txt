Tax authorities shall inform taxpayers in writing of any amendments related to their tax situation.  Notification shall be effected by the methods stipulated in Article 10 of this Code. The notification shall include, in particular:

- The type of tax audit;
- The assets covered by the tax assessment;
- The substance of the irregularities and the method used to readjust the assessment;
- The amount of tax due or the amendments relating to the tax surplus, losses, and deferred expenses according to the law;
- The penalties incurred;
- An invitation to the taxpayer to submit their observations, objections, and reservations regarding the audit results within the time limit set according to the circumstances: forty-five days from the date of notification for initial tax audits or in-depth tax audits, and ten days from the same date for limited tax audits. (This article was amended by Article 48 of Decree No. 21 of 2021 dated December 28, 2021, relating to the Finance Act for 2022).

Notification is deemed to have been effected by the taxpayer's or their representative's signature, in the presence of the competent official, on the notification document or on the summons to appear at the appointed time.
