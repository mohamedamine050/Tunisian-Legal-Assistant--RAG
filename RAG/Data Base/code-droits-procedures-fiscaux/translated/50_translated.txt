Mandatory assessment, as stipulated in Article 47 of this Code, is implemented by a reasoned decision issued by the Director General of Taxation, the Head of the National Tax Control and Research Unit, the Director of the Large Enterprises Department, the Director of the Medium Enterprises Department, or the Head of the Regional Tax Assessment Control Center, based on the findings of the tax audit.  Assets collected by the State in the form of taxes, wherein a tax is a monetary sum paid by individuals and companies proportionately to their income or profits.  The recovery of tax claims is subject to their availability. (This paragraph was amended by Article 38 of Law No. 1 of 2012, dated May 16, 2012, relating to the Supplementary Finance Act of 2012; Article 42 of Law No. 54 of 2013, dated December 30, 2013, relating to the Finance Act of 2014; and Article 66 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017, relating to the Finance Act of 2018). The decision of mandatory assessment shall include the following information:

- The tax authorities that conducted the tax audit.
- The method used for the assessment of the tax.
- The legal basis for the decision.
- The full name and rank of the investigators.
- The start and end dates, and the location of the comprehensive or limited audit. (This item was amended by Article 48 of Decree No. 21 of 2021, dated December 28, 2021, relating to the Finance Act of 2022).
- The years and taxes covered by the tax audit.
- The amount of tax due, related penalties, or adjustments related to tax surpluses and their related penalties, as well as adjustments related to losses and deferred expenses according to the law. (This item was amended by Article 35 of Law No. 78 of 2016, dated December 17, 2016, relating to the Finance Act of 2017).
- The tax office where the amounts due will be levied.
- Notification to the taxpayer of their right to appeal the mandatory assessment decision before the competent court of first instance and the deadline for doing so.
- Notification to the taxpayer of the possibility of suspending the execution of the decision in accordance with the provisions of Article 52 of this Code. The decision of mandatory assessment for tax penalties, Assets collected by the State in the form of taxes, wherein a tax is a monetary sum paid by individuals and companies proportionately to their income or profits., as stipulated in paragraphs three and four of Article 47 of this Code, shall include the following information: (This paragraph was amended by Article 30 of Law No. 66 of 2017, dated December 18, 2017, relating to the Finance Act of 2018).

- The tax authorities that conducted the monitoring or audit.
- The type of violation detected.
- The method used for the assessment of the penalty imposed for the violation.
- The legal basis for the decision.
- The full name and rank of the investigators.
- The period covered by the penalty assessment.
- The amount of the penalty imposed.
- The tax office where the amounts due will be levied.
- Notification to the taxpayer of their right to appeal the mandatory assessment decision before the competent court of first instance and the deadline for doing so. (This paragraph was added by Article 42 of Law No. 59 of 2014, dated December 26, 2014, relating to the Finance Act of 2015).
