In all cases of forgery, the allegedly forged documents shall be deposited with the court registry upon submission. The court clerk shall sign each document and prepare a detailed report on the physical condition of the documents, and the person submitting them shall also sign. If the latter refuses to sign or is unable to do so, this shall be stated. If the allegedly forged documents are taken from a public repository, the official handing them over shall sign them in the manner described above.  Furthermore, the allegedly forged documents shall be signed by the judicial police officer, the investigating judge, and the private prosecutor, if present, and the suspect at the time of his presence. If the suspect or the private prosecutor refuses to sign or is unable to do so, this shall be stated in the report.
