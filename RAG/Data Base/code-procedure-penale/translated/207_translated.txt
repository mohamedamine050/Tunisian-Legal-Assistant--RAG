(Amended by Law No. 43 of 2000 dated April 17, 2000). - Judgments issued in criminal and misdemeanor matters may be appealed.  The appeal is a legal procedure enabling the party of choice to request a review of the judgment issued by the previous court. Judgments issued by the lower courts in misdemeanor matters and by the lower courts hearing felonies are appealed to the Court of Appeal. A court is a place where litigation takes place between litigants; it is a civil institution under the judicial authority that must have its independence within the three branches of government.
