The Attorney General of the Republic, under the supervision of the Secretary of State for Justice, is responsible for ensuring the application of criminal law throughout the Republic's territory.

He may represent the Public Prosecution, the entity responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the judicial courts, before the Courts of Appeal, and he has authority over all other representatives of the Public Prosecution.

He also has the right to call upon public force during the performance of his duties.

In the event of an impediment, the Attorney General of the Republic shall be replaced by a Public Prosecutor appointed by the Secretary of State for Justice.
