Decisions of the Investigating Judge

The Investigating Judge, a person assigned by the judiciary or the Public Prosecution to investigate a case, shall immediately forward all decisions to the Public Prosecutor for review. The Public Prosecutor has the right to appeal them in all circumstances within four days of their date. The party with a personal right shall be informed of the decisions within forty-eight hours and has the right to appeal any decision that is prejudicial to his civil rights before the expiry of four days from the date of notification.  The decision to refer the case to the Indictment Chamber shall be notified to the accused, who shall have the right to appeal within the same timeframe.
