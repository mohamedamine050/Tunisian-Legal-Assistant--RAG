The Investigating Judge, appointed by the judiciary or the Public Prosecutor's Office to investigate the case, either on his own initiative or at the request of the Attorney General, may proceed to the scene of the crime, or to the residence of the suspect, or to any other place where he suspects the existence of things useful for uncovering the truth. When proceeding to the scene of the crime on his own initiative, he must inform the Attorney General; however, if the latter is not present, he shall carry out the necessary actions without waiting for his presence.  A person suspected of [a crime], before any charges are filed, may be transferred to the location of the investigation if deemed necessary.
