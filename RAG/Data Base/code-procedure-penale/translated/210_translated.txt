(Amended by Law No. 43 of 2000, dated April 17, 2000). - The right of appeal is granted to:  Firstly: The accused; the convicted person;  an accused against whom a judgment has been rendered, determining their responsibility for a crime, and imposing a penalty or precautionary measure.  For a felony; a felony is a serious crime punishable under the law and subject to severe penalties such as imprisonment; or a misdemeanor; misdemeanors are crimes that warrant punishment by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars; and the civilly liable party. Secondly: The party with a personal right concerning their civil rights only. Thirdly: The agent of the Republic. Fourthly: Public administrations and financial branches, acting as representatives of the Public Prosecutor's Office in cases where the law grants them the right to exercise the public prosecution. Fifthly: The Attorneys General at the Courts of Appeal.
