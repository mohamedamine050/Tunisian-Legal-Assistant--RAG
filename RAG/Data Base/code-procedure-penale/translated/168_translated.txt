Every judgment must state: Firstly, the court that issued the judgment, the names of the judges, and the representative of the Public Prosecution; the Public Prosecution, the body responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the judicial courts, and the court clerk who attended the session, and the date of the judgment; Secondly, the names, titles, professions, addresses, and criminal records of the accused; Thirdly, the subject of the charge; Fourthly, the factual and legal grounds, even in the case of an acquittal; Fifthly, the text of the judgment ordering punishment or acquittal and the penal provisions applied; Sixthly, the costs.
