If the Court of Appeal undertakes the case in the first instance, it is competent to rule on requests for referral according to the conditions stipulated in Articles 184 and 187.  It retains this jurisdiction, even after a final ruling on the original matter, to authorize referral according to the conditions stipulated in paragraphs 1 and 2 of Article 189.  The court is a place where litigation takes place between specialists, and it is a civil court that falls under the judicial authority, which must have its independence within the three branches of government.
