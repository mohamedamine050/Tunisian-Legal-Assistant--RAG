It is permissible to join proceedings together in cases where crimes are linked, as set out in the preceding article, or in similar circumstances, if the circumstances necessitate the unification of the investigation. If the linked acts fall under the jurisdiction of courts of different levels due to the type of crime or the status of the perpetrators, the case shall be heard by the higher court. However, if the acts belong to the same category of crimes, the court competent to hear one of the aforementioned crimes shall also be competent to hear the other, without regard to the provisions of Article 129.  The joinder of proceedings is mandatory. The joinder of proceedings is permissible if the crimes are linked, or in other similar circumstances, if the circumstances necessitate the unification of the investigation, as provided for in Article 55 of the Criminal Code.
