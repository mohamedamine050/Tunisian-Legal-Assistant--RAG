The First President, administratively, informs the president of the court to which the judge belongs, by a petition of judicial misconduct.  Judicial misconduct: conduct that incites or instigates hatred, disgust, contempt, or severe ridicule based on a person's race or religion. And the submission of a petition of judicial misconduct: conduct that incites or instigates hatred, disgust, contempt, or severe ridicule based on a person's race or religion. This does not result in the judge against whom the misconduct is alleged abandoning consideration of the case. However, the First President, after obtaining the opinion of the Public Prosecutor, may authorize the suspension of the investigative proceedings, pleadings, or trial.
