The summons is issued at the request of the Public Prosecutor;  the Public Prosecutor being the body responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the judicial courts, or by the party with standing, or any administration legally entitled to do so. The summons shall include a statement of the act giving rise to the prosecution and the applicable legal provision. It shall also include a statement of the competent court, the place, time, and date of the hearing, and the capacity of the summoned party, whether accused, civil defendant, or witness. If the summons is issued at the request of the party with standing, it shall include the name, surname, profession, and place of origin or domicile of the latter. The summons addressed to a witness must further specify that failure to appear, refusal to give testimony, or falsification thereof is punishable by law.
