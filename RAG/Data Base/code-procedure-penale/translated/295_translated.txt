(Amended by Law No. 43 of 2000, dated April 17, 2000). - If a misdemeanor is committed—a misdemeanor being defined in this law as crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars—or an offense—an offense being defined in this law as crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years—or a misdemeanor—a misdemeanor being defined as crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars—or an offense—an offense being defined as crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine of sixty dinars.  Article 122, Code of Criminal Procedure.

During the court session, the judge—a judge being a judicial officer who makes legal decisions in court and contributes to the enforcement of law and the administration of justice—of the district court, or the presiding judge of the criminal court, or the primary criminal division, or the criminal division—the criminal division being a division of a court dealing with criminal matters—shall hear misdemeanors in the first instance, except those under the jurisdiction of the district judge.  The Court of Appeal or the appellate criminal division shall draw up a report on the incident, shall proceed to hear the accused and the witnesses, then the court shall impose the penalties provided for by law, by a judgment enforceable regardless of appeal. If the crime committed during the court session of the district court—a court being a place where litigation takes place between parties, and a civil institution belonging to the judicial authority, which must have its independence within the three branches of government—falls under the jurisdiction of the criminal court, then the district judge shall draw up a report and immediately forward it to the public prosecutor—a public prosecutor being an official who acts on behalf of the state. If the crime committed is a felony—a felony being a serious crime punishable under the law and subject to severe penalties such as imprisonment—then the district judge, or the presiding judge of the criminal court, or the primary criminal division, or the criminal division shall hear misdemeanors in the first instance, except those under the jurisdiction of the district judge. The Court of Appeal or the appellate criminal division shall immediately forward the reports drawn up on the incident and the hearing of the accused and witnesses to the public prosecutor, who shall authorize the opening of an investigation. However, the criminal court may judge the crime immediately.
