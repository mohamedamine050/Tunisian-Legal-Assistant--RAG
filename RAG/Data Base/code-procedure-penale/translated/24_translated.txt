The Public Prosecutor shall be represented by himself or by his assistants.  The Public Prosecution is the body responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the courts.  Before the Court of Appeal, which is a venue for litigation between parties, a civil entity under the judicial authority, which must have its independence within the three branches of government, the Public Prosecutor is responsible for ensuring the application of criminal law throughout the region under the jurisdiction of the Court of Appeal. He has authority over all representatives of the Public Prosecution subordinate to the Court of Appeal. He also has the right to directly call upon public force in the exercise of his duties.
