The provisions of this Act set forth the rules to be followed for the optimal organization and use of land, and for the planning and development of residential communities, with the aim of:

- Adapting the living environment;
- Ensuring the efficient use of resources;
- Protecting conservation areas;
- Protecting natural and cultural sites, including archaeological sites as defined in Article 2 of the Law on the Protection of Archaeological, Historical and Traditional Arts Heritage No. 35 of 1994, dated February 24, 1994;
- Maintaining public safety and health;
- Ensuring a balanced distribution between urban and rural areas, within a framework that reconciles economic development, social development and environmental balances, thereby guaranteeing sustainable development and the right of the citizen to a healthy environment.
