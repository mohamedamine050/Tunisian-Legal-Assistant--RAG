The Member of Parliament does not enjoy parliamentary immunity with respect to the crimes of defamation, slander, and insult. 
Defamation occurs through any accusation or attribution of a matter in public that would tarnish the honor or reputation of a person or official body.
The Member of Parliament also does not enjoy immunity in cases of disruption of the normal functioning of the Assembly, whether within or outside the Assembly, including insults, defamation, slander, and physical violence.
