No person may establish or manage a collective employment institution or a money-transfer company:

- If they have been the subject of a written court decision, which resolves a legal issue or fact, for committing forgery, theft, breach of trust, fraud, or for:

* Misappropriation of the funds or property of another, or embezzlement committed by a public trustee; or
* Issuing a check without sufficient funds or participating in such an act; or
* Engaging in a felony, as defined by this criminal law, which is punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years.
* Engaging in a misdemeanor, as defined by this criminal law, which is punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
* Engaging in an offense, as defined by this criminal law, which is punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or by a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)

- If they have been subject to an administrative regulation concerning money transfers, or a decision declaring them bankrupt or responsible for bankruptcy.
