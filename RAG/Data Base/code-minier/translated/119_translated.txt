## Title VIII

**On the Verification of Offenses and Penalties**

Every offense described as a felony, within the meaning of this law, which warrants punishment by death or imprisonment exceeding five years, shall be verified.

Offenses described as misdemeanors, which warrant punishment by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, shall also be verified.

Finally, offenses described as violations, which warrant punishment not exceeding fifteen days of imprisonment or a fine of sixty dinars, shall also be verified.

The provisions of this Code shall be implemented through reports drafted in accordance with the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure by Judicial Police Officers, officers of the Ministry of Mines, or other legally qualified officials.

The appropriate individual shall be appointed for the position, ensuring that they possess the qualifications and competencies necessary to fulfill the requirements of the role.

Officers of the Ministry of Mines or other qualified officials, tasked with verifying offenses described as felonies, within the meaning of this law, which warrant punishment by death or imprisonment exceeding five years, shall be appointed.

Similarly, officers of the Ministry of Mines or other qualified officials, tasked with verifying offenses described as misdemeanors, which warrant punishment by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, shall be appointed.

Finally, officers of the Ministry of Mines or other qualified officials, tasked with verifying offenses described as violations, which warrant punishment not exceeding fifteen days of imprisonment or a fine of sixty dinars, shall be appointed. 

These officials shall be selected from among public officials belonging to the engineering or technical corps, with at least five years of service and sufficient experience in the field of mining research and exploitation.
