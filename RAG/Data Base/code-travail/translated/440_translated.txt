Every nomination for the Order of Labour must be submitted by the employer and, except for employees of the State, local public authorities, and public institutions with an administrative character, directed to the Wali of the region where the nominee resides. It must include the following documents:

1) An application written by the employer on plain paper, specifying the name and surname of the nominee, his date and place of birth, his profession, the exact date of his entry into the employer's service, and where applicable, the date of his departure therefrom, the dates of his absences from work and their reasons, as well as the employer's name, profession, and address. The application must be accompanied by a report from the employer concerning the reasons justifying the award of the Order of Labour to the nominee proposed by him, specifying, where applicable, the position held by the nominee and the date of his last promotion.

2) A certificate of criminal record for the employee, dated within the last three months.

3) The application must be accompanied, where applicable, by all necessary certificates proving the employee's seniority.
