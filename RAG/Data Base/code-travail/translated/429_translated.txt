The Order of Merit, designated the "Order of Labour," is awarded to reward the productivity and professional conscientiousness demonstrated by workers in the services they perform in trade, industry, agriculture, and their related sectors, regardless of type.

This Order is also awarded to workers in the liberal professions, traditional industrial establishments, mutual aid societies, civil companies, unions, associations, and groups, regardless of type.

The Order of Labour may be awarded to workers employed by the state, local authorities, and public administrative bodies.
