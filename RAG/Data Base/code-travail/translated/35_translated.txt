Legally constituted groups bound by a common work agreement may, in their own name, claim damages against other groups, against their own members, or against any person bound by the agreement who may violate the stipulated obligations.  Damages [1] refers to harm inflicted in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. [2]  Damages also encompass loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, etc. In crimes and quasi-crimes, damages are the actual loss suffered by the claimant, the necessary expenses incurred or to be incurred to repair the consequences of the harmful act, and any benefit the claimant has been deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of that act.
