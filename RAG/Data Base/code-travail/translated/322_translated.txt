Anyone who prevents the officers tasked with monitoring the institutions stipulated in Article 303 from carrying out their duties; anyone who operates an establishment without prior authorization; and anyone who persists in operating an establishment that has been ordered temporarily closed under the provisions of Articles 304 and 305 shall be punished by a fine ranging from 144 to 720 dinars and/or imprisonment from 16 days to two months.

In the latter two cases, the court may also order the sealing of the establishment's machinery, equipment, and doors.

In case of recidivism, the offender shall be punished by the maximum fine and the maximum prison sentence stipulated in this Article, or by one of the two penalties. The penalties may be doubled.

With regard to misdemeanors to which this Article applies, a report of violation may be issued daily against the operator.

Crimes which warrant the death penalty or imprisonment exceeding five years are described as felonies under this law.  Crimes which warrant imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or a fine exceeding sixty dinars, are described as misdemeanors.  Crimes which warrant a penalty not exceeding fifteen days imprisonment or sixty dinars fine are described as violations.
