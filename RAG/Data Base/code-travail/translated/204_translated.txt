The parties may, before the Labour Department, be assisted by a worker or employer in the same profession, by a lawyer, or be represented by one of them.

The head of the department may order the personal presence of the parties.

Heads of institutions who are represented by the managing director or an employee of the institution are considered to be personally present before the department.

The representative must hold a power of attorney drawn up on plain paper or a copy thereof.

Lawyers are exempt from submitting a power of attorney.
