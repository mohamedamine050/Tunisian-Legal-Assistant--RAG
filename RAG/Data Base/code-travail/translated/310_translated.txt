If the operation of legally licensed industrial establishments, or establishments existing prior to the organization of their subsidiary industry, or also industrial establishments not included in the list of organized establishments, results in serious harm or significant danger to workers, neighbors, or public health—defined as a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being—that cannot be eliminated by the measures stipulated in this chapter and the texts adopted for its application, then these establishments may be definitively closed by a decision of the Minister responsible for Industry, after obtaining the opinion of the Special Committee on Dangerous, Health-Harming, and Noisy Establishments.
