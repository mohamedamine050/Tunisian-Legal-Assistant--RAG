In non-industrial and non-agricultural activities:

1. Children aged thirteen years or older may be employed in light work that does not harm their health and development, or affect their school attendance, their ability to study, or their participation in vocational guidance or training programs approved by the competent public authorities.

2. No child under the age of sixteen years may be employed in light work for more than two hours a day, whether on school days or school holidays, and the total number of hours allocated to school and light work shall not exceed seven hours a day.

3.  The types of light work and the necessary preliminary precautions to be taken when employing children in such work shall be determined by order. This order shall also determine the number of working hours for children aged between sixteen and eighteen years in light work.

4. The employment of children in light work is prohibited on weekly rest days and public holidays.
