In the case of recidivism, the prescribed punishment is doubled.  Recidivism, within the meaning of this Code, is considered to have occurred if an offense is committed similar to the first offense within one year following the date of the final judgment.  Crimes described as felonies, within the meaning of this law, are those that warrant punishment by death or imprisonment exceeding five years.  Crimes described as misdemeanors are those that warrant punishment by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.  Crimes described as infractions are those that warrant punishment not exceeding fifteen days' imprisonment or sixty dinars' fine.  Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
