The Order of Labour comprises five grades:

— An Exceptional Grade ("Gold Medal"), awarded unconditionally and exceptionally to certain individuals who have rendered valuable services in the field of labour.

— A Grand Grade ("Gold Medal"), awarded after 30 years of actual service.

— A First Grade ("Gold-plated Silver Medal"), awarded after 25 years of actual service.

— A Second Grade ("Silver Medal"), awarded after 20 years of actual service.

— A Third Grade ("Bronze Medal"), awarded after 15 years of actual service.
