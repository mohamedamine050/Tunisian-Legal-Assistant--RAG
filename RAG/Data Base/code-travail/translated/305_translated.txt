If a risk or harm is anticipated to the safety, health, and well-being of workers, neighbors, or public health from the operation of an establishment not included in the list of classified establishments, the Minister responsible for industry, after obtaining the opinion of the special committee on hazardous, health-damaging, and nuisance establishments, may authorize its owner to take the necessary measures to legally remove the identified risks or harm.  If the establishment owner fails to comply within the specified timeframe, the Minister may temporarily suspend the operation of the establishment, regardless of the penalties provided for in Section Five of this Chapter.
