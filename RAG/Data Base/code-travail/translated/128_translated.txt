If a contract is terminated, and the contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights concerning the specific transaction; and an employee was employed by the lessor, and this employee had spent at least six consecutive months working before enjoying his paid annual leave, and this termination was not the result of a serious fault committed by the employee, then this employee is entitled to a grant corresponding to the number of leave days he should have enjoyed due to the continuous work he performed at the time the contract was terminated and which he was unable to enjoy thereafter.
