Disputes arising between suppliers or their representatives and seafarers, except for masters, shall be settled by way of amicable settlement.

**Amicable Settlement**

[1] **Contract**
A contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights in relation to the specific transaction.

An amicable settlement is a mechanism for resolving a dispute and ending litigation. This is achieved by each party relinquishing some of their claims or by the delivery of money or a right.

[2] **Contract**
A contract is a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights in relation to the specific transaction.

Amicable settlement enables the parties to resolve an existing dispute or to prevent a potential dispute, by each party mutually relinquishing their rights.

If an amicable settlement is not possible, the dispute shall be decided according to the rules of the competent court and the procedures prescribed in this regard.  This also applies to claims relating to liability for errors committed during the execution of the charter contract.
