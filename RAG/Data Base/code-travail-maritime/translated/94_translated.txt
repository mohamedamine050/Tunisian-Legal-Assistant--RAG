The seaman may disembark ashore if the ship's doctor or any other doctor designated by the maritime authority proves the necessity of doing so. The seaman shall be admitted to hospital if his condition warrants it. If the disembarkation mentioned in the preceding paragraph takes place in a foreign port, the maritime authority may require the master to deposit in the fund designated by it the amount that may be required for the seaman's treatment and the costs of his repatriation, subject to subsequent adjustment of the amount.
