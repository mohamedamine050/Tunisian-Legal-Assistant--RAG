Legally constituted consortia, bound by a joint maritime labor agreement, may, in their own name, claim damages against other consortia, against their own members, or against any person connected to the agreement who breaches the stipulated obligations.

**Damages**

[1] Injury inflicted unlawfully, causing harm to a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or reputation.

[2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, etc.  - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damages are the actual losses incurred by the claimant, the necessary expenses they were or will be forced to incur to remedy the consequences of the harmful act, and the benefits they have been deprived of within the normal scope of the consequences of this act.
