Persons bound by a Joint Maritime Labour Agreement may bring an action for damages against other persons or entities bound by the Agreement who violate their contractual obligations. 

**[1] Harm inflicted in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor.** 

**[2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or impairment of physical well-being, reputation, etc. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, damage refers to the actual loss suffered by the claimant, as well as the necessary expenses incurred or to be incurred to remedy the consequences of the act that caused harm. It also includes any benefits the claimant was deprived of within the ordinary scope of the consequences of that act.**
