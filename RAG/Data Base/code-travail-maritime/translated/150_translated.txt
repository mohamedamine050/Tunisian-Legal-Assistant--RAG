Any person, even if they exceed the age of eighteen years, who boards a ship after presenting proof that they have the cultural level determined by a decision of the Secretary of State in charge of the merchant fleet, with the intention of preparing themselves for the functions of a ship officer apprentice, its machinery, or the electrical wireless service, shall be considered a trained sailor.  The duration of the person's sailing or voyages as a trainee shall not exceed twenty-four months. Trained sailors shall board the ship in all cases, in addition to the number of sailors required by the work regulations on board the ship. The shipowner shall accept the sailing of the trained sailor presented to them by the maritime authority, to the extent that the ship's capacity allows.  A trained sailor shall hold the rank of an officer apprentice.
