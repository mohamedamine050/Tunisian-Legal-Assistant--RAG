Subject to the provisions of the preceding article, auditors, as well as their assistants and experts, are obligated not to disclose professional secrecy regarding the facts, acts, and information of which they become aware by virtue of performing their duties.  Auditors must draw the attention of the general meeting to any irregularities or improprieties they have discovered during the performance of their duties. They are also required to inform the Public Prosecutor of any acts constituting crimes that come to their knowledge, without incurring any liability for disclosing professional secrecy.
