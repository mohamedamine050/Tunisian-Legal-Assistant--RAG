Holders of preferred profit shares, exceeding the company's assets over its liabilities by including the company's capital within its debts at the end of the financial year, without voting rights, may not contest the company's dissolution before liquidation, whether resulting from losses incurred, merger with another company, or any other reason. However, holders of preferred profit shares, exceeding the company's assets over its liabilities by including the company's capital within its debts at the end of the financial year, without voting rights, retain their right to bring an action against the company for damages, [1] harm caused in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or assets, [2] loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or damage to physical well-being, reputation, or the like - damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the loss actually suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he was or will be compelled to spend to repair the consequences of the act which caused him harm, as well as what he was deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act, which they may not exercise except collectively through their representatives, provided that they initiate the action within six months of the date of publication of the extraordinary general meeting's dissolution decision; otherwise, their right shall lapse.
