Mergers, divisions, changes of form, or groupings are permitted to achieve one of the following purposes: - To keep pace with economic changes at both domestic and international levels. - To provide capital to facilitate further investment, employment, and production. - To develop work and distribution methods. - To obtain new technology and improve product quality. - To gain greater export capacity and competitiveness. - To bolster the confidence of stakeholders in the institution. - To create and strengthen employment opportunities.

A citizen is defined as any individual enjoying all rights, whether civil, political, economic, social, cultural, or solidarity rights, in the state to which they belong.

Mergers, divisions, changes of form, or groupings are not permitted if they aim to evade payments or to achieve any of the purposes prohibited by Articles 5, 6, 7, and 8 of the law concerning competition and prices.
