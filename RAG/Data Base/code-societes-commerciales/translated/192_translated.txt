(Amended by Law No. 65 of 2005 dated July 27, 2005)

A member of the board of directors of a limited liability company must inform the company's legal representative of their appointment as an agent or a member of the board of directors, CEO, general manager, member of a collective management body, or supervisory board of another company, within one month of assuming the position. The company's legal representative must inform the ordinary general meeting of shareholders thereof at its first meeting. The company may claim damages resulting from the cumulation of duties.  Its right to do so lapses after three years from the date of commencing the new duties.
