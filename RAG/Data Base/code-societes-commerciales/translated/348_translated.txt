Preferred shares in profit exceeding the company's liabilities, entered into the company's capital as part of its debts at the end of the fiscal year, without voting rights, cannot represent more than one-third of the company's capital.  All shares constituting the capital of companies issuing preferred shares in profit exceeding the company's liabilities, entered into the company's capital as part of its debts at the end of the fiscal year, without voting rights, shall be mandatorily transferable without any restriction. Any condition contrary to this shall be null and void. Preferred shares in profit exceeding the company's liabilities, entered into the company's capital as part of its debts at the end of the fiscal year, without voting rights, must have the same par value as ordinary shares.
