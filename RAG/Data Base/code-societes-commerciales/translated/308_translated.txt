The subject matter upon which the speaker or writer bases their statement may be the reduction of capital, the return of contributions, the relinquishment of shares subscribed to but not issued, and the formation of a legal reserve or the rebalancing of the company's assets and its capital, which has decreased as a result of losses.  A capital reduction may also be resorted to if the company's losses reach half of its equity and it continues its activity without those assets being reconstituted.
