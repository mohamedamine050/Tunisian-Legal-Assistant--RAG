(Amended by the first section of Law No. 65 of 2005 dated July 27, 2005)

An expert, registered on the list of judicial experts, appointed by the President of the Primary Court, shall prepare, under his personal responsibility, a written report on the method of merger, after reviewing all necessary documents that the merging or absorbing company must provide him with and enable him to conduct all necessary investigations.  He shall also assess the value of the in-kind contributions and special privileges. The expert shall verify that the exchange ratio is fair and that the value assigned to the financial assets transferred is accurate. He shall also specify the method(s) used to determine the exchange ratio and explain whether it is appropriate. He must specify any particular difficulties in the valuation.  The expert in this case is considered a contributions supervisor.
