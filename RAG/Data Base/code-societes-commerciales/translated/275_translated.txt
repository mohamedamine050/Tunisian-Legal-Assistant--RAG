The general ordinary meeting must be held at least once a year within six months following the close of the fiscal year, for the purpose of:

- Monitoring the company's transactions.
- Approving the accounts of the past year, as the case may be.
- Taking decisions regarding the results after reviewing the report of the Board of Directors or the report of the collective management body and the report of the auditor.  The general meeting's decision approving the financial statements shall be void if it is not preceded by the submission of the report(s) of the auditor(s). (Paragraph 2 amended by Section 1 of Law No. 65 of 2005 dated July 27, 2005)
