The liquidator may not commence any liquidation proceedings until the decision appointing him is registered in the commercial register and published within 15 days of the appointment date.  Upon commencing his work, he must, with the participation of the managers, prepare a list of the company's assets and liabilities, signed by all of them.  He may not contravene the decisions of the general meeting of partners regarding the company's management, disposal, and the disposition of its assets.  Crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years are described as felonies under this law. Crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, are described as misdemeanors. Crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine not exceeding sixty dinars are described as offenses.  The liquidator is prohibited from undertaking arbitration or delivering a deed of settlement, except after obtaining express authorization from the governing bodies or the judge, as appropriate.  A settlement [1] is a contract designed to resolve a dispute and end litigation, either through mutual concessions by the parties or the payment of money or transfer of a right; [2] a contract by which the parties terminate an existing dispute or prevent a potential one, each reciprocally relinquishing their rights.
