Members of the collective management body or the supervisory board cannot be individuals declared bankrupt within five years from the date of the bankruptcy judgment, persons against whom a court order prohibits them from managing and administering companies, minors lacking legal capacity, persons convicted and prohibited from exercising public functions, or persons convicted of a felony or misdemeanor affecting public morals, public order, or the rules governing companies, nor those who cannot practice commerce by virtue of their profession. (Amended by Law No. 36 of 2016 dated April 29, 2016).

Furthermore, an employee in the service of the administration cannot be a member of the collective management body or the supervisory board, except with special authorization from the supervising ministry.
