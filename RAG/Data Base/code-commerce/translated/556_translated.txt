The creditor may also claim the entire goods or a part thereof, as long as they exist in kind, if they were delivered to the debtor to be secured with him by way of deposit or for the purpose of selling them on the owner's account.

The creditor may also claim the price of the aforementioned goods or part thereof if the price agreed upon by the parties was not paid, or if it exceeded the value of the goods sold or fell short of it, or if no consideration was given for it, or if it was not offset by a current account between the debtor and the buyer.
