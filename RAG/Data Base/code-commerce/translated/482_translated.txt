In confirmed cases, such as when the debtor closes their stores and flees, or the establishment is dissolved, or it is proven that the debtor or manager intentionally emptied their financial assets or dissipated their assets, the creditors, or some of them, or the Public Prosecutor 
[Public Prosecutor: the authority responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the courts] 
may bring the matter before the court convened in the Chamber of Deliberation.

The court will rule on the matter and declare its judgment in an open session. The court may authorize the taking of necessary precautionary measures to protect the rights of the creditors, at the request of the Public Prosecutor 
[Public Prosecutor: the authority responsible for defending the interests of society and applying the law before the courts]
, or one of the creditors, or on its own initiative.
