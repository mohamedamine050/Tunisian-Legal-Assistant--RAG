It is not permissible to appoint a judicial administrator who is a spouse, ascendant, descendant, relative, or in-law of the debtor up to the fourth degree of kinship, or who was employed by the debtor during the five years preceding the opening of the settlement proceedings, or any of the debtor's creditors, or an auditor of the debtor's accounts.

Furthermore, it is not permissible to appoint as judicial administrator any person who has a relationship of any kind with the company's agent, general manager, chief executive officer, member of its board of directors, member of its collective management body, sole general manager, member of its supervisory board, the general partner, or any partner.
