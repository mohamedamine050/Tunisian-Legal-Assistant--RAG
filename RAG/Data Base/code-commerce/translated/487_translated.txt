The waiver of rights does not encompass rights related solely to the debtor's person nor rights pertaining to purely intellectual interests. However, the trustee may intervene in lawsuits that could potentially result in a 
judgment
judgment
A written decision by a court that resolves a legal or factual issue.
 involving the payment of a sum of money.

Furthermore, the waiver of rights does not extend to the following:

1.  Earnings that are legally exempt from seizure.

2.  Salaries and wages earned by the debtor for his activities, without preventing the trustee from pursuing the actions prescribed in these cases. However, profits that are neither salaries nor wages shall be subject to seizure only to the extent determined by the appointed judge and deemed appropriate for the debtor's and his family's living necessities.
