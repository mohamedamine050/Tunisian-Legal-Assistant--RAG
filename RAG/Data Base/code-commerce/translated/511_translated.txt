The seals are removed from the relevant ledgers and documents, and the designated judge or his deputy hands them over to the custodian after briefly describing their condition in the minutes.

Commercial papers containing debts owed by the debtor that are due in the near future, or that require acceptance, or that necessitate preservation measures, are extracted by the designated judge or his deputy from the sealed items and handed over to the custodian for collection of their amounts after describing them.

Letters addressed to the debtor are also handed over to the custodian, who opens them. The debtor, if present, may witness their opening.
