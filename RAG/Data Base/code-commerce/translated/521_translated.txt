Within fifteen days of assuming his duties, the trustee must submit to the designated judge a concise report on the apparent state of the bankruptcy, its causes, its original circumstances, and any special features it reveals. The report shall be accompanied by the documents and supporting evidence that demonstrate the foregoing.

The designated judge shall immediately forward this report, together with his observations, to the Public Prosecutor, the entity responsible for defending the interests of society and enforcing the law before the courts. If the report is not submitted to him within the prescribed time limit, he shall inform the Public Prosecutor accordingly.
