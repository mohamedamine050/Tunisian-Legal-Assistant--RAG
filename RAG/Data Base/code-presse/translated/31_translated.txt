Courts are responsible for putting an end to the exercise of the right of reply whenever it is established that the expressions of the reply are:

- **Contrary to law**, 
- **Contrary to good morals**, 
- **Harmful to the legitimate interests of others**, or 
- **Detrimental to the journalist's honor or standing**.

The courts will consider the reply to be:

- **Crimes** if they constitute offenses punishable by death or imprisonment for more than five years, 
- **Misdemeanors** if they constitute offenses punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars, or 
- **Offenses** if they constitute offenses punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine of sixty dinars.

(Article 122, Code of Criminal Procedure)
