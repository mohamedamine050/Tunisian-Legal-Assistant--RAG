It is incumbent upon the plaintiff or the Public Prosecution, the entity responsible for defending the interests of society and enforcing the law before the judicial courts, as the case may be, to inform the defendant within five days following, and in any case, three days prior to the commencement of the hearing, by means of a bailiff in the former case and administratively in the latter, that copies of the documents and the names of the witnesses whom they intend to use to present their counter-arguments, along with their professions and addresses, have been placed at his disposal by the court in writing.
