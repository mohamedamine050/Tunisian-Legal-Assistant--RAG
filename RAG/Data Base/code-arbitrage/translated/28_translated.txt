The Arbitral Tribunal shall undertake all investigations, including the receiving of evidence, conducting examinations, and any other actions aimed at revealing the truth. 

If one of the parties holds any means of proof, it has the right to present it.

It also has the right to hear anyone it deems beneficial for the assessment of the dispute.

The Arbitral Tribunal may, in writing, appoint one of its members to perform a specific task.

It may also request judicial assistance to obtain any ruling that would enable it to achieve the purposes outlined in this chapter.
