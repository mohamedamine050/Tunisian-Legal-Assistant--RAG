It is permissible to request the annulment of an arbitral award, even if the parties have stipulated otherwise, in the following circumstances:

Firstly, if the award was issued without relying on or exceeding the scope of the arbitration agreement.

Secondly, if the award was issued based on a void arbitration agreement or outside the time limits for arbitration.

Thirdly, if the award includes matters not requested.

Fourthly, if the award violates a rule of public order.

Fifthly, if the arbitral tribunal was not lawfully constituted.

Sixthly, if the fundamental rules of procedure were not observed.
