An appeal transfers the lawsuit in its state as it was before the issuance of the appealed judgment, specifically in relation to the subject matter upon which the appeal is brought. 
[1] One party requests their right from another in the presence of a judge. The requesting party is called the plaintiff and the party from whom the right is requested is called the defendant.
[2] A lawsuit is the right that belongs to every claimant to present their claim to the court for judgment in their favor. In relation to the opposing party, it is the right to present defenses or arguments aimed at refuting that claim.
