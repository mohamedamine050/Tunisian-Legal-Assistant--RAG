(Amended by Law No. 87 of 1986 dated September 1, 1986, and by Law No. 18 of 2007 dated March 22, 2007). - The appeal is filed by means of a written petition submitted by a lawyer to the Registry of the Court which rendered the judgment appealed against. The clerk who receives it signs it, indicates the date of its submission, enters it immediately in a special register prepared for this purpose, issues a receipt containing the date of its submission and immediately informs the Registry of the Court of Cassation.

The Court of Cassation is the highest court in the judicial hierarchy. It is justified by the principle of cassation to oversee the proper application of laws by the courts of first instance.
