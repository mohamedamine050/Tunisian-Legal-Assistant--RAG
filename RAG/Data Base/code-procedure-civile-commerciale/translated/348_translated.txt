Every writ of attachment, which empowers the creditor holding an enforceable instrument to place the tangible movable property in the possession of his debtor under judicial seizure, and every notice of referral shall be made: 1) to the general receiver, if they relate to amounts owed by the public treasury under any heading whatsoever, whether as a section of the budget, as a deposit, or as security, 2) to the comptroller responsible for disbursement, if they relate to amounts owed by public institutions with financial autonomy or by local authorities, 3) to the receiving comptroller, if they relate to amounts paid as international financial advances.  Writs of attachment and notices of referral made to any person other than those mentioned above shall be invalid.
