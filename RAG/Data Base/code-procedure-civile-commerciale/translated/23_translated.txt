If the value of an object is not specified but can be determined, the court hearing the case shall determine it according to its absolute discretion, relying on the value of that object on the day the lawsuit was filed.

[1]  A person seeking their right from another in the presence of a judge; the claimant is called the plaintiff and the party against whom the claim is made is called the defendant. [2] A lawsuit is the right of anyone who has a claim to bring it before the judiciary for a ruling on its subject matter. With regard to the defendant, it is the right to present reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting that claim. The court may also, if there is a dispute, authorize the proof of that value if requested, or may order its own assessment by experts. If the matter concerns a lease without dispute, the value of the disputed object shall be determined by the amount of the annual rent.
