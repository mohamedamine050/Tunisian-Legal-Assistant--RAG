(Amended by Law No. 87 of 1986 dated September 1, 1986). - The plaintiff may amend part of the claim
[1] A request by one party from another to adjudicate a claim in the presence of a judge; the claimant is referred to as the plaintiff and the respondent is referred to as the defendant. [2] The claim is the right of any claimant to bring their claim before the court for adjudication in their favor. With respect to the defendant, the claim is the right to present defenses or arguments aimed at refuting that claim, or to amend or modify it within the period specified in the preceding paragraph.
