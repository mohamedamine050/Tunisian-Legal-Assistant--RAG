The petition for appeal, in addition to the required details in a petition initiating a lawsuit, must include:

[1] A request by one party for their right from another in the presence of the judge; the requesting party is called the plaintiff and the party requested from is called the defendant. 

[2] The lawsuit is the right of every claimant to present their claim to the judiciary to obtain a judgment in their favor. It is the right of the opposing party to present reasons for defense or arguments aimed at refuting that claim.

The petition for appeal must state the requested judgment and the reasons for the appeal, otherwise it will be invalid.
