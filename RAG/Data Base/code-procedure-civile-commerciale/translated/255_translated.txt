It is stipulated that the **Court Clerk**, a judicial officer responsible for maintaining records and managing court documents, shall ensure that each **judgment**, a written decision by the court resolving a legal or factual issue, or its extracted copies, are delivered with a **certified copy**, in law, a certified copy refers to a certified copy of a court decision or a documentary instrument,  of each **abstract**, or **execution copy** thereof, along with a statement of the date of delivery, the name of the person to whom it was delivered. Otherwise, the **Court Clerk**, a judicial officer responsible for maintaining records and managing court documents, shall be liable to a fine of five dinars for each **violation**.

**Violations** are defined as follows:

* **Felonies**, in the sense of this law, are crimes punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years.
* **Misdemeanors** are crimes punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or by a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
* **Offenses** are crimes punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine of sixty dinars.

**Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure**

It is further stipulated that the above fine shall not prevent the assessment of **damages**, which are [1] harm inflicted in violation of the law that affects a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation [2] loss or damage incurred by property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, **damages** represent the actual loss suffered by the plaintiff and the necessary expenses that they have incurred or will be forced to incur to repair the consequences of the act that caused them harm, as well as any benefits that they have been deprived of within the normal scope of the consequences of this act.  This may be required for the person who may have been harmed by the aforementioned.
