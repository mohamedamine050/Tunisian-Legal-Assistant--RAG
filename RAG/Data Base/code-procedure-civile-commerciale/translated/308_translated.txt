The following items are not subject to any form of seizure: 1) what is necessary for the debtor and his family, such as bedding, clothing, and cooking utensils; 2) tools and books necessary for the debtor's profession, up to a value of one hundred dinars, according to his choice; 3) books and school supplies necessary for the education of children under the debtor's care; 4) sufficient food for the debtor and his family for a period of fifteen days; 5) medals, letters, personal papers, sacred objects, and what is necessary for the performance of religious duties. The record of the seizure or attempted seizure must specify, where applicable, the items not subject to seizure that remained in the debtor's possession.
