(Amended by Law No. 82 of 2002 dated August 3, 2002).- Any person may increase the purchase price by an amount not less than one-sixth within ten days following the day of the judgment, after securing the amount of the increase, the full price of the initial purchase, the costs and fees assessed by the Deposit and Consignments Fund, or presenting a certified check with sufficient funds or an irrevocable bank guarantee, otherwise their increase will be canceled. This increase is made through a lawyer by a statement registered with the court where the sale took place. The statement shall include the name, surname, profession, address, description, purchase price, the amount of the increase, the costs and fees assessed as shown in the purchase record, and a receipt for the security.

It is not permissible to retract the increase.
