The valuation of costs and fees is subject to objection within a period of fifteen days from the date of notification thereof. Any objection filed after this period shall be void. The objection shall be raised by one of the executing bailiffs and the applicant shall be notified thereof with summons to appear before the court responsible for the sale within a period of no less than eight days and no more than fifteen days. The objection must be reasoned, otherwise it shall be null and void. The court shall decide on the objection within a month by a judgment that is not subject to appeal. The objection filed within the legal period shall suspend the payment of the valued costs and fees; however, it shall not prevent the continuation of the settlement procedures.
