(Amended by Law No. 87 of 1986 dated September 1, 1986). - The combined divisions shall also hear: 1) If the case calls for the unification of legal opinions between the divisions. 2) When there is a manifest error in a decision issued by one of the divisions in the first instance. An error is considered manifest: 1) If the decision to reject the case was based on a clear mistake of form. 2) If the decision relied on a legal text that was previously repealed or amended, making it no longer applicable. 3) When the decision was participated in by someone who previously examined the matter.
