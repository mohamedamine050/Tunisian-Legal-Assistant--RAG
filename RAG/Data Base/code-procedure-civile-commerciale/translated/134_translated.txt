(Amended by Law No. 87 of 1986 dated September 1, 1986).- The appellant shall do the following: - Summon his opponents to the hearing according to the procedure set forth in Chapter Five, within a period of not less than twenty days prior to the date of the hearing. This period shall be reduced to three days if the appealed judgment was issued in urgent matters or in cases of the type stipulated in Chapter 81. The summons shall be accompanied by a copy of the appeal petition and a counterpart of the appellant's documents which shall be submitted to the court registry, together with a copy of the judgment and any documents in his possession, accompanied by a list that takes into account the provisions of Chapter 72. - The appellant shall specifically state in the summons of his opponents that they must submit their responses to the grounds of appeal through a lawyer no later than the date of the hearing. - Subject to the provisions of Chapter 71 regarding any deficiencies or errors in the summons concerning the name and surname of the respondent, the court, the date of the hearing, or the attendance dates.
