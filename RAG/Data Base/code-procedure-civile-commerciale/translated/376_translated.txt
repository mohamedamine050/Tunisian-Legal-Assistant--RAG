The debtor has eight days from the date of the notification mentioned above to file an objection by making a statement to the court clerk. The court shall rule on the objection in accordance with the rules of the relevant court as set forth in Article 369. The clerk shall notify all parties concerned to appear at the first available hearing, taking into account the time limit stipulated in Article 362. The judgment issued at that time shall be considered as served. If no objection is filed against the decision of the judge of the district court within eight days, it shall become final and executory upon the request of the debtor or the most diligent creditor, based on a copy delivered by the court clerk.  This judgment shall be endorsed with the executory formula.
