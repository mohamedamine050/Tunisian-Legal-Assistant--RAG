(Amended by Law No. 87 of 1986 dated September 1, 1986) - Upon the expiration of the aforementioned period set forth in the preceding Article, the clerk shall transmit the case file to the Public Prosecutor at the Court of Cassation. The Court of Cassation is the highest court in the judicial hierarchy, responsible for upholding the principle of annulment to ensure the proper application of laws by the courts of first instance.  

The Public Prosecutor, either personally or through one of the Deputy Public Prosecutors at the Court of Cassation, shall draft written observations on the validity of the appeal. They shall not raise any new grounds except if they are related to public order. Subsequently, the Public Prosecutor shall transmit these observations along with the case file to the First President of the Court of Cassation, who shall assign the case to a hearing. The clerk shall notify the lawyers in writing of the date of the hearing at least eight days prior to its commencement.
