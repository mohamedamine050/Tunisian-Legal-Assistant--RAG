(Amended by Law No. 82 of 2002 dated August 3, 2002). - It is reasonable for the party concerned, if they have a legitimate excuse, to submit their statement, rectify any deficiencies, or add supporting documents, as long as the case is pending before the Court of First Instance. This Court is a civil court that falls under the judicial authority, which must be independent within the three branches of government. The decision of the Court of First Instance is not subject to any form of appeal until the closing of the pleadings in the Court of Appeal.
