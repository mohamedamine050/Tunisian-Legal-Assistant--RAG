(Amended by Law No. 14 of 1980 dated April 3, 1980). - If it becomes necessary to receive evidence by testimony, the presiding judge or the "reporting judge"(*) shall authorize the party relying on such testimony to bring the witness before him at the designated date, time, and place. The presiding judge or the "reporting judge"(*) shall personally hear the witnesses and, if necessary, may delegate this task to one of the judges assigned to the nearest center to the witness's location. Any testimony received other than in this manner shall be considered null and void and shall not be taken into account.
