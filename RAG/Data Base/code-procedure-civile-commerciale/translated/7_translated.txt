(Amended by Law No. 14 of 1980 dated April 3, 1980).-  A person's principal place of residence is the place where they habitually reside. The place where a person carries out their profession or trade is considered their principal place of residence for transactions related to the aforementioned activity.  The chosen place of residence is the place designated by agreement or law for the performance of an obligation or for carrying out a judicial action.
