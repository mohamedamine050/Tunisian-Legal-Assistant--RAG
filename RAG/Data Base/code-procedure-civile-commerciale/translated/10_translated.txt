(Amended by Law No. 82 of 2002 dated August 3, 2002).- If the person to be notified has left his residence and his whereabouts are unknown, a sealed envelope containing only the name of the person to be notified shall be deposited with the Secretariat of the Court where the dispute between the parties is to be adjudicated, which is a civil court subject to the judicial authority that must be independent within the three powers of the state.  In the absence of such a Court, it shall be deposited with the Mayor of the locality or the National Security Center or the National Guard Center of his last known residence. If the whereabouts are completely unknown, a copy of the notification shall be posted at the Court of First Instance and another copy at the seat of the governorate within whose jurisdiction the aforementioned Court is located.
