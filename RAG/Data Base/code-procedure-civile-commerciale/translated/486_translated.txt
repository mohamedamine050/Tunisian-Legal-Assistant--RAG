The **Court of First Instance** 
The **Court of First Instance** 
is often the first court to hear a dispute and make a decision.

maintains a separate register for the distribution of assets and another for the ordering of creditors. All procedures and obligations stipulated in this chapter, including those relating to the appeal case, are recorded in these registers. For this purpose, the 
**Court Clerk**
**Court Clerk**
, a judicial officer responsible for maintaining the records and managing the court's documents, is responsible for notifying the **Court of Appeal** in writing of all procedures and obligations relating to the appeal case. This notification is added to the register.
