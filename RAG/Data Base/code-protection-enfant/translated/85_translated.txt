The Public Prosecutor and the Investigating Judge, acting in accordance with the provisions of Articles 27, 28, and 53 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, shall undertake all investigative actions in felonies and misdemeanors committed by children, unless these actions conflict with the provisions of this Code. In the case of a crime within the jurisdiction of the administration to pursue, the Public Prosecutor alone has the standing to initiate proceedings based on a prior complaint from the administration concerned.
