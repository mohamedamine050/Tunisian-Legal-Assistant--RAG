This law guarantees the child's right to benefit from various preventive measures, of a social, educational, and health nature, as well as other provisions and procedures aimed at protecting him/her from all forms of violence or harm.

[1] Harm, in violation of the law, that affects a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation. [2] Loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, harm to physical well-being, reputation, etc. - Harm in crimes and quasi-crimes is the loss actually suffered by the plaintiff and the necessary expenses that he/she has been or will be forced to incur to repair the consequences of the act committed to his/her detriment, as well as what he/she has been deprived of in the ordinary scope of the consequences of this act.

Or physical, psychological, or sexual abuse, neglect, or negligence that results in mistreatment or exploitation.
