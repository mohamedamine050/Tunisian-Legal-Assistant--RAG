## English Translation:

1. **The States Parties recognize the right of every child to a standard of living adequate for the child's physical, mental, spiritual, moral and social development.**

2. **The parents or, as the case may be, the legal guardians of the child have the primary responsibility to provide, within their abilities and financial resources, for the conditions of living necessary for the child's development.**

3. **States Parties, in accordance with their national conditions and within their means, shall take appropriate measures to assist parents and others responsible for the child to realize this right and shall, where necessary, provide material assistance and support programmes, particularly with regard to nutrition, clothing and housing.**

4. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that a child who is capable of working shall not be employed in any work that is likely to be hazardous or to interfere with the child's education, or to be harmful to the child's health or physical, mental, spiritual, moral or social development.**

5. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure the effective implementation of the right recognized in this article.  This includes, where appropriate, the establishment of a minimum wage and other forms of financial protection, and the provision of social and economic services and benefits, such as child allowances, parental leave, and free or subsidized childcare.**

6. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to protect children from economic exploitation and from any form of work that is likely to be hazardous or to interfere with the child's education, or to be harmful to the child's health or physical, mental, spiritual, moral or social development.**

7. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children are not subjected to any form of exploitation, including sexual exploitation, trafficking in children, child labour and child soldiery.**

8. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children are protected from all forms of violence, including physical, sexual and psychological abuse, neglect and maltreatment.**

9. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children have access to the best possible health care, including preventive health care, prenatal and postnatal care, and the provision of essential medicines and vaccines.**

10. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children have access to education, which shall be free and compulsory at least at the primary level, and that they have access to other forms of education, including vocational training and higher education.**

11. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children have access to adequate housing, safe drinking water, sanitation and other essential services.**

12. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children have access to social security and other forms of social protection.**

13. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children are protected from discrimination of any kind, including discrimination on the basis of race, colour, sex, language, religion, political or other opinion, national or social origin, property, birth or other status.**

14. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children are protected from all forms of exploitation and abuse, including the illegal detention, abduction, sale, or trafficking of children.**

15. **States Parties shall take all appropriate measures to ensure that children have access to justice, including the right to a fair trial and the right to legal aid.**
