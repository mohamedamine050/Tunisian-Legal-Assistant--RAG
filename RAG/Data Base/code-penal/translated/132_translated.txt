(Amended by Law No. 93 of 1995 dated November 9, 1995),

Anyone who joins a gang or participates in an agreement between two or more persons to commit a felony or a misdemeanor of theft, fraud, or forgery, whether specified or unspecified, or in acts preparing for or facilitating their commission, provided that the agreement is organized, even in its initial formation, and continuous, even for a short period, as defined in Article 131 of the Code, shall be punished by imprisonment for six years.  The penalty—a punishment and remedy imposed in the name of society upon a person criminally responsible for a crime committed based on a court judgment—shall be twelve years for the leaders of the aforementioned gangs, and also in the case of using a child or several children under eighteen years of age in the acts specified in Article 131 of the Code.
