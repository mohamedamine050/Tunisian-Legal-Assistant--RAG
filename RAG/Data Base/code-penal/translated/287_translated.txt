If the accused, in the situation described in Article 286 of this Code, asserts a right of ownership or other real right, the court shall assess the necessity of referring the non-violent aspects of the actions to the competent court.  The accused may only raise the aforementioned defense if it is based either on a clear plan or on possessory elements equivalent thereto, precisely stated.  The plan or the stated elements, if accepted by the competent court, must remove any criminal aspect from the actions under consideration.
