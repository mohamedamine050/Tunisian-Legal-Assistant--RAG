(Amended by Law No. 44 of 1969, dated July 26, 1969)

Whoever intentionally, without using explosives or fire, causes damage [1] harm inflicted in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation; [2] loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like â€“ damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the loss actually suffered by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he was or will be obliged to spend to repair the consequences of the act committed to his detriment, as well as what he was deprived of in the normal scope of the consequences of this act; by means of another's property, whether real or personal, shall be punished by imprisonment for three years and a fine of two thousand dinars. If the damage is such as to put the existence or health of a thing in danger, the punishment shall be imprisonment for five years and a fine of three thousand dinars.  Attempt shall be punishable.
