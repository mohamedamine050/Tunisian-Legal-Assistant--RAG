(Amended by Law No. 33 of 1998, dated May 23, 1998),

Any person practicing a medical or paramedical profession who, out of courtesy, provides a certificate containing incorrect facts relating to a person's health, or conceals or falsely attests to the existence of a non-existent illness, disability, or pregnancy, or provides false information about the source of an illness, disability, or cause of death, shall be punished by imprisonment for one year and a fine of one thousand dinars.  The punishment shall be increased to five years imprisonment and a fine of five thousand dinars if the person, in the context of practicing the medical or paramedical profession, solicited or accepted for himself or for another, directly or indirectly, through efforts, promises, gifts, presents, or benefits, in exchange for issuing a certificate containing materially incorrect facts.
