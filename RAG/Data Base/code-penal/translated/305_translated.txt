The penalties stipulated in the preceding article shall be doubled if the harm caused [1] is an injury inflicted in violation of the law, affecting the person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation; [2] the loss or damage inflicted on property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like.  In crimes and quasi-crimes, harm is the loss actually suffered by the claimant, and the necessary expenses which he has been or will be compelled to incur to repair the consequences of the act committed to his detriment, as well as what he has been deprived of within the normal limits of the consequences of this act, with the intent to cause suffering:

Firstly: By a public official or someone similar due to an order related to their duties;

Secondly: By a witness due to their testimony.
