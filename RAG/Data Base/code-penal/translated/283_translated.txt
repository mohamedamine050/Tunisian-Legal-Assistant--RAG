(Amended by Law No. 23 of 1989 dated February 27, 1989)

Anyone who, through fraud, force, violence, coercion, or threats in writing or verbally, obtains a signature, writings, a contract—a contract being a legally binding agreement that obligates the contracting parties and defines their obligations and rights concerning the specific transaction—a drawing, or any other document containing or entailing an obligation or waiving or releasing one, even if the use of force or other means mentioned is directed against a person other than the one from whom it is obtained, shall be punished by imprisonment for twenty years.
