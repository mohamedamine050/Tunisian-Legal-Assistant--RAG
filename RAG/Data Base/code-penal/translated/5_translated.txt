Amended by Law No. 34 of 1964 dated July 2, 1964, Law No. 63 of 1966 dated July 5, 1966, Law No. 23 of 1989 dated February 27, 1989, and Law No. 89 of 1999 dated August 2, 1999, the penalties are as follows:

1) Principal Penalties:

1- Death penalty

2- Life imprisonment

3- Imprisonment for a specified term

4- Community service

5- Fine

6- Compensation.  Paragraph 6 was added by Law No. 68 of 2009 dated August 12, 2009.

7- Electronic monitoring. Paragraph 7 was added by Article 3 of Government Order No. 29 of 2020 dated June 10, 2020.


b) Supplementary Penalties:

1- (a) Repealed by Law No. 9 of 1995 dated January 23, 1995)

2- Banishment

3- Administrative supervision

4- Confiscation of proceeds as provided by law

5- Seizure of property

6- Exclusion as provided by law

7- Deprivation of the following rights and privileges:

a) Public office or certain professions such as lawyer, public official, doctor, veterinarian, midwife, educational institution director or employee thereof regardless of title, notary, bailiff, expert witness, or court witness, except for providing statements for guidance purposes.

b) Carrying weapons and all official honorary decorations.

c) Right to vote.

8- Publication of the contents of certain judgments.
