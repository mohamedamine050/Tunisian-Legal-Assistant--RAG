(Amended by Law No. 85 of 1985 dated August 11, 1985).  A public official or equivalent, and any director, member, or employee of a local public authority, associations of national interest, or public industrial and commercial institutions, or companies in which the state participates directly or indirectly in their capital, wholly or partially, or companies affiliated with local public authorities, entrusted by virtue of their position with the sale, manufacture, purchase, management, or preservation of any assets, who exploits their position to obtain an unwarranted benefit for themselves or others, or to harm the administration, or violates the applicable regulations concerning such operations to achieve the benefit or inflict the harm, shall be punished by imprisonment for ten years and a fine equal to the value of the benefit obtained or the harm caused to the administration.

[1] Harm inflicted in violation of the law affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, honor, property, or reputation. [2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, or the like. - In crimes and quasi-crimes, harm refers to the loss actually suffered by the claimant, the necessary expenses incurred or to be incurred to repair the consequences of the act that caused the harm, as well as the deprivation of benefit within the normal limits of the consequences of said act.
