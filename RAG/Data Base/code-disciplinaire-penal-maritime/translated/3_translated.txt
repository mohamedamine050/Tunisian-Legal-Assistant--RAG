The Master, in order to safeguard the public interest and in accordance with the provisions of Article 59 of the Maritime Commerce Code, shall have the necessary authority over all persons on board the vessel, regardless of their status, as required by necessity to preserve the security and safety of the passengers, the vessel or the cargo, and the smooth running of the sea voyage.

For this purpose, he may use all necessary means of pressure and seek the assistance of all persons on board. All measures taken by the Master, as well as the circumstances that necessitated them, must be recorded in the disciplinary logbook as provided for in Chapter IV of this Code.
