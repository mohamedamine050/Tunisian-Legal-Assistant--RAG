##  Serious Disciplinary Offenses:

1) Any offense:

- **Described as a felony** under the meaning of this law: offenses punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years.
- **Described as a misdemeanor:** offenses punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
- **Described as a violation:** offenses punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)
- **Light disciplinary offenses** committed again during the same voyage by any sailor who has previously been subject to one of the penalties stipulated in Chapter Eight of this Code, within a period not exceeding one month for coastal navigation and not exceeding two months for all other types of navigation. Light offenses considered serious offenses under the provisions of this paragraph cannot be misdemeanors. 

2) Any offense:

- **Described as a felony** under the meaning of this law: offenses punishable by death or imprisonment exceeding five years.
- **Described as a misdemeanor:** offenses punishable by imprisonment exceeding fifteen days but not exceeding five years, or a fine exceeding sixty dinars.
- **Described as a violation:** offenses punishable by imprisonment not exceeding fifteen days or a fine not exceeding sixty dinars. (Article 122 of the Code of Criminal Procedure)
- **Which may affect the safety of the vessel**.

3) **Non-compliance, resistance to any order related to work**, after a formal, official warning issued by the Head, except for the cases stipulated in Article 51 of this Code.

4) **Drunkenness on board the vessel with disruption**, except for the case stipulated in Article 48 of this Code.

5) **Lack of respect towards the Head and direct insults towards a subordinate**, whether on board the vessel or on land.

6) **Negligence in carrying out a task or guarding, especially sleeping during driving, watchkeeping, alongside the mooring, working on deck, in the engines or in the premises.**

7) **Starting a fire without authorization or smoking in a forbidden area.**

8) **Using a vessel belonging to the ship without authorization**, which results in loss, destruction, or damage.

9) **Unauthorized absence from the vessel** committed by:

- **The sailor who is absent for more than four hours** in the circumstances stipulated in paragraph 3 of Article 6 of this Code.
- **The sailor assigned to a watch or security post**, when his absence does not result in harmful consequences.

10) **Embezzlement or theft** which, in the opinion of the Master or the maritime authority, does not warrant a complaint for theft.

11) **Intentional damage to furniture**, except for the cases stipulated in Article 41 of this Code.

12) **Leaving the place of execution of the penalty** without justification by anyone punished with confinement or arrest.

13) **A Officer, Head or crew member going ashore despite the Master's decision posted at the entrance of the vessel.**
