Interveners shall be liable for all damages caused to the correspondence of others as a result of sending prohibited items or failing to comply with the conditions of dispatch. Interveners may seek recourse against the sender responsible for the damage to obtain compensation.

[1] Harm inflicted in violation of the law, affecting a person's body, mental or psychological health, reputation, property, or honor. 
[2] Loss or damage to property, deprivation of comfort, or harm to physical well-being, reputation, etc. - Damage in crimes and quasi-crimes is the actual loss incurred by the claimant and the necessary expenses that he was or will be forced to spend to repair the consequences of the act that caused harm to him, as well as what he was deprived of in the normal scope of the consequences of this act.
