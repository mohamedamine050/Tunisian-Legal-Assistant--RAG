(Amended by Law No. 66 of 2009 dated August 12, 2009). - The driver's license shall be suspended for a maximum period of two years, if the traffic accident resulted in death or bodily injury by fault. The maximum suspension period shall be increased to four years if it is proven that the driver, at the time of the accident:

- was under the influence of alcohol or refused to undergo procedures to prove the state of intoxication,

- did not possess a driver's license of the required category,

- was driving in violation of the driving license suspension decision,

- deliberately fled in accordance with Article 91 of this Code.

The suspension shall be for a maximum period of four years in the event of a recurrence within two years from the date of expiration of the driving license suspension period, pursuant to the first paragraph of this article.
