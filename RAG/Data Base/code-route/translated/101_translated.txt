(Added by paragraphs 2 and 3 of Law No. 54 of 2006 dated July 28, 2006). - Upon observing any of the crimes provided for in Articles 85, 86, 87, 88, 89, 90, and 91, a report shall be drawn up concerning it, to be referred to the judiciary. A copy of this report shall be sent to the competent administrative authorities, if the crime is one that carries an administrative penalty. (Amended by Law No. 66 of 2009 dated August 12, 2009)

When observing ordinary violations as provided for in Article 83 of this Code and its implementing provisions, the officer shall draw up a report stating that the offender has been informed that the report will not be referred to the Court of First Instance if the fine is paid in full at one of the financial collection offices. The officer shall deliver to the offender a copy of the report to be used for paying the fine at one of the financial collection offices. If the offender refuses to sign, this shall be stated in the report. A copy of the report shall be sent to the financial collector appointed for the same territorial jurisdiction.
