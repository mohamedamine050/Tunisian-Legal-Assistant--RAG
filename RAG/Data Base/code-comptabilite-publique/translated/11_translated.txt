The **Court of Audit**, a court where litigation takes place between parties, is a civil court under the judicial authority, which must be independent within the three branches of government. The Court of Audit is a judicial institution tasked with examining public accounts and funds, verifying the legality of government expenditures, and ensuring the efficient and transparent management of public funds. It monitors the results of its work and the extent to which its recommendations are implemented, in accordance with the established forms of oversight on the management of public funds.
